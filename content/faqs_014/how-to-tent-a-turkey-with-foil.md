<p>Tenting a turkey with foil is a great way to keep your turkey moist and juicy when roasting. It prevents the turkey from drying out and keeps the skin crisp and golden brown. The key to tenting your turkey correctly is to use the right amount of foil and to make sure that it is securely attached to the roasting pan.</p>

<h2>What You Need to Tent a Turkey with Foil</h2>

<p>To tent your turkey with foil, you will need:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Heavy duty aluminum foil</li>
  <li>Sharp kitchen shears</li>
  <li>A roasting pan large enough to fit your turkey</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps for Tenting a Turkey with Foil</h2>

<p>Here are the steps for tenting your turkey with foil:</p>

<ol>
  <li>Preheat your oven to the desired temperature (usually 325-350 degrees).</li>
  <li>Place your turkey in the roasting pan and add about 1/2 cup of water to the bottom of the pan.</li>
  <li>Place a sheet of heavy duty aluminum foil large enough to cover the entire turkey over the top of the turkey.</li>
  <li>Secure the edges of the foil to the sides of the roasting pan with kitchen shears.</li>
  <li>Place the roasting pan in the oven and cook according to the recipe instructions.</li>
  <li>Once the turkey is done cooking, remove the foil and let the turkey rest for 10-15 minutes before carving.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Tips For Tenting a Turkey with Foil</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Be sure to use heavy-duty foil to avoid tearing.</li>
  <li>Make sure the foil is securely attached to the roasting pan to prevent steam from escaping.</li>
  <li>Be sure to remove the foil for the last 15-20 minutes of cooking to allow the skin to crisp up.</li>
</ul>
