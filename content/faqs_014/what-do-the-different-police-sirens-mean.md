<h2>Understanding the Different Types of Sirens</h2>

When it comes to police sirens, there are three main types that are used by law enforcement officers. Each type of siren has its own distinct sound and meaning. The three types of sirens are:

<h3>1. Wail</h3>

The wail siren is the most commonly used type of police siren. It is a long, continuous sound that is usually used to alert other drivers of the presence of an emergency vehicle. The wail siren is usually used when an officer is responding to a call or chasing a suspect.

<h3>2. Yelp</h3>

The yelp siren is a short, intermittent sound that is used to indicate that an emergency vehicle is slowing down or coming to a stop. This type of siren is typically used when an officer is on the scene of an emergency or crime and needs to communicate their presence to other drivers.

<h3>3. Hi-Lo</h3>

The hi-lo siren is a combination of the wail and yelp siren and is used to indicate that an emergency vehicle is coming to a stop. This type of siren is usually used when an officer is on the scene of an emergency or crime and needs to communicate their presence to other drivers.
