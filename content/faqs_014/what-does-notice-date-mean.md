<h2>What Is Notice Date?</h2>

<p>Notice date is the date on which the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) or other tax authority sends notice of a tax liability to the taxpayer. This date is not the same as the due date for filing taxes. It is the date that the IRS or other tax authority notifies the taxpayer of the tax liability and sets the due date for filing taxes. This date is important because it establishes when the taxpayer must file their taxes and pay the associated tax liability.</p>

<h2>When Is Notice Date Sent?</h2>

<p>Notice date is typically sent when a taxpayer has an unpaid tax liability or fails to file a tax return on time. This notice will contain the amount of taxes owed and the due date for filing taxes. Notice date is also sent if the taxpayer is due a refund, but the refund has not been issued.</p>
