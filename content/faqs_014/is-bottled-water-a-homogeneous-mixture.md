<h2>What is Homogeneous Mixture?</h2>

<p>A homogeneous mixture is a type of mixture in which all of the components are evenly distributed throughout the mixture. This means that all of the components have the same properties and cannot be physically separated. An example of a homogeneous mixture is salt water, which is made up of two components: salt and water. Both components are evenly distributed throughout the mixture and cannot be physically separated.</p>

<h2>What is Heterogeneous Mixture?</h2>

<p>A heterogeneous mixture is a type of mixture in which the components are not evenly distributed throughout the mixture. This means that the components have different properties and can be physically separated. An example of a heterogeneous mixture is orange juice, which is made up of two components: orange pulp and water. The orange pulp is not evenly distributed throughout the mixture, and can be physically separated from the water.</p>

<h3>Mineral Water</h3>

<p>Mineral water is water that has been artificially filtered and has a high mineral content. Mineral water is usually a homogeneous mixture, as the minerals are evenly distributed throughout the water. Mineral water can be used for drinking or cooking, and is often bottled and sold commercially.</p>

<h3>Purified Water</h3>

<p>Purified water is water that has been artificially filtered to remove minerals and other impurities. Purified water is usually a heterogeneous mixture, as the minerals and impurities are not evenly distributed throughout the water. Purified water is often used for drinking and cooking, and is usually bottled and sold commercially.</p>
