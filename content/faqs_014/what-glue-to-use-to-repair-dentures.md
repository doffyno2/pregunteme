<h2>Types of Glue For Dentures</h2>

<p>There are two main types of glue used to repair dentures: epoxy and acrylic. Epoxy is a strong and durable adhesive that is typically used to repair metal dentures. It is a two-part glue that is mixed together to form a thick paste. Acrylic is a more flexible adhesive that is used to repair acrylic or porcelain dentures. It is a one-part glue that sets quickly and is easy to use. Both types of glue are available in many different forms, such as powders, liquids, and gels.</p>

<h2>How To Repair Dentures Using Glue</h2>

<p>Repairing dentures using glue is a fairly simple process, but it is important to follow the directions carefully in order to ensure a successful repair. First, the denture should be cleaned thoroughly with warm water and a soft toothbrush. Once the denture is clean, the broken pieces should be aligned in their correct positions. It is important to use only a small amount of glue, as too much can cause the pieces to slide out of alignment. The glue should then be allowed to dry completely before the denture is worn.</p>
