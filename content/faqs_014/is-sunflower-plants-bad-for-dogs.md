<h2>What Are Sunflower Plants?</h2>

Sunflowers are a type of annual flowering plant native to the Americas. They come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, and can reach heights of up to 10 feet tall. Sunflowers are popular for their bright yellow petals and bold dark centers, which can be seen in gardens, fields, and bouquets. 

Sunflowers produce a substance called helianthin, which is a natural pesticide. This substance is toxic to certain insects, but it has not been found to be toxic to animals. In fact, the seeds of sunflowers are often used as a birdseed, which means they can be eaten safely by birds.
