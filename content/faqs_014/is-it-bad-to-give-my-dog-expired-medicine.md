<h2>What is the Risk of Giving My Dog Expired Medicine?</h2>

<p>The risk of giving your pet expired medicine is that it may not work properly. The active ingredients in the medicine may have broken down over time, reducing the potency of the drug. This can be especially dangerous if the medicine is intended to treat a serious condition. Additionally, expired medication can cause unexpected side effects, such as nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, or an allergic reaction. In some cases, expired medicine can even cause death.</p>

<h2>What Happens When Medicine Expire?</h2>

<p>When medication expires, the active ingredients in the drug can break down. This can make the medicine less potent, or even ineffective. Additionally, expired medication can become contaminated with bacteria or other contaminants, which can be dangerous for your pet. In some cases, long-term exposure to expired medication can cause liver or kidney damage.</p>

<h2>How Can I Tell if My Pet's Medicine Has Expired?</h2>

<p>The best way to tell if your pet's medicine has expired is to check the expiration date printed on the package or bottle. If the expiration date has passed, it is best to discard the medication and purchase a new supply. Additionally, if the medication has been stored in a warm or damp environment, it is best to discard it as the active ingredients may have broken down.</p>

<h2>Are There Any Alternatives to Expired Medication?</h2>

<p>Yes, there are alternatives to expired medication. The best option is to purchase a new supply of the medicine from your veterinarian or pharmacy. Additionally, you can speak to your veterinarian about alternative medicines that may be available. Your veterinarian may also be able to provide you with a prescription for a different medication that is not expired.</p>
