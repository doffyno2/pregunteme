<h2>Tools and Materials Needed to Repair a Broken Earring Post</h2>

<p>To repair your broken earring post, you will need the following tools and materials:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Needle-nose pliers</li>
  <li>Wire cutters</li>

  <li>Toothpicks</li>
  <li>Cotton swabs</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step 1: Removing the Old Post</h2>

<p>Using your needle-nose pliers, carefully grasp the post and unscrew it from the back of the earring. If the post is too damaged to unscrew, use your wire cutters to cut it off. </p>

<h2>Step 2: Cleaning the Earring</h2>

<p>Once the post is removed, use a cotton swab and some jewelry cleaner to clean the remaining portion of the earring, paying special attention to the area where the new post will be attached. </p>

<h2>Step 3: Attaching the New Post</h2>

<p>Using the jewelry glue and a toothpick, carefully apply a small amount of glue to the area where the post will be attached. Be sure not to use too much glue or else it will be difficult to remove later. </p>

<p>Once the glue is applied, attach the new post. You may need to use the needle-nose pliers to twist the post into place. </p>

<p>Allow the glue to dry completely before wearing the earrings. </p>
