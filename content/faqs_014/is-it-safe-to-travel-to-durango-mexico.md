<h2>Introduction</h2>

Durango is a state in northern Mexico that has recently been gaining in popularity for its beautiful landscape and attractions. But is it safe to visit? We take a look at Durango’s crime rate, safety tips, and more to help you decide if this is the right destination for you.

<h2>Crime Rate in Durango</h2>

Durango is one of the safest states in Mexico, with a low crime rate. According to the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), Durango’s rate of intentional homicide is lower than the national average. The state also has a relatively low rate of robbery, with a rate of 1.6 per 100,000 people in 2019. 

That said, it’s still important to exercise caution while visiting Durango. Petty theft, such as pickpocketing, is not uncommon in some areas, especially in tourist spots. It’s also important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

<h2>Safety Tips</h2>

When visiting Durango, there are some basic safety tips you should keep in mind.

• Avoid walking alone at night. Stick with friends or take a taxi. 

• Don’t carry large amounts of cash. Credit cards and travelers checks are a safer option. 

• Stay in well-lit, populated areas and avoid deserted streets and alleys. 

• Don’t leave your drinks unattended or accept drinks from strangers. 

• Don’t wear expensive jewelry or flashy clothing. 

• Don’t flash large amounts of cash or openly discuss your financial situation. 

• Don’t leave your belongings unattended. 

• Pay attention to your surroundings and trust your gut if something feels wrong. 

• Let someone know where you’re going and when you plan to return. 

• Always keep your passport and other important documents in a safe place. 

• Research your destination before you go, and be aware of any local customs or laws. 

<h2>Popular Attractions in Durango</h2>

Durango is a beautiful state with plenty to offer visitors. From its stunning mountain landscapes to its vibrant culture and history, there’s something for everyone. Here are just a few of the popular attractions to check out while you’re in Durango: 

• The Natural History Museum of Durango is a great place to learn about the state’s geology, archaeology, and history. 

• The Basílica de Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe is a popular pilgrimage site for Catholics and tourists alike. 

• The Durango Zoo is home to a variety of animals, including lions, tigers, and giraffes. 

• The Sombrerete Valley is a stunning natural area with lush forests, waterfalls, and hot springs. 

• The Cerro de la Bufa is a mountain that offers stunning views of Durango. 

• The Ciudad Durango Museum is a great place to learn about the city’s history and culture. 

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions About Durango</h2>

<h3>Q: Is Durango safe to visit?</h3>

A: Durango is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s important to exercise caution, especially at night. Stick with friends and take taxis when possible, and always be aware of your surroundings.

<h3>Q: Is Durango a good place for families?</h3>
