<h2>What is B12?</h2>

B12 is a vitamin that is found naturally in many foods such as meat, dairy, eggs, and fortified cereals. It is also available in supplement form. B12 is essential for maintaining healthy red blood cells, proper nerve functioning, and healthy metabolism. It is also needed for the production of DNA and RNA, the genetic material that makes up our bodies. B12 helps to form healthy red blood cells, which transport oxygen and other nutrients throughout the body. B12 also helps to maintain healthy nerve cells and is involved in the production of certain neurotransmitters, which are chemicals that help to regulate mood and behavior.

<h2>Why Do We Need B12?</h2>

B12 is essential for overall health and well-being. B12 deficiency can cause anemia, fatigue, nerve damage, and other health problems. A lack of B12 can also lead to a weakened immune system, which makes it more difficult for the body to fight off infections. B12 deficiency is also linked to psychological issues such as depression, anxiety, and dementia.

Maintenance B12 is often recommended for people who have a B12 deficiency or who are at risk of developing one.
