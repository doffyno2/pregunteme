<p>The Toyota Highlander has a maintenance required light that comes on every 5,000 miles to remind you to do a few basic maintenance tasks such as checking the oil, tires, and fluid levels. If the light comes on, you'll need to reset the maintenance required light to get it to go off.</p>

<h2>What Does the Maintenance Required Light Mean?</h2>

<p>The maintenance required light is a reminder to do basic maintenance tasks on your Toyota Highlander. This light will come on every 5,000 miles and will stay lit until the maintenance has been performed. If you don't reset the light after performing the maintenance, the light will stay on.</p>

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Required Light</h2>

<p>Resetting the maintenance required light on a 2018 Toyota Highlander is easy to do and only takes a few minutes. Here's what you need to do:</p>

<ol>
  <li>Turn the ignition switch to the "On" position.</li>
  <li>Press and hold the "Trip" button on the instrument panel for 10 seconds.</li>
  <li>Release the "Trip" button and the maintenance required light will go off.</li>
</ol>

<p>That's all there is to it. The maintenance required light will now be reset and will not come on again until the next 5,000 mile interval.</p>

<h2>What Maintenance Should I Do?</h2>

<p>When the maintenance required light comes on, it's important to do the necessary maintenance tasks to keep your Toyota Highlander running at its best. Here are the maintenance tasks you should do when the light comes on:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Check the oil level and add oil if needed.</li>
  <li>Check the tire pressure and add air if needed.</li>
  <li>Check the fluid levels and top off if needed.</li>
  <li>Check the air filter and replace if needed.</li>
</ul>

<p>These are the basic maintenance tasks that should be done when the maintenance required light comes on. Doing these tasks will help keep your Toyota Highlander running in top condition.</p>
