Cave spiders are a type of arachnid that can be found in caves and other dark, damp places. They are small, typically less than an inch in length, and generally harmless. But they can be a nuisance if they start to climb up walls in your home. 

Fortunately, there are some simple steps you can take to prevent cave spiders from climbing up walls inside your home. In this article, we'll discuss some of the most effective ways to keep cave spiders from entering your home and climbing up walls.

<h2>What Attracts Cave Spiders? </h2>

Cave spiders are attracted to dark, damp places, so if your home has any of these conditions, it may be a target for cave spiders. They also like to feed on other insects, so if you have a lot of flying insects in or near your home, you may have a problem with cave spiders.

<h2>How to Keep Cave Spiders Out of Your Home </h2>

The best way to keep cave spiders out of your home is to make sure they don't have a way in. Check the exterior of your home for any cracks or gaps that may be letting them in. Make sure all screens and doors fit tightly, and seal up any holes or cracks that you find.

You can also install sticky traps on walls or ceilings near areas where cave spiders may be entering your home. These traps will capture any spiders that try to climb up the walls.

<h2>How to Prevent Cave Spiders from Climbing Walls</h2>

Once you have taken steps to keep cave spiders from entering your home, you can then take steps to prevent them from climbing walls. The most effective way to do this is to use a spray repellent that contains pyrethrins or pyrethroids. These chemicals will deter spiders from climbing walls, and they are also safe to use around humans and pets.

You can also use sticky traps on walls and ceilings to capture any spiders that do make it into your home. Make sure to check the traps regularly, and remove any spiders that you find.
