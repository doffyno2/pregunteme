<h2>What You Need</h2>

<p>Before you can get started picking a lock with a knife, you will need the following tools: a flat head screwdriver, a metal file, a knife, and a tension wrench. <h2>Steps to Picking a Lock with a Knife</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Insert the Tension Wrench</h3>

<p>The first step in picking a lock with a knife is to insert the tension wrench into the lock. The tension wrench is a small metal tool that is usually shaped like a key. It is inserted into the lock and held in place with tension. This is what will allow you to manipulate the pins inside the lock.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Insert the File</h3>

<p>Once the tension wrench is in place, the next step is to insert the file into the lock. The file should be inserted at an angle and gently pushed towards the back of the lock. This will cause the pins inside the lock to move slightly and create an opening for the knife.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Insert the Knife</h3>

<p>Once the file has created an opening for the knife, the next step is to insert the knife into the lock. The knife should be inserted at an angle and gently pushed towards the back of the lock. This will cause the pins inside the lock to move further and create an even larger opening for the knife.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Manipulate the Pins</h3>

<p>Once the knife has created an opening, the next step is to manipulate the pins inside the lock. This is done by slowly and gently pushing the pins back and forth with the knife. As the pins are moved, they will eventually line up in a way that will allow the lock to be opened.</p>

<h3>Step 5: Open the Lock</h3>

<p>Once the pins have been manipulated and lined up correctly, the next step is to open the lock. This is done by slowly and gently turning the tension wrench in the direction that the key would turn. This will cause the lock to open and the door will be unlocked.</p>
