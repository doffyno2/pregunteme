<h2>Step 1: Check the Oil Level</h2>

<p>The first step in turning off the maintenance required light is to make sure the oil level in your car is correct. To do this, open the hood of your car and locate the dipstick. Pull the dipstick out and check to see if the oil level is within the acceptable range. If it is not, add oil until it reaches the correct level. Once the oil level is correct, you can proceed to the next step.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Replace the Oil Filter</h2>

<p>The next step in turning off the maintenance required light is to replace the oil filter. Locate the oil filter and unscrew it. Make sure to place a pan beneath the filter to catch any remaining oil. Once the filter is removed, replace it with a new one and screw it back into place.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Change the Oil</h2>

<p>The third step in turning off the maintenance required light is to change the oil. Locate the oil drain plug and remove it. Place a pan beneath the plug to catch any remaining oil. Once the oil is drained, replace the plug and add new oil to the car. Make sure to check the oil level again to ensure it is correct.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Reset the Maintenance Required Light</h2>

<p>The final step in turning off the maintenance required light is to reset it. To do this, locate the reset button on the dashboard. Hold the reset button for approximately 10 seconds until the light turns off. Once the light is off, your maintenance required light is reset and you can continue driving your car.</p>
