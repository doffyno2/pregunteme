<h2>What You Need</h2>
<p>To repair a hole in pantyhose, you will need:</p>
<ul>
  <li>A pair of pantyhose with a hole, snag, or tear</li>
  <li>A sewing needle</li>
  <li>Thread (preferably the same color as the pantyhose)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step-By-Step Guide</h2>
<p>Repairing a hole in pantyhose is a relatively simple process and can be done in just a few easy steps:</p>
<ul>
  <li>First, locate the hole, snag, or tear in the pantyhose. If the hole is small, it may be difficult to locate. If so, use a magnifying glass to help you find it.</li>
  <li>Once you have located the hole, put a knot in the end of the thread. This will prevent the thread from slipping through the hole and making the repair more difficult.</li>
  <li>Thread the needle and begin to sew the hole shut. Start at the bottom of the hole and work your way up, making small stitches as you go. Make sure to go over the hole several times to ensure a secure repair.</li>
  <li>Once you have finished sewing the hole, tie off the thread and cut off any excess thread.</li>
  <li>Finally, use a hot iron to press the repair flat. This will help to ensure that the repair is secure and will not come undone.</li>
</ul>
