<h2>What Is the Public Health Intervention Wheel?</h2>

The public health intervention wheel is a tool used by public health practitioners to assist in the identification, assessment, and resolution of public health issues. It is composed of five components: prevention, surveillance, epidemiology, health promotion, and evaluation. Each of the components is designed to work together to provide a holistic approach to public health care. 

Prevention is the first component of the public health intervention wheel. This involves the identification of potential public health issues and the development of strategies to prevent them. This includes interventions such as immunizations, health screenings, and other preventative measures.

The second component of the public health intervention wheel is surveillance. This involves the tracking of public health issues and the evaluation of the effectiveness of prevention strategies. This includes data collection, analysis, and reporting.

Epidemiology is the third component of the public health intervention wheel. This involves the study of patterns, causes, and effects of public health issues. This includes the identification of risk factors and the investigation of disease outbreaks.

The fourth component of the public health intervention wheel is health promotion. This involves the identification and implementation of strategies to improve the health of a population. This includes activities such as health education, advocacy, and community mobilization.

The fifth and final component of the public health intervention wheel is evaluation. This involves the assessment of the effectiveness of prevention, surveillance, and health promotion strategies. This includes the use of quantitative and qualitative data to measure progress and evaluate the impact of interventions.
