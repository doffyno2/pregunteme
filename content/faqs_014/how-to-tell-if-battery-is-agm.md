<p>If you're in the market for a new battery, you may have heard of AGM batteries. AGM stands for Absorbed Glass Mat, and it is a type of battery technology that promises increased power and better longevity. But how do you know if your battery is AGM or not? In this article, we'll look at the key signs that your battery is AGM and how you can use them to make an informed decision when buying a new battery.</p>

<h2>What Is AGM Battery Technology?</h2>

<p>AGM batteries are a type of sealed lead acid battery that uses an absorbent glass mat to separate the acid and plates. This helps to keep the acid in the battery, which can increase its power, enhance its performance, and prolong its life. It also helps to reduce the risk of spills and explosions, making AGM batteries ideal for use in a range of environments, including automotive, marine, industrial, and medical.</p>

<h2>Signs That Your Battery Is AGM</h2>

<p>So how do you know if your battery is AGM or not? Here are some key signs to look out for:</p>

<ul>
  <li><b>The Battery Is Sealed:</b> AGM batteries are sealed, so you won't be able to add or remove acid from the cells. This helps to keep the acid in the battery, which increases its power and prolongs its life.</li>
  <li><b>The Battery Is Maintenance-Free:</b> AGM batteries don't require any maintenance, such as adding acid or water. This makes them ideal for a range of applications, such as automotive, marine, and industrial.</li>
  <li><b>The Battery Has a Long Life-Span:</b> AGM batteries are designed to last for a long time, often up to 10 years or more. This makes them an ideal choice for those who want a battery that will last a long time.</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Test If Your Battery Is AGM</h2>

<p>If you're not sure if your battery is AGM or not, there are a few simple tests you can do to find out. These include:</p>

<ul>
  <li><b>Check the Battery's Label:</b> Many AGM batteries will have a label that clearly states that they are AGM. This can help you to identify the battery quickly and easily.</li>
  <li><b>Check the Battery's Design:</b> AGM batteries are usually designed differently to conventional lead-acid batteries. They tend to be taller and have a sealed design, with no vents or openings.</li>
  <li><b>Check the Battery's Voltage:</b> AGM batteries usually have a higher voltage than conventional lead-acid batteries. This can be a good indicator that the battery is an AGM.</li>
</ul>
