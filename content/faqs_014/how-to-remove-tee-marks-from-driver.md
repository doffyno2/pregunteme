<h2>What Causes Tee Marks?</h2>

<p>Tee marks are caused by the club head hitting the ground when hitting a shot. This can be due to a number of factors, including the angle of the club face when striking the ball or due to the club head being too close to the ground. A club head that is too close to the ground can create a divot in the turf, and this can cause a tee mark to develop in the area.</p>

<h2>How to Remove Tee Marks from Driver</h2>

<p>The best way to remove tee marks from driver is to start by cleaning the club head with a soft cloth. This will help remove any dirt that has built up on the club head, and it will make it easier to identify the tee marks. Once the club head is clean, use a soft bristle brush to gently scrub the area where the tee mark is located. This will help to remove any debris that has become stuck in the grooves of the driver.</p>

<p>Once the area is cleared, you can then use a mild abrasive cleaner to help remove the tee marks. These cleaners are designed to help remove dirt and grime without damaging the club head.
