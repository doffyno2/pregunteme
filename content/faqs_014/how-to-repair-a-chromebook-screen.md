<h2>Tools and Materials Needed</h2>

<p>Before you begin the repair process, you will need to gather some tools and materials. <h2>Disassembling the Chromebook</h2>

<p>The first step in repairing a Chromebook screen is to disassemble the device. Start by removing the battery and any other components that may be connected to the device. Once you have those removed, use the Phillips screwdriver to remove the screws located on the back of the Chromebook. Next, use the plastic opening tools to carefully pry off the back cover of the device. Be careful not to damage any of the internal components.</p>

<h2>Removing the Broken Screen</h2>

<p>Once you have the Chromebook disassembled, you can begin the process of removing the broken screen. Start by disconnecting the LCD ribbon cable from the motherboard. <h2>Installing the Replacement Screen</h2>
