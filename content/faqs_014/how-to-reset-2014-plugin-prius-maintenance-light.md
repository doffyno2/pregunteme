<p>The 2014 Plugin Prius is a popular hybrid vehicle that features a maintenance light. This light is designed to indicate when the car needs to have its fluid levels checked, filters changed, and other routine tasks performed. It's important to keep this light in check to ensure your vehicle is running smoothly and efficiently. Fortunately, it's easy to reset the 2014 Plugin Prius maintenance light.</p>

<h2>Step One: Check the Maintenance Light</h2>

<p>The first thing you should do when resetting the maintenance light is to check it. The light should be steady and not blinking, which indicates that all the fluids and filters are up to date and the car is in good condition. If the light is blinking, this could indicate a bigger problem and you should take your car to a certified mechanic for a check-up.</p>

<h2>Step Two: Locate the Reset Button</h2>

<p>Next, you'll need to locate the reset button. This is usually located on the dashboard of the car, near the steering wheel. It may be a small button that you'll need to press and hold for a few seconds. It may also be a knob that you'll need to turn clockwise until it clicks.</p>

<h2>Step Three: Reset the Maintenance Light</h2>

<p>Once you've located the reset button, you can now reset the maintenance light. To do this, simply press and hold the reset button for a few seconds. If it's a knob, turn it clockwise until it clicks. This will reset the maintenance light and it should now be off.</p>

<h2>Step Four: Check the Light Again</h2>

<p>Once you've reset the maintenance light, it's important to check that it is off. If it's still on, you may need to take your car to a certified mechanic for a check-up. Alternatively, you can try resetting the light again.</p>
