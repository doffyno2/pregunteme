<h2>Why Soak a Whetstone?</h2>

<p>Soaking a whetstone is important because it helps to keep the stone wet while you work. This is important because a dry stone will quickly become clogged with metal particles, making it much less effective. A wet stone will also help to prevent the blade from overheating while you sharpen it.</p>

<p>When a whetstone is soaked in water, the water molecules are absorbed into the stone. This helps to keep the stone lubricated while you work, allowing the blade to slide across the surface more easily. Soaking also helps to remove any impurities that may be present in the stone.</p>

<h2>How to Soak a Whetstone</h2>

<p>Soaking a whetstone is a simple process. Start by filling a container with room temperature water. Place the whetstone into the water and let it soak for the recommended amount of time. Make sure the stone is completely submerged in the water. Once the stone has finished soaking, it's ready to use.</p>

<p>It's important to note that some whetstones are made of porous materials that can absorb water.
