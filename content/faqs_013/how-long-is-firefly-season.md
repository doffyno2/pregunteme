<h2>Where Do Fireflies Live?</h2>

Fireflies are found on every continent except Antarctica. They live in a wide range of climates, from humid tropical rainforests to arid deserts. In the United States, they are most commonly found in the eastern and midwestern states, but they can be found as far west as California, Arizona, and New Mexico.

<h2>What Time of Year are Fireflies Most Active?</h2>

Fireflies are most active in late May to early June, when temperatures are warm and the nights are long. Depending on the species, fireflies may be seen in the twilight hours of late spring and early summer. In the Northern Hemisphere, firefly season typically lasts from May to August. In the Southern Hemisphere, it typically lasts from October to February.

<h2>What Conditions Do Fireflies Need to Thrive?</h2>

Fireflies need warm temperatures, long nights, and moist soil to thrive. They are most active on humid nights with little wind. Additionally, they need a moist environment to survive and lay their eggs, so fireflies are often found near streams and ponds.

<h2>Do All Fireflies Glow?</h2>

Not all fireflies glow. In fact, some species do not glow at all. These non-glowing fireflies feed on nectar and pollen and do not use their light to attract mates. The glowing species, however, use their light to communicate with each other and to find mates.

<h2>Are Fireflies Dangerous?</h2>

Fireflies are not dangerous to humans. They do not bite or sting and are harmless to touch. In fact, many people find their presence to be calming and peaceful.
