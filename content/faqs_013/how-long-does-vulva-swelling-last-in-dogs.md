<h2>What Causes Vulva Swelling in Dogs?</h2>

Vulva swelling in dogs can be caused by a number of different factors. The most common cause is a urinary tract infection, which can cause the vulva to swell up. Other possible causes include an allergic reaction, a cyst or tumor, or even an abscess. In rare cases, vulva swelling can be a sign of a more serious condition such as heatstroke, diabetes, or cancer.

If your pup is exhibiting any of these symptoms, it is important to take them to the vet as soon as possible.

<h2>How Long Does Vulva Swelling Last in Dogs?</h2>

The length of time that vulva swelling lasts in dogs can vary depending on the underlying cause. If the swelling is due to an underlying medical condition, such as diabetes or cancer, it may not resolve until the condition is treated.
