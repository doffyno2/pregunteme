<p>The United States Postal Service (USPS) offers a convenient and secure way to send money to friends and family, called a Postal Money Order. Postal Money Orders are much like cash, and can be used to pay bills or make purchases. This article will explain how to fill out a Postal Money Order and provide some tips for avoiding common mistakes.</p>

<h2>Steps for Filling Out a Postal Money Order</h2>

<p>Filling out a Postal Money Order is a simple process. Here are the steps to complete your Postal Money Order:</p> 

<ol>
    <li>Write the name and address of the person or business receiving the money order in the “Pay to the Order Of” line.</li>
    <li>Write the amount of the money order in numbers in the “Amount” line.</li>
    <li>Write the amount of the money order in words in the “Payable in U.S. Currency” line.</li>
    <li>Sign your name in the “Purchaser’s Signature” line.</li>
    <li>Write your address in the “Purchaser’s Address” line.</li>
</ol>

<p>To ensure the money order is secure, it is important to make sure you have filled out all sections correctly. This includes double-checking that you have written the correct name, address, and amount for the recipient.</p>

<h2>Tips for Avoiding Common Mistakes</h2>

<p>When filling out a Postal Money Order, it is important to avoid common mistakes. Here are some tips to help you avoid common mistakes:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Make sure you have written the correct name and address of the person or business receiving the money order.</li>
    <li>Verify the amount of the money order to make sure it is correct.</li>
    <li>Write the amount of the money order in words to ensure accuracy.</li>
    <li>Make sure you sign your name in the signature line.</li>
    <li>Write your address in the Purchaser’s Address line.</li>
</ul>

<p>Following these tips will help you avoid common mistakes when filling out a Postal Money Order.</p>
