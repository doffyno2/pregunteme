How to Break in Leather Riding Boots

Leather riding boots are an essential piece of kit for any horse rider, providing protection, style and support. But, breaking in a pair of leather boots can be a tricky and time-consuming process. In this article, we’ll explain the best ways to break in your leather riding boots, so you can get out there and hit the saddle in comfort.



Breaking in a pair of leather riding boots is essential for getting the most out of them. Leather boots are a long-term investment, so it’s important to ensure that you break them in correctly, to get the most out of them. The best way to break in your leather riding boots is to wear them often and in different conditions, while taking care to keep them clean and conditioned. 

<h2>The Benefits of Breaking in Your Leather Riding Boots</h2>

Breaking in your leather riding boots can have many benefits. Doing so will make them more comfortable, as the leather will mould to the shape of your feet, creating a custom fit. Breaking in your leather riding boots will also help to protect your feet, as they will fit more snugly, making them less likely to slip while riding. Additionally, breaking in your boots will make them last longer, as the leather will be less likely to crack or tear over time. 

<h2>How to Break in Your Leather Riding Boots</h2>

Breaking in your leather riding boots can seem like a daunting task, but it doesn’t have to be. Here are some tips on how to break in your leather riding boots:

<h3>1. Wear Them Around the House</h3>

This is the best way to start breaking in your leather riding boots. Start by wearing your boots around the house for short periods of time, such as when you’re watching TV or doing chores. This will help the leather to mould to the shape of your feet, as well as allowing you to get used to the feel of the boots.

<h3>2. Wear Them Outdoors</h3>

Once you’re comfortable wearing your boots around the house, you can start wearing them outside. Wear them for short periods of time while you’re running errands or doing light activities, such as walking the dog. As you wear them more, the leather will mould to the shape of your feet and become more comfortable.

<h3>3. Wear Them While Riding</h3>

Once you’re comfortable wearing your boots for short periods of time, you can start wearing them while riding. Wear them for short riding sessions, and gradually increase the length of your rides as your boots become more comfortable. This will help you to get used to the feel of the boots, as well as allowing the leather to mould to the shape of your feet. 

<h3>4. Keep Them Clean and Conditioned</h3>

It’s important to keep your leather riding boots clean and conditioned, as this will help to keep them supple and prevent them from cracking or tearing. Use a soft brush to clean off any dirt or debris, and then use a leather conditioner to keep the leather soft and supple.

You should wear your leather riding boots as often as possible, while taking care to keep them clean and conditioned. 

<h3>4. What is the best way to clean leather riding boots?</h3>
