<p>Ranch dressing is a popular condiment and salad dressing made from a combination of buttermilk, mayonnaise, herbs, and spices. It's often served as a dip for vegetables or as a salad dressing. But how many calories are in a serving of ranch dressing?</p>

<p>A one-tablespoon serving of ranch dressing contains about 90 calories. That's about five percent of the recommended daily caloric intake for a 2,000 calorie diet. The calorie count for ranch dressing can vary depending on the brand, but it's generally around the same amount. A two-tablespoon serving of ranch dressing contains about 180 calories.</p>

<p>Ranch dressing is a high-fat food, with 9 grams of fat in a one-tablespoon serving. Most of that fat is from the mayonnaise and buttermilk, both of which are high in saturated fat. A two-tablespoon serving of ranch dressing contains about 18 grams of fat.</p>

<p>In addition to fat, ranch dressing contains a good amount of sodium. A one-tablespoon serving contains about 160 milligrams of sodium, and a two-tablespoon serving contains about 320 milligrams of sodium. Eating too much sodium can increase your risk of high blood pressure, heart disease, and stroke.</p>

<p>Ranch dressing also contains a moderate amount of carbohydrates. A one-tablespoon serving contains about 1 gram of carbohydrates, and a two-tablespoon serving contains about 2 grams of carbohydrates. Most of the carbohydrates in ranch dressing come from the buttermilk.</p>
