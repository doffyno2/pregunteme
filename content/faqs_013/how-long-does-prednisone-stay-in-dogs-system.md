<h2>What is Prednisone?</h2>

Prednisone is a synthetic corticosteroid. It is similar to the hormones produced naturally by the body's adrenal gland. It is used to reduce inflammation and suppress the immune system. Prednisone is used to treat a wide range of conditions, including allergies, asthma, arthritis, skin diseases and ulcerative colitis. Prednisone is also used as an immunosuppressant in cases of organ transplantation.

<h2>How Does Prednisone Work?</h2>

Prednisone works by blocking the production of certain chemicals in the body called cytokines. Cytokines are responsible for inflammation and the body's immune response. By blocking their production, prednisone reduces inflammation and suppresses the immune system. This can reduce pain, swelling, and other symptoms related to the condition being treated.

<h2>What Are the Side Effects of Prednisone?</h2>

Prednisone can cause a variety of side effects in dogs. The most common side effects are increased thirst and urination, increased appetite, panting, and weight gain. Other side effects include vomiting, diarrhea, increased panting, and changes in behavior. It is important to monitor your dog closely while they are on prednisone and to speak to your veterinarian if you notice any changes in your dog's behavior.

<h2>How Long Does Prednisone Stay in Dogs System?</h2>

The amount of time that prednisone stays in your dog's system will depend on several factors, including the dosage, how often your dog is taking the medication, and individual factors such as age and weight. In general, it takes about three to five days for prednisone to completely leave your dog's system.
