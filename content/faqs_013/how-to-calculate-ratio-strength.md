<h2>What is Ratio Strength?</h2>

<p>Ratio strength is a measure of how well a company is managing its finances. It is calculated by comparing the company’s performance against industry averages. For example, if a company’s sales are higher than the industry average, its ratio strength is said to be strong. Conversely, if a company’s sales are lower than the industry average, its ratio strength is said to be weak.</p>

<p>Ratio strength can also be used to compare a company’s performance against its own past performance. If a company’s sales are higher than in the past, its ratio strength is said to be increasing. Conversely, if a company’s sales are lower than in the past, its ratio strength is said to be decreasing.</p>

<h2>What Ratios Are Used to Calculate Ratio Strength?</h2>

<p>The most common ratios used to calculate ratio strength are the Current Ratio, the Quick Ratio, and the Debt-to-Equity Ratio. The Current Ratio measures a company’s ability to pay its short-term debts, while the Quick Ratio measures its ability to pay its short-term debts without relying on inventory. The Debt-to-Equity Ratio measures a company’s debt relative to its equity.</p>

<p>To calculate the Current Ratio, divide a company’s current assets by its current liabilities. A ratio of 1.0 or higher indicates that the company has enough assets to cover its liabilities. A ratio of less than 1.0 indicates that the company does not have enough assets to cover its liabilities.</p>

<p>To calculate the Quick Ratio, divide a company’s current assets minus its inventory by its current liabilities. A ratio of 1.0 or higher indicates that the company has enough assets to cover its liabilities without relying on inventory. A ratio of less than 1.0 indicates that the company does not have enough assets to cover its liabilities without relying on inventory.</p>

<p>To calculate the Debt-to-Equity Ratio, divide a company’s total liabilities by its total equity. A ratio of 1.0 or higher indicates that the company has more debt than equity. A ratio of less than 1.0 indicates that the company has more equity than debt.</p>

<h2>What is a Good Ratio Strength?</h2>

<p>The ideal ratio strength varies depending on the industry and the company’s goals. Generally speaking, a ratio strength of 1.0 or higher is considered good for the Current Ratio and the Quick Ratio, and a ratio strength of less than 1.0 is considered good for the Debt-to-Equity Ratio. However, it is important to note that these are just guidelines and there may be cases where a different ratio strength is more appropriate.</p>

<h2>How to Calculate Ratio Strength</h2>

<p>To calculate ratio strength, you must first identify the industry averages for the ratios you are measuring. Then, divide the company’s performance by the industry average to get the ratio strength. For example, if a company’s Current Ratio is 1.2 and the industry average is 1.0, then the company’s ratio strength is 1.2/1.0 = 1.2, which is considered good.</p>
