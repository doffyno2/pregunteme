<h2>What is Time and Velocity?</h2>

<p>Time is an abstract concept that is used to measure the duration of an event or the interval between two events. It is measured in seconds, minutes, hours, days, weeks, and years. Velocity, on the other hand, is the rate of change of the position of an object with respect to time. Velocity is usually expressed in terms of speed, which is the rate at which an object covers a given distance in a given time.</p>

<h2>How to Calculate Total Distance Traveled?</h2>

<p>The total distance traveled can be calculated by using the following formula: total distance = velocity x time. This formula assumes that the velocity remains constant throughout the duration of the travel. If the velocity changes during the travel, then the total distance traveled must be calculated by considering the average velocity of the object.</p>

<h2>Example of Calculating Total Distance Traveled</h2>

<p>Let's say that an object is traveling at a velocity of 60 miles per hour for a total duration of 1 hour. To calculate the total distance traveled, you need to multiply the velocity (60 miles per hour) by the time (1 hour). Therefore, the total distance traveled would be 60 miles.</p>

<h2>What is Average Velocity?</h2>
