<h2>What is a Calorie?</h2>

A calorie is a unit of energy. Our bodies use them to power our daily activities, from walking and talking to exercising and digesting food. Calories come from the food we eat and the drinks we consume.

<h2>How Many Calories Should You Eat?</h2>

The amount of calories you need to eat each day depends on your age, sex, height, weight, and activity level. Generally speaking, the average adult needs around 2,000 calories per day to maintain their weight. 

<h2>What Happens When You Eat Fewer Calories?</h2>

When you eat fewer calories than your body needs, your body enters a state of caloric deficit. This means your body has to draw energy from stored sources, such as fat or muscle, to make up for the lack of energy from food. This process is known as weight loss.

<h2>How Much Weight Can You Lose Eating 500 Calories a Day?</h2>
