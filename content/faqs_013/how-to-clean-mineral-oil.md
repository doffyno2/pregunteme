<p>Mineral oil is a highly effective lubricant and is widely used in many industries, from automotive to manufacturing. It is also used in the home, for things such as cutting boards, countertops, and even furniture. Over time, the mineral oil can become dirty, leaving behind a film or residue that can be difficult to clean.</p>

<p>Mineral oil is a petroleum-based product, so it is not biodegradable and can be difficult to remove. Fortunately, there are several methods that can be used to clean mineral oil, depending on the type of surface it is on. This guide provides an overview of the best ways to clean mineral oil, including what products and tools are needed.</p>

<p>Before attempting to clean mineral oil, it is important to have the right supplies on hand. Depending on the type of surface the oil is on and the amount of oil that needs to be cleaned, the supplies needed may vary. Generally, the following supplies are necessary:</p>

<ul>
  <li>White distilled vinegar</li>
  <li>Baking soda</li>
  <li>Dish soap</li>
  <li>Dish rag or sponge</li>
  <li>Paper towels</li>
  <li>Clean cloth</li>
</ul>

<h2>Removing Mineral Oil From Wood</h2>

<p>If the mineral oil is on a wood surface, such as a cutting board, the best way to remove it is to use a combination of white distilled vinegar and baking soda. Start by mixing equal parts of white distilled vinegar and baking soda in a bowl. Once the mixture is ready, apply it to the affected area and let it sit for a few minutes. Then, scrub the area with a damp cloth or sponge. Rinse off the mixture with warm water and dry the surface with a clean cloth.</p>

<h2>Removing Mineral Oil From Stone</h2>

<p>Stone surfaces, such as countertops, can be tricky to clean. To remove mineral oil from a stone surface, start by wiping it down with a damp cloth or sponge and some mild dish soap. If the oil is stubborn, try using a mixture of white distilled vinegar and baking soda. Mix the two ingredients together and apply it to the surface. Let the mixture sit for a few minutes before scrubbing it off with a damp cloth or sponge. Once the mixture is removed, rinse the area with warm water and dry it with a clean cloth.</p>

<h2>Removing Mineral Oil From Metal</h2>

<p>Metal surfaces can be difficult to clean because of their porous nature. To remove mineral oil from metal, start by wiping down the surface with a damp cloth or sponge and some mild dish soap. If the oil is stubborn, try using a mixture of white distilled vinegar and baking soda. Mix the two ingredients together and apply it to the surface. Let the mixture sit for a few minutes before scrubbing it off with a damp cloth or sponge. Once the mixture is removed, rinse the area with warm water and dry it with a clean cloth.</p>

<h2>Removing Mineral Oil From Fabric</h2>

<p>Fabric can also be difficult to clean due to its porous nature. To remove mineral oil from fabric, start by blotting the area with a paper towel to absorb as much of the oil as possible. Then, mix a solution of equal parts white distilled vinegar and baking soda and apply it to the affected area. Let the mixture sit for a few minutes before scrubbing it off with a damp cloth or sponge. Once the mixture is removed, rinse the area with warm water and dry it with a clean cloth.</p>
