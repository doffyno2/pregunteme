<p>Broken glasses arm can be a major headache. It's not just an annoyance, it can also be a safety hazard if you’re not careful. Fortunately, it’s a relatively easy fix and can be done at home with just a few tools. In this article, we’ll discuss how to fix broken glasses arm with glue.</p>

<h2>Tools and Materials Needed for the Job</h2>

<p>To fix broken glasses arm with glue, you will need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Glue</li>
  <li>Tweezers</li>
  <li>Scissors</li>
  <li>Needle</li>
  <li>Toothpick</li>
  <li>Cotton swab</li>
  <li>Cleaning cloth</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step-by-Step Guide to Fix Broken Glasses Arm with Glue</h2>

<p>Once you’ve gathered all the necessary tools and materials, you can begin the repair process. </p>

<ol>
  <li>The first step is to clean the frame and lens. Use a cleaning cloth to remove any dirt or debris that may have collected on the frame or lens. </li>
  <li>Once the frame and lens are clean, you can begin to repair the broken arm. Use tweezers to carefully remove the broken piece of the arm. </li>
  <li>Next, take the needle and use it to puncture the end of the broken arm. This will help create a flat surface for the glue to adhere to. </li>
  <li>Apply a small amount of glue to the punctured end of the arm and then use the tweezers to reattach the broken piece of the arm. </li>
  <li>Once the arm is reattached, use a cotton swab to wipe off any excess glue. </li>
  <li>Allow the glue to dry for at least 24 hours before wearing the glasses. </li>
</ol>

<h2>Tips for Fixing Broken Glasses Arm with Glue</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Use a high-quality glue that is designed for use on glasses frames. </li>
  <li>Allow the glue to dry completely before wearing the glasses. </li>
  <li>If the arm is still loose after gluing, you can use a toothpick to add additional glue to the inside of the arm. </li>
</ul>
