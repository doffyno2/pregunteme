<h2>What is Certified Mail?</h2>

<p>Certified mail is a type of mail service offered by the United States Postal Service (USPS). It is an official form of mail delivery that requires a signature from the recipient upon delivery. Certified mail is also a traceable form of mail delivery, as the sender can track the progress of their mail through the USPS website. Certified mail is typically used for important documents or packages, as it is more secure than regular mail.</p>

<h2>How to Request Certified Mail</h2>

<p>To send certified mail, you must first purchase a certified mail label from the USPS. You can purchase these labels online or at any post office. Once you have purchased the label, you must attach it to your envelope or package. The label will contain a unique tracking number that you can use to track the progress of your mail.</p>

<h2>How to Check the Status of Your Certified Mail</h2>

<p>Once you have sent your certified mail, you can track its progress through the USPS website. Once you have entered the tracking number, you will be able to view the current status of your mail. You will also be able to view the date and time your mail was delivered, as well as the signature of the recipient.</p>
