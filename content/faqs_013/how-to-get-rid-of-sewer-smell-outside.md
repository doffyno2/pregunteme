How To Get Rid Of Sewer Smell Outside


It is not unusual for homeowners to experience sewer odors outside the home. Sewer odor outside the home can be caused by a number of different sources, including sewage backups, plumbing traps that are not functioning properly, or even a broken sewer line. Knowing how to identify the source of the sewer odor and how to properly address the issue can help you get rid of the smell quickly and efficiently.

<h2>What Causes Sewer Smell Outside?</h2>

The most common cause of sewer odors outside the home is a broken sewer line or a plumbing trap that is not working properly. A plumbing trap is a U-shaped pipe that is designed to hold water and prevent sewer gases from entering the home. If the trap is too far away from the point of use, the water can evaporate and the sewer gases can escape. Additionally, a broken sewer line can cause sewage backups and allow sewer gases to enter the home.

In order to identify the source of the sewer odor, you must first determine where the smell is coming from. If the odor is coming from a sink or tub, it is likely caused by a blockage in the drain or a plumbing trap that is not working properly.

<h2>What Can I Do to Get Rid of the Sewer Odor?</h2>

Once you have identified the source of the sewer odor, you can take steps to get rid of it.
