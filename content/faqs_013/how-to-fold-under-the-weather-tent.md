<h2>Gather Your Materials</h2>

<p>Before you can start folding, you’ll need to gather your materials. You’ll need the weather tent itself, as well as any poles and stakes that you’ll be using to set it up. Make sure that you have all of the necessary components before you begin. </p>

<h2>Lay Out the Tent</h2>

<p>Next, you’ll need to lay out the tent on the ground. Make sure that the floor of the tent is facing up, and position the poles and stakes in their respective positions. If you’re using a ground tarp, lay it out as well. </p>

<h2>Remove Any Poles or Stakes</h2>

<p>Once the tent is laid out, you’ll need to remove any poles or stakes that are attached to it. This includes any that are attached to the walls of the tent, as well as any that are attached to the ground. Be careful not to damage the tent while doing this. </p>

<h2>Fold the Tent in Half</h2>

<p>Once the poles and stakes have been removed, you’ll be ready to start folding the tent in half. Start at the top of the tent, and fold it over in a zigzag pattern. As you do this, make sure that the fabric is evenly distributed on both sides. When you’re finished, the tent should be folded in half, with the floor of the tent facing out. </p>

<h2>Fold the Sides Inward</h2>

<p>Once the tent is folded in half, you’ll need to fold the sides of the tent inward. Start at one end and fold the sides inwards in an accordion-like pattern. Make sure that the folds are even and symmetrical. Once you’ve finished folding the sides inwards, the tent should be folded into a neat, rectangular package. </p>

<h2>Secure the Tent</h2>

<p>Once the tent is folded, you’ll need to secure it in order to prevent it from unfolding. This will help keep the tent secure and prevent it from unfolding. </p>
