Head gasket failure is one of the most common problems faced by Subaru owners. It can cause a variety of issues, from decreased performance to engine overheating and even complete engine failure. But just how much does it cost to repair a Subaru head gasket? Let’s take a look.

<h2>The Cost of Parts and Labor</h2>

The cost of replacing a head gasket can vary greatly depending on the model and year of your Subaru, as well as the type of head gasket you need. Generally, head gaskets can cost anywhere from $50 to $500. Labor costs can add up quickly, too. Depending on the complexity of the job and the hourly rate of the mechanic, labor can cost between $400 and $1,500. So, it’s important to factor in both parts and labor when estimating the total cost of head gasket repair.

<h2>Common Causes of Head Gasket Failure</h2>

Head gasket failure can be caused by a variety of factors, including: 

<ul>
    <li>Overheating engine</li>
    <li>Improperly installed head gasket</li>
    <li>Leaking coolant</li>
    <li>Low engine oil</li>
    <li>Excessive oil pressure</li>
    <li>Worn out head gasket</li>
</ul>

In some cases, head gasket failure can be caused by a combination of these factors. It’s important to get your Subaru checked out by a qualified mechanic if you suspect head gasket failure.

<ul>
    <li>White smoke from the tailpipe</li>
    <li>Overheating engine</li>
    <li>Leaking coolant</li>
    <li>Fouled spark plugs</li>
    <li>Milky oil</li>
    <li>Low engine compression</li>
    <li>Increased oil consumption</li>
</ul>

If you notice any of these symptoms, it’s important to get your Subaru checked out by a qualified mechanic.

<h2>How to Prevent Head Gasket Failure</h2>

<ul>
