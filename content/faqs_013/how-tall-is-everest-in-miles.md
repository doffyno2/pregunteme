<h2>What is Altitude?</h2>

<p>Altitude is the height of an object or a point relative to sea level. It is usually measured in feet or meters and can be used to describe the height of mountains, hills, and other geographical features.</p>

<p>Altitude is different from elevation, which is the height of a point relative to the ground. For example, the elevation of a mountain peak is the height at which it reaches its peak, while its altitude is the height of that peak relative to sea level.</p>

<h2>How is Altitude Measured?</h2>

<p>The most common way to measure altitude is with a barometer, which is an instrument that measures atmospheric pressure. By measuring the atmospheric pressure at different altitudes, it is possible to determine the height of a point relative to sea level.</p>

<p>Another way to measure altitude is with a Global Positioning System (GPS) device. GPS devices use satellites to calculate the height of a point relative to sea level.</p>
