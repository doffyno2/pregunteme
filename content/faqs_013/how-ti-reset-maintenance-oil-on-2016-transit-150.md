<h2>Step 1: Open the Hood</h2>

<p>The first step in resetting the maintenance oil on your Transit 150 is to open the hood. To do this, locate the hood release lever, which is usually located on the driver's side of the dashboard. Pull the lever to release the hood latch and then lift the hood to open it.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Locate the Oil Filter</h2>

<p>Once you have the hood open, you need to locate the oil filter. This is usually located on the driver's side of the engine compartment. The oil filter looks like a large, cylindrical canister. Make sure to note the orientation of the oil filter so that you can replace it in the same way after the reset is complete.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Remove the Oil Filter</h2>

<p>Now that you've located the oil filter, you need to remove it. To do this, use an oil filter wrench to loosen the filter. Once it's loose, you can carefully remove it from the engine. Make sure to place it in a safe place so that you don't lose it.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Replace the Oil Filter</h2>

<p>Once the old oil filter is removed, you need to replace it with a new one. To do this, simply reverse the process that you used to remove the filter. Make sure to orient the new filter in the same way as the old one, and make sure to tighten it securely with the oil filter wrench.</p>

<h2>Step 5: Reset the Maintenance Oil</h2>

<p>Now that the oil filter is replaced, you can reset the maintenance oil. To do this, locate the maintenance oil reset button. This is usually located near the oil filter, but can vary depending on the model of Transit 150. Press and hold the reset button for a few seconds until the maintenance light turns off. This indicates that the reset is complete.</p>

<h2>Step 6: Close the Hood and Test Drive</h2>

<p>Once the reset is complete, you can close the hood and take your Transit 150 for a test drive. Make sure to drive it for a few minutes to ensure that the reset was successful. If the maintenance light comes back on, you may need to repeat the process.</p>
