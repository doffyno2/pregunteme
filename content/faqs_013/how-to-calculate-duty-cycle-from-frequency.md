<h2>What is Duty Cycle?</h2>

<p>Duty cycle is a measure of the fraction of time during a period of a repetitive waveform that the waveform is active. It is often described in terms of a percentage, where it is the ratio of the amount of time that the waveform is active to the total cycle time of the waveform. Duty cycle can be used to calculate the frequency of a signal, as well as the average power of the waveform.</p>

<h2>How to Calculate Duty Cycle from Frequency</h2>

<p>In order to calculate duty cycle from frequency, you will need to know the frequency of the waveform. The frequency is the number of times that the waveform cycles in one second. Once you know the frequency, you can calculate the duty cycle by dividing the amount of time that the waveform is active by the total cycle time of the waveform. The total cycle time is equal to one divided by the frequency. The result of this calculation is expressed as a percentage.</p>
