<h2>What is the Shelf Life of Cooked Fish?</h2>

The shelf life of cooked fish depends on the specific type of fish, the way it was cooked, and how it was stored. Generally, cooked fish will last 3-4 days in the refrigerator, although it can last up to 7 days if the fish is cooked and stored properly. 

When it comes to cooked fish stored at room temperature, the shelf life is shorter. Cooked fish should not be left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours, as this increases the risk of food-borne illness. 

<h2>How to Store Cooked Fish?</h2>

When storing cooked fish, it’s important to ensure that it is stored properly. To do this, you will need to make sure that it is stored in an airtight container and kept in the refrigerator. You should also make sure to label and date the container so that you can keep track of how long the fish has been stored. 

<h2>How to Tell if Cooked Fish is Bad?</h2>

Cooked fish will go bad if it is not stored properly or left out at room temperature for too long. It’s important to inspect the fish before consuming it to make sure that it is still safe to eat. 

If the fish has a sour or off smell, slimy texture, or discoloration, it is best to discard it as it has likely gone bad. Additionally, if the fish has been left out at room temperature for more than 2 hours, it is best to discard it as it is no longer safe to consume. 

<h2>How to Reheat Cooked Fish?</h2>

If you want to reheat cooked fish, it is important to make sure that it is done safely. To do this, you should place the fish in an oven-safe dish and cover it with aluminum foil. Then, preheat your oven to 350°F and bake the fish for 10-15 minutes, or until it is heated through. 

<h2>How to Freeze Cooked Fish?</h2>

If you want to freeze cooked fish, you should make sure that it is stored in an airtight, freezer-safe container. It’s also important to label and date the container so that you can keep track of how long the fish has been stored. Generally, cooked fish will last up to 3 months in the freezer.
