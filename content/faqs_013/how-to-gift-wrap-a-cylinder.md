<h2>What You Need To Gift Wrap A Cylinder</h2>

Gift wrapping a cylinder can be a bit tricky, but with the right materials it can be a breeze. Here is what you need to get started: 

<ul>
<li>Wrapping paper (at least 2 feet longer than the circumference of the cylinder)</li>
<li>Tape</li>
<li>Scissors</li>
<li>Tissue paper (optional)</li>
<li>Ribbon (optional)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step-By-Step Guide To Gift Wrap a Cylinder</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Measure and Cut Wrapping Paper</h3>

Start by measuring the circumference of your cylinder. Add 2 feet to this number and use this as the length for your wrapping paper. Cut the paper to the desired length. Place the wrapping paper on a flat surface with the pattern facing down.

<h3>Step 2: Place the Cylinder on the Wrapping Paper</h3>

Place the cylinder on the wrapping paper so that it is centered. Make sure to leave enough wrapping paper on each side to cover the ends of the cylinder.

<h3>Step 3: Wrap the Cylinder</h3>

Starting from the bottom, wrap the cylinder in the wrapping paper. Make sure to overlap the edges of the paper and use tape to secure it in place.

<h3>Step 4: Fold the Ends of the Wrapping Paper</h3>

Once the cylinder is fully wrapped, fold the ends of the wrapping paper into one another. Secure this in place with tape.

<h3>Step 5: Add Ribbon and Tissue Paper</h3>

If desired, you can add ribbon and tissue paper to your gift wrap. Simply tie the ribbon around the cylinder and add tissue paper to the ends.
