<h2>What Is An Umbilical Hernia?</h2>

<p>An umbilical hernia is an opening or weakness in the abdominal muscles near the umbilicus (belly button) of a dog. It is most commonly seen in puppies, but can also occur in adult dogs. The hernia can vary in size, from a small opening no larger than a pea to a large opening several centimeters across. In some cases, the hernia can contain a portion of the intestines, which can lead to complications if they become trapped, or strangulated.</p>

<h2>What Causes An Umbilical Hernia?</h2>

<p>Umbilical hernias can be caused by a number of factors, including genetics, birth trauma, obesity, or a weakened abdominal wall. Older dogs may also develop hernias due to wear and tear on the muscles in the area.</p>

<h2>What Are The Symptoms Of An Umbilical Hernia?</h2>

<p>The most common symptoms of an umbilical hernia are a bulge or lump near the belly button, pain, and vomiting. In some cases, the hernia may be visible without palpation, while in other cases the hernia may be too small to feel. If the hernia contains any of the intestines, the dog may also have difficulty passing stool.</p>

<h2>How Is An Umbilical Hernia Diagnosed?</h2>

<p>Umbilical hernias are typically diagnosed through physical examination. During the exam, your veterinarian will feel the area around your dog's belly button for any lumps or bulges. In some cases, your veterinarian may also recommend further testing, such as an abdominal ultrasound or x-ray, to confirm the diagnosis.</p>

<h2>What Is The Treatment For An Umbilical Hernia?</h2>

<p>The treatment for an umbilical hernia will depend on the size and severity of the hernia. In some cases, a hernia will resolve on its own without any treatment. However, if the hernia is large or contains any of the intestines, surgery may be necessary to repair the hernia and prevent any further complications.</p>
