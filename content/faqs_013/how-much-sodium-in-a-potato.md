Potatoes are a staple in many diets around the world. They are a versatile vegetable, low in fat and high in potassium, magnesium and vitamin C. However, they can contain relatively high amounts of sodium. Knowing how much sodium is in a potato can help you plan your diet and make sure you are getting enough but not too much of this important mineral. 

<h2>What Is Sodium?</h2>

Sodium is an essential mineral that helps regulate the amount of water in the body, assists in nerve and muscle function, and helps with blood pressure regulation. The body needs some sodium to function, but too much can cause serious health problems. The American Heart Association recommends that adults consume no more than 2,300 milligrams of sodium per day, and those with high blood pressure, diabetes, or kidney disease should consume no more than 1,500 milligrams per day. 

<h2>How Much Sodium Is In A Potato?</h2>

The amount of sodium in a potato depends on the type of potato and how it is prepared. A plain, baked potato with the skin on contains 18 milligrams of sodium. If you remove the skin, the sodium content drops to 12 milligrams. French fries contain between 135 and 270 milligrams of sodium per serving, depending on the size of the fries. Mashed potatoes with butter and salt added have between 300 and 600 milligrams of sodium per serving.

<h2>How Can I Reduce The Sodium In My Potatoes?</h2>

There are several ways to reduce the sodium content of potatoes. One easy way is to use less salt when cooking them. When baking potatoes, leave the skin on to retain more of the natural minerals and vitamins. If you are making mashed potatoes, use low-sodium broth or milk instead of butter and salt. Consider adding herbs, garlic, or other seasonings to potatoes instead of salt.
