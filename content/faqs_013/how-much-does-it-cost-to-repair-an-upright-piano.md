How Much Does It Cost To Repair An Upright Piano?


Pianos are classic instruments that have been around for centuries and have been used by some of the world's most renowned composers and musicians. But even with the highest quality care, pianos can have problems and require repair. So, if you have an upright piano that needs to be repaired, you may be wondering how much it will cost. In this article, we will explore the factors that go into the cost of repairing an upright piano, so you can be prepared when taking it in for service.

<h2>Factors That Impact The Cost Of Piano Repair</h2>

The cost of repairing an upright piano can vary significantly depending on the type of repair that needs to be done and the extent of the damage. Some common factors that will impact the cost of piano repair include:

<h3>Type of Repair</h3>

The type of repair that is needed will have a major impact on the cost of piano repair. Common repairs include tuning, restringing, replacing parts, and refinishing the instrument. Depending on the type of repair, the cost can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars.

<h3>Extent of Damage</h3>

The extent of the damage to the piano will also impact the cost of the repair. If only minor repairs are needed, such as tuning or restringing, the cost will be much lower than if more extensive repairs are needed, such as replacing parts or refinishing the instrument.

<h3>Age of Piano</h3>

The age of the piano will also have an impact on the cost of repair. Older pianos may require more extensive repairs, which can increase the cost.

<h3>Type of Technician</h3>

The type of technician performing the repair will also have an impact on the cost of the repair. A professional technician who specializes in piano repair may charge more than a general technician who is not as experienced with pianos.

<h2>Average Cost To Repair An Upright Piano</h2>

The cost to repair an upright piano can vary significantly depending on the factors discussed above. For minor repairs such as tuning or restringing, the cost can range from $100-$400. For more extensive repairs such as replacing parts or refinishing the instrument, the cost can range from $500-$2500 or more.
