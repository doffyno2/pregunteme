<h3>Truss Bridges</h3>

Truss bridges are another type of bridge that can be used to cross the Mississippi River in Louisiana. These bridges are constructed from a series of triangular frames or trusses. The trusses are connected to form a rigid structure that is strong enough to support the weight of traffic. Truss bridges are commonly used on shorter spans of water and can carry a medium amount of traffic. 

<h3>Cantilever Bridges</h3>

Cantilever bridges are another type of bridge that can be used to cross the Mississippi River in Louisiana. These bridges are constructed from a single beam that is cantilevered out from one side of the river. The beam is supported by towers on either side of the river, allowing the bridge deck to span a long distance without the need for additional support. Cantilever bridges are ideal for long spans of water and can carry a large amount of traffic.
