It’s a creamy dressing made with mayonnaise, buttermilk, garlic, onion, herbs, and spices. While ranch dressing is widely enjoyed, there is some concern about how much sugar is in ranch dressing. In this article, we’ll explore the sugar content of ranch dressing, as well as healthier alternatives. 

<h2>What is the Nutrition Profile of Ranch Dressing?</h2>

Ranch dressing is a mayonnaise-based condiment, with a creamy texture. Most commercial ranch dressings are made with mayonnaise, buttermilk, garlic, onion, herbs and spices. The exact ingredients and nutrition profile of ranch dressing depends on the brand or recipe used. 

A 1-tablespoon (15-gram) serving of commercial ranch dressing typically contains:

<ul>
<li>Calories: 93</li>
<li>Fat: 10 grams</li>
<li>Carbohydrates: 1 gram</li>
<li>Protein: 0.3 grams</li>
</ul>

The fat content of ranch dressing is mostly from the mayonnaise, which is made from vegetable oils. 

<h2>How Much Sugar is in Ranch Dressing?</h2>

Most commercial ranch dressings contain very little sugar. It is possible to find ranch dressings with added sugar, but most brands contain no more than 1 gram of sugar per serving.

The sugar content of ranch dressing is mainly from the buttermilk that is used to make the dressing. Most recipes add a small amount of sugar to the dressing to balance out the tartness of the buttermilk.

If you’re looking for a lower-sugar ranch dressing, it’s best to read the ingredients list and nutrition label on the package to find out how much sugar is in the product. 

<h2>Are There Healthier Alternatives to Ranch Dressing?</h2>

Yes, there are healthier alternatives to ranch dressing. If you’re looking for a lower-sugar option, you can make your own ranch dressing at home. Homemade ranch dressing recipes typically use ingredients like Greek yogurt, sour cream, garlic, onion, herbs, and spices.

These ingredients don’t contain added sugar, so you can control how much sugar is in your homemade ranch dressing. 

You can also look for store-bought ranch dressings made with reduced-fat mayonnaise or Greek yogurt. These types of dressings may contain slightly less fat and fewer calories than regular ranch dressing. 

If you’re looking for a low-calorie alternative to ranch dressing, you can try making a vinaigrette or lemon juice-based dressing. These types of dressings are usually much lower in calories and fat than ranch dressing.
