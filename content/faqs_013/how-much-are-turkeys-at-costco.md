<h2>Types of Turkeys Available at Costco</h2>

<p>Costco offers a variety of different types of turkeys for your Thanksgiving meal. The most common variety, and one of the most popular, is the frozen turkey. These types of turkeys are not pre-cooked, which means you will need to prepare them from scratch. They are also more expensive than frozen turkeys.</p>

<h2>How Much Do Turkeys At Costco Cost?</h2>

<p>The cost of a turkey at Costco depends on the type of bird you select. Frozen turkeys typically cost around $1.50 per pound of turkey, while organic, free range, and heritage turkeys can cost up to $5.00 per pound. The cost can also vary depending on the size of the turkey. A smaller bird will cost less than a larger one. Additionally, you may be able to find special promotions or discounts on turkeys at certain times of the year.</p>

<p>Once you’ve purchased your turkey from Costco, you’ll need to prepare it for your meal. If you purchased a frozen turkey, you’ll need to thaw it first. <p>If you purchased an organic, free range, or heritage turkey, you’ll need to prepare it from scratch.
