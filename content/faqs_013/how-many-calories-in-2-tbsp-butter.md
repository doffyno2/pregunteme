<h2>Types of Butter</h2>

Butter is a dairy product made by churning cream or milk until it separates into solid and liquid components. It contains fat, water, and protein. Depending on the type of butter, there are different amounts of calories in it. 

<h3>Unsalted Butter</h3>

Unsalted butter is the most common type of butter used in cooking and baking. It has a creamy white to pale yellow color and a rich, creamy texture. Unsalted butter typically contains about 102 calories per 2 tablespoons. 

<h3>Salted Butter</h3>

<h3>Clarified Butter</h3>

Clarified butter is butter that has been melted and then had the water and milk solids removed. It has a yellowish-golden color and a much higher smoke point than regular butter. Clarified butter typically contains about 120 calories per 2 tablespoons. 

<h3>Whipped Butter</h3>

Whipped butter is butter that has been whipped with air to make it lighter and fluffier. It has a pale yellow color and a light, creamy texture. Whipped butter typically contains about 102 calories per 2 tablespoons.
