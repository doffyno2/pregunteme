<h2>What Is a Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)?</h2>

A urinary tract infection (UTI) is an infection of the urinary tract, which includes the bladder, kidneys, and ureters. UTIs are caused by bacteria entering the urinary tract through the urethra. The bacteria can cause inflammation and irritation, leading to symptoms such as frequent urination, pain or burning during urination, bloody or cloudy urine, and strong-smelling urine. Left untreated, UTIs can cause more serious problems such as bladder or kidney infections, or even sepsis.

<h2>Can Cranberry Juice Help Treat UTIs in Dogs?</h2>

Cranberry juice has been used to treat UTIs in humans for many years, and it is becoming increasingly popular as a natural remedy for UTIs in dogs. Cranberry juice is thought to work by acidifying the urine and preventing bacteria from adhering to the walls of the bladder. This can help prevent UTIs from occurring and can also help the body to flush out bacteria that has already entered the bladder.

<h2>How Much Cranberry Juice Should I Give My Dog?</h2>

The amount of cranberry juice you should give your dog will depend on their size, age, and overall health. Generally speaking, it is recommended to give your dog one teaspoon of cranberry juice per 10 pounds of body weight, up to a maximum of one tablespoon per day. It is best to give your dog diluted cranberry juice, as undiluted juice can be too acidic for some dogs. It is also important to note that cranberry juice is not a substitute for veterinary care. If your dog is showing symptoms of a UTI, it is important to take them to the vet for diagnosis and treatment.
