To make the sport safer, bull riders use a special drop barrel. This barrel is designed to give the rider a soft landing if they are thrown off the bull. The barrel also allows the rider to practice their moves without the danger of a live bull. In this article, we’ll take a look at how to build a drop barrel for bull riding.</p>

<h2>Gather the Materials</h2>

<p>The first thing you’ll need to do is gather all the materials you’ll need to construct the drop barrel. You’ll need four 8-foot 2x6s, two 8-foot 2x4s, five 4-foot 2x4s, two 8-foot 4x4s, one 4-foot 4x4, five 4-foot 1x6s, four 4-foot 1x4s, screws, a drill, saw, and a jigsaw. You’ll also need a piece of plywood measuring 8x4 feet and at least one sheet of foam padding.</p>

<h2>Cut the Boards</h2>

<p>Once you have all the materials, it’s time to start building. Start by cutting the boards to size. You’ll need to cut the 4x4s and 2x4s into four 4-foot lengths, and the 2x6s into eight 4-foot lengths. You’ll also need to cut the 1x6s and 1x4s into eight 4-foot lengths.</p>

<h2>Build the Frame</h2>

<p>Once the boards are cut, it’s time to start constructing the frame. Start by attaching two of the 8-foot 2x6s to the 8-foot 4x4s with screws to create the sides of the frame. Make sure the 2x6s and 4x4s are flush against each other. Once the sides are attached, attach the 8-foot 2x4s to the 4x4s on top of the 2x6s to create the top of the frame. Finally, attach the 4-foot 4x4s to the sides of the frame.</p>

<h2>Attach the Plywood</h2>

<p>Now that you have the frame built, it’s time to attach the plywood. Start by attaching the plywood to the top of the frame with screws. Make sure the plywood is flush against the frame and that there are no gaps between the plywood and the frame. Once the plywood is attached, turn the frame over and attach the plywood to the bottom of the frame.</p>

<h2>Secure the Foam Padding</h2>

<p>The next step is to secure the foam padding to the frame. Start by cutting the foam padding to size. You’ll need to have a piece of foam that is 8x4 feet. Once the foam is cut, lay it on top of the plywood and secure it with screws. Make sure the foam is flush against the plywood.</p>

<h2>Attach the Sides</h2>

<p>The final step is to attach the sides of the frame to the top and bottom of the frame. Start by attaching the four 4-foot 2x4s to the top and bottom of the frame.
