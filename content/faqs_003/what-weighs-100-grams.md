Almost any object can be weighed in terms of grams. It is a unit of mass and is equal to one thousandth of a kilogram. Grams are often used to measure small objects and items that may be too small to measure in other units of weight. So, what exactly weighs 100 grams?

<h2>What is a Gram?</h2>

A gram is a unit of mass in the metric system. It is the basic unit of mass in the International System of Units, and the most commonly used unit of mass in the world. A gram is defined as one thousandth of a kilogram, and is equal to the mass of 1 milliliter of water at 4 degrees Celsius.

<h2>What Weighs 100 Grams?</h2>

There are many items that weigh approximately 100 grams, including:

<h3>Coins</h3>

A collection of coins can weigh 100 grams. This depends on the size, composition, and number of coins. For example, a collection of 20 Canadian loonies, each weighing 5 grams, would weigh 100 grams.

<h3>Foods</h3>

Many types of food weigh approximately 100 grams. This includes bread, vegetables, fruits, and nuts. For example, a medium-sized apple typically weighs around 100 grams.

<h3>Household Items</h3>

Many household items can weigh 100 grams. This includes items such as paper clips, batteries, pens, and paper. For example, a standard alkaline AA battery typically weighs around 20 grams, and five of them would weigh 100 grams.

<h3>Other Items</h3>
