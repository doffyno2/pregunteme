<h2>What You’ll Need</h2>

<p>Before you start your fire, you’ll need to make sure you have the right materials. You’ll need tinder, which is any material that will catch and hold a spark; kindling, which is wood that is small enough to ignite easily; and larger pieces of wood that will sustain the fire. You’ll also need rocks that are flat on one side and curved on the other, and a piece of string or leather.</p>

<h2>Gathering Your Materials</h2>

<p>The best tinder for a fire is dry and lightweight. You can use anything from dry grass to bark chips to newspaper. The tinder should be able to catch a spark and hold it. The kindling should be small pieces of wood that will ignite easily. It can be pine needles, twigs, and small sticks. Once the kindling is burning, you’ll need larger pieces of wood to sustain the fire. The wood should be dry and as large as you can easily handle. <h2>Preparing the Fire</h2>

<p>The first step in starting a fire is to create a platform for the fire. Gather a few stones and create a circle that is about two feet in diameter. This will help contain the fire and keep it from spreading. Once you’ve created the platform, you’ll need to gather your tinder, kindling, and wood and arrange it in a pile. Start with the tinder in the center, followed by the kindling, and then the larger pieces of wood. Make sure that there is plenty of air circulation around the pile so that the fire will be able to breathe.</p>

<h2>Creating a Spark</h2>

<p>Once you’ve prepared your materials, you’re ready to create a spark. You can use a number of techniques to create a spark. The most common is to use a bow drill. To do this, you’ll need to loop the string or leather around the rocks and then create a back-and-forth motion with the rocks. This will create a spark that will ignite the tinder. You can also use a flint and steel, which is a method of striking the flint against the steel to create sparks.</p>

<h2>Igniting the Fire</h2>

<p>Once you’ve created a spark, you’ll need to ignite the tinder. Gently blow on the tinder to help it catch the spark. Once it has ignited, the kindling will catch the flame and the fire will start to burn. You can then add more wood to the fire, as needed. Make sure to keep adding wood to the fire, so that it doesn’t go out.</p>

<h2>Maintaining the Fire</h2>

<p>Once your fire is burning, you’ll need to maintain it in order to keep it going. Make sure to keep adding wood to the fire, as needed. You can also move the wood around in the fire to keep the air flowing and ensure that the fire is burning evenly. If the fire starts to die down, you can blow on it to help it catch again.</p>

<h2>Putting Out the Fire</h2>

<p>Once you’re done with your fire, you’ll need to put it out. Make sure to douse the fire with water, and stir up the ashes with a stick to make sure that no embers are left burning. Once the fire is completely extinguished, you can spread the ashes around so that they don’t blow away and start another fire.</p>
