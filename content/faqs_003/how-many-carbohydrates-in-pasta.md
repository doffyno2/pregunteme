<h2>What Are Carbohydrates?</h2>

Carbohydrates are one of the three macronutrients, along with protein and fat. They are the body’s primary source of energy and can be found in a variety of foods, such as grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes, and dairy products.

Carbohydrates are composed of three components: glucose, fructose, and galactose. Glucose is the most readily available form of energy for the body and is broken down into individual units of energy. Fructose and galactose are converted into glucose before they can be used for energy.

<h2>Types of Carbohydrates</h2>

Carbohydrates can be divided into two main categories: simple carbohydrates and complex carbohydrates. Simple carbohydrates are found in foods such as sugar, honey, and fruit. These are quickly digested and can cause a spike in blood sugar levels. Complex carbohydrates, on the other hand, are found in grains, beans, and starchy vegetables. They take longer to digest and can provide sustained energy.
