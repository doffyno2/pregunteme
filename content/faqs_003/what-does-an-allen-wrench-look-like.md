An Allen wrench, also known as a hex key, is a tool used for tightening or loosening screws or bolts with a hexagonal shape. It is named after its inventor, William Allen, who patented the first Allen wrench in 1943. The Allen wrench is a versatile tool that can be used for a variety of tasks, such as tightening or loosening nuts and bolts, adjusting furniture, and even assembling bicycles. It's a must-have tool for any home or auto mechanic.

<h2>What Does an Allen Wrench Look Like?</h2>

An Allen wrench typically has a long handle and a short shank with a hexagonal shape at one end. The hexagonal shape is designed to fit into the hexagonal-shaped holes in screws and nuts. The short shank allows the user to apply adequate pressure to the screw or nut to tighten or loosen it. The handle of the Allen wrench is usually made of plastic or metal, and is either straight or angled. Some Allen wrenches have a ballpoint tip which helps to reduce the effort required to tighten or loosen screws or nuts.

<h2>What Sizes Do Allen Wrenches Come In?</h2>

Allen wrenches come in a variety of sizes, and the size of the wrench you need will depend on the size of the hexagonal hole in the screw or nut. The most common sizes are 1/8", 5/32", 3/16", 7/32", 1/4", 5/16", 3/8", and 7/16". Some common metric sizes are 2mm, 2.5mm, 3mm, 4mm, 5mm, and 6mm. 

<h2>How Are Allen Wrenches Used?</h2>

Allen wrenches can be used to tighten or loosen screws and nuts with a hexagonal shape. To use the Allen wrench, insert the short shank of the wrench into the hexagonal hole in the screw or nut. Apply pressure to the handle of the wrench to tighten or loosen the screw or nut. Make sure to apply the pressure in the direction of the arrow on the wrench.

<h2>What Tools Can Be Used With an Allen Wrench?</h2>

Allen wrenches can be used with a variety of tools, such as a ratchet, wrench, or socket. The Allen wrench can also be used with an adjustable wrench or pliers to provide extra leverage when tightening or loosening screws or nuts.

<h2>Are Allen Wrenches Reversible?</h2>

Yes, Allen wrenches are reversible. The short shank of the wrench has two sides, which can be used to tighten or loosen screws and nuts. The side with the arrow should be used to tighten the screw or nut, and the side without the arrow should be used to loosen the screw or nut.
