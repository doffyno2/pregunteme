With Google Maps, runners can plan their runs in advance, track their progress while running, and even compare and share their runs with friends.

<h2>What is Google Maps?</h2>

Google Maps is a web-based mapping service developed by Google. It offers satellite imagery, street maps, 360° panoramic views of streets, real-time traffic conditions, and route planning for traveling by car, foot, bicycle, or public transportation. Google Maps can display maps in three dimensions, as well as Street View, which provides 360° views of streets and buildings. Google Maps also has a feature that allows runners to track and map out their runs.

<h2>How to Map a Run on Google Maps</h2>

Mapping a run on Google Maps is simple and easy. All you need is a smartphone or device with Google Maps installed, and an internet connection. Here are the steps to map a run:

<h3>1. Open the Google Maps App</h3>

Open the Google Maps app on your device and make sure you’re logged in to your Google account.

<h3>2. Tap the “Search” bar</h3>

Tap the “Search” bar at the top of the screen.

<h3>3. Enter your starting point</h3>

Enter the address or location of your starting point.

<h3>4. Tap the “Directions” button</h3>

Tap the “Directions” button at the bottom of the screen.

<h3>5. Select “Running”</h3>

Select “Running” from the list of transportation options.

<h3>6. Enter your destination</h3>

Enter the address or location of your destination.

<h3>7. Tap the “Start” button</h3>

Tap the “Start” button at the bottom of the screen.

<h3>8. Begin your run</h3>

Once you’ve tapped the “Start” button, your run will begin and Google Maps will track your progress.
