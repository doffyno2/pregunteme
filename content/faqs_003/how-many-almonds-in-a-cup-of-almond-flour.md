Almond flour is ground up almonds, usually blanched and peeled. It’s a great alternative to wheat flour, which is higher in carbohydrates and gluten. <h2>How Many Almonds Are in a Cup of Almond Flour?</h2>

The answer to this question is not as straightforward as it may seem. The exact amount of almonds in a cup of almond flour will depend on the coarseness of the flour. Generally speaking, a cup of almond flour will contain about 100 grams of almonds, which is about 1/4 of a cup of whole almonds. 

<h2>What is the Difference Between Almond Flour and Almond Meal?</h2>

Almond flour and almond meal are often confused for one another, but they are actually two different products. Almond flour is made from blanched almonds that have been ground into a fine powder. Almond meal, on the other hand, is made from almonds that have not been blanched and therefore retain their skins. Almond meal is coarser than almond flour and contains more calories and fat.
