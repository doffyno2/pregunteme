Gender is another factor that can affect a toddler’s height. Boys tend to be taller than girls, and this difference is more pronounced during the toddler years. The exact difference in height between boys and girls varies but is generally around two inches.

Genetic background also plays a role in a toddler’s height. Children with parents who are tall are more likely to be taller than those with shorter parents. This is because genetic factors play a role in determining a person’s height.
