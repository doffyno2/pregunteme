<h2>How Long is 400m?</h2>

400 meters is equal to 437.445 yards, or 0.4 kilometers. It is a quarter-mile distance, and is often used as a training distance for middle-distance runners. 400m is one lap around a standard outdoor track. It is approximately the same length as four football fields, or the length of four city blocks.

<h2>How Long Does It Take to Run 400m?</h2>

The time it takes to run 400m depends on the speed of the runner. A professional sprinter can complete a 400m race in under 45 seconds, while a recreational runner may take up to two minutes to run the same distance. The world record for men is 43.03 seconds, while the world record for women is 47.60 seconds.

<h2>What Pace Should You Run 400m?</h2>

The pace you should aim for when running 400 meters depends on your goals. If you are training for a race, it is important to practice running at race pace. If you are running to improve your fitness, then aim to run at a comfortable pace that you can sustain throughout the entire distance.

<h2>How Do You Train for 400m?</h2>
