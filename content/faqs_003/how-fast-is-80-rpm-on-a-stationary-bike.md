<h2>What is RPM on a Stationary Bike?</h2>

RPM stands for "revolutions per minute." It is the number of times the flywheel of a bike turns in a minute. The flywheel is the part of a bike that is connected to the pedals and is responsible for providing the resistance necessary to simulate riding a bike outdoors. The flywheel is connected to a motor, which controls the resistance and the speed of the bike.

The RPM of a bike is usually displayed on the console of the bike, which also displays other information such as speed, distance, calories burned, and more. It is important to note that the RPM displayed on the console is the maximum RPM that the bike can reach. The actual RPM of the bike will depend on the speed at which the user pedals.

<h2>What is a Good RPM for a Stationary Bike?</h2>

The ideal RPM for a stationary bike depends on the user's fitness level and goals. Beginners should aim for a low RPM, as they are still getting used to the motion of the bike and may not be able to sustain a high RPM for a long period of time. For more experienced cyclists, a higher RPM should be aimed for, as this will allow them to get the most out of their workout.

On average, a good RPM for a stationary bike is between 60 and 80 RPM. This range of RPM ensures that the user is getting a good workout without putting too much strain on their body. It is important to remember that the RPM should be adjusted based on the user's fitness level and goals.
