<h2>Using Incense with Dogs</h2>

Most people would agree that dogs are family members, just like humans. As such, it’s important to consider their well-being when considering the use of incense. While many incense scents can be used safely with dogs, there are some that should be avoided.

<h3>What Incense is Safe for Dogs?</h3>

When choosing incense to use with your dog, it’s important to select an option that is all natural and contains no chemicals or toxins. Natural incense scents that are safe for dogs include lavender, chamomile, sandalwood, jasmine, and vanilla. These scents are not only safe for your pup, but also have calming properties that may help reduce stress and anxiety. 

<h3>What Incense is Unsafe for Dogs?</h3> 

In general, it’s best to avoid scents that contain synthetic ingredients or strong essential oils, as these can be toxic to animals. Some of the most popular scents to avoid when burning incense around your dog include cinnamon, pine, citrus, and peppermint. Additionally, it’s important to note that strong incense smoke can cause respiratory irritation in both dogs and humans, so it’s best to keep the room well-ventilated when burning incense.

<h3>Keep the Room Well-Ventilated</h3>

<h3>Start Slow</h3>

When introducing incense to your dog, it’s important to start slow. Try burning a small amount of incense in a well-ventilated room and observe your pup’s reaction. If your pup seems to be having a positive reaction, you can slowly increase the amount of incense that you burn.
