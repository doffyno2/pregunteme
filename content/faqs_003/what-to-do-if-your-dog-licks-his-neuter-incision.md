Having a dog neutered is a great way to help them live a more comfortable, healthier life. It can also help reduce unwanted behaviors and save you money in the long run. Unfortunately, though, there is the risk of your pup licking their neuter incision. This can be especially problematic if the incision becomes infected, leading to a lot of discomfort and potentially serious health risks.

The good news is that you can take steps to help prevent your dog from licking their neuter incision. In this article, we’ll explore what you can do to stop your pup from licking their neuter incision and how to handle the situation should your pup start to lick it. 

<h2>Why Is My Dog Licking His Neuter Incision?</h2>

It’s natural for dogs to lick their wounds, and this includes licking the incision from their neuter surgery. Dogs may lick the incision for a variety of reasons, such as to clean the wound, soothe the area, or simply out of boredom.

Unfortunately, if your pup starts licking the neuter incision, it can lead to a number of problems. Firstly, it can slow down the healing process and introduce bacteria into the wound. This can increase the risk of infection and may even require additional veterinary care.

<h2>How Can I Stop My Dog From Licking His Neuter Incision?</h2>

If you want to stop your pup from licking their neuter incision, there are a few things you can do.

<h3>1. Use an Elizabethan Collar</h3>

Also known as an “E-collar” or “lampshade,” an Elizabethan collar is a plastic or foam cone-shaped accessory that prevents your pup from licking or biting their incision. It fits around your pup’s neck and is designed to be comfortable and lightweight.

<h3>2. Use an Anti-Lick Bandage</h3>

An anti-lick bandage is a specialized product that goes over your pup’s incision. It’s designed to be bitter-tasting, which helps discourage your pup from licking the area. These bandages are typically made from a breathable material, so your pup won’t overheat.

<h3>3. Distract Your Dog</h3>

Another way to stop your pup from licking their neuter incision is to distract them with toys and activities. You can also offer your pup treats or play games to help keep their mind off of licking the area.

<h2>What Are the Signs of an Infected Neuter Incision?</h2>

If your pup has been licking their neuter incision, it’s important to keep an eye out for signs of infection. These can include redness, swelling, discharge, or a foul odor. If you notice any of these signs, it’s important to contact your veterinarian right away.
