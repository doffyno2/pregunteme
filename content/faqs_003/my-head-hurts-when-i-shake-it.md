My Head Hurts When I Shake It: Causes, Treatments, and Prevention



<p>If you experience a headache when you shake your head, it could be a sign of a serious medical condition. While not all causes of a head-shaking headache are serious, it is important to be aware of potential causes and take steps to prevent the condition. In this article, we will discuss the possible causes, treatments, and prevention of a head-shaking headache.</p>

<h2>What Causes a Head-Shaking Headache?</h2>

<p>There are a few potential causes of a head-shaking headache. The most common cause is inflammation of the blood vessels in the head, a condition known as vertebrobasilar insufficiency. This is caused by narrowing of the blood vessels that supply blood to the brain, which can occur due to a variety of conditions, including hypertension, diabetes, and atherosclerosis. Other causes of a head-shaking headache include:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Injury or trauma to the head or neck</li>
  <li>Stress</li>
  <li>Dehydration</li>
  <li>Low blood sugar</li>
  <li>Drug or alcohol use</li>
  <li>Certain medications</li>
</ul>

<h2>What Are the Symptoms of a Head-Shaking Headache?</h2>

<p>The most common symptom of a head-shaking headache is a sharp, throbbing pain that is localized to one area of the head. Other symptoms may include nausea and vomiting, sensitivity to light, dizziness, and blurred vision. If you experience any of these symptoms, it is important to seek medical attention as soon as possible.</p>

<h2>How Is a Head-Shaking Headache Diagnosed?</h2>

<p>In order to diagnose a head-shaking headache, your doctor will likely perform a physical examination and take a medical history. They may also order imaging tests, such as a CT scan or MRI, to rule out any structural abnormalities in the brain or neck. Blood tests may also be ordered to check for any underlying medical conditions.</p>

<h2>What Are the Treatments for a Head-Shaking Headache?</h2>

<p>The treatment for a head-shaking headache will depend on the underlying cause. For example, if the headache is due to a medical condition, such as vertebrobasilar insufficiency, your doctor may prescribe medications to reduce inflammation and improve blood flow. If the headache is due to injury or trauma, your doctor may recommend physical therapy. If the headache is due to stress, your doctor may recommend relaxation techniques or cognitive behavioral therapy.</p>
