<h2>What is the Difference Between a Standard Wall Outlet and a GFCI Outlet?</h2>

<p>A standard wall outlet is a device that provides electricity to an appliance or device. A GFCI outlet, on the other hand, is an outlet that is designed to shut off the electricity in the event of an electrical overload. GFCI outlets are typically found in bathrooms, kitchens, and other locations where there is a risk of electrical shock.</p>

<p>GFCI outlets are designed to detect when there is an electrical overload and to shut off the electricity to prevent an electrical shock. The GFCI outlet monitors the current flowing through the outlet and will shut off the power if the current exceeds a predetermined level. GFCI outlets are typically rated for 15 amps or less, and the voltage is usually the same as a standard wall outlet.</p>

<p>It is important to note that GFCI outlets are not designed to provide additional power. GFCI outlets are designed to provide protection from electrical overloads. For this reason, GFCI outlets should not be used to power appliances or devices that require more than 15 amps of power.</p>
