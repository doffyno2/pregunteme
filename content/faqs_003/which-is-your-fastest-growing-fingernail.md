<h2>How Fast Do Fingernails Grow?</h2>

<p>On average, fingernails grow about 3.5 millimeters per month. This growth rate can vary depending on a number of factors, including age, gender, and overall health. For instance, fingernails tend to grow faster in children and teenagers, while they may grow slower in adults. Similarly, certain health conditions and medications can impact nail growth.</p>

<h2>Which Fingernail Grows Fastest?</h2>

<p>Although all of your fingernails may appear to be the same length, the nail on your dominant hand (usually your right hand if you’re right-handed) typically grows faster than the nails on your other hand. This is because the dominant hand is used more often, and this increased use helps stimulate the nail growth.</p>

<p>In addition, the thumbnail—the nail on your thumb—often grows the fastest. This is because your thumb works harder than your other fingers. So, the increased pressure from use helps stimulate the nail growth.</p>

<p>If you want to maximize nail growth, there are a few steps you can take. <p>It’s also important to avoid activities that can damage your nails, such as biting your nails or using harsh chemicals. Additionally, using nail hardeners or nail strengtheners can help protect your nails from damage and maximize growth.</p>
