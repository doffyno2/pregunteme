<p>Do you hear a high-pitched noise coming from your shower that is driving you crazy? It’s likely caused by a phenomenon known as ‘water hammer’. Water hammer occurs when water is forced through a pipe too quickly, causing the pipe to vibrate. This vibration is what creates the high-pitched noise that is so irritating. Fortunately, there are a few simple solutions that you can try to stop the noise.</p>

<h2>What is Water Hammer?</h2>

<p>Water hammer is a common plumbing issue that occurs when water is forced through a pipe too quickly. As the water rushes through the pipe, it is compressed and creates a pressure wave that travels through the pipe. This pressure wave then causes the pipe to vibrate, which produces the high-pitched sound you are hearing from your shower. The sound can be quite loud and annoying, but fortunately, it is fairly easy to fix.</p>

<h2>What Causes Water Hammer?</h2>

<p>There are several factors that can cause water hammer. The most common is a problem with the water pressure. If the water pressure is too high, it can cause the water to rush through the pipes too quickly and create the pressure wave that leads to water hammer. Other causes can include a faulty valve or a leaky faucet. If you suspect that your water pressure is too high, it’s best to contact a plumber to have it checked.</p>
