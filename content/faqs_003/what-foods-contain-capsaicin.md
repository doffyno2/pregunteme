<h2>What is Capsaicin?</h2>

Capsaicin is a naturally occurring compound found in chili peppers. It is an alkaloid, which means it is an organic compound containing nitrogen that has a bitter taste. Capsaicin is what gives chili peppers their pungent and spicy flavor. 

In addition to being an important component of chili peppers, capsaicin is also used for medicinal purposes. It has antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic properties. Additionally, it has been known to help with weight loss and digestion, as well as reduce the risk of certain diseases, such as cancer.
