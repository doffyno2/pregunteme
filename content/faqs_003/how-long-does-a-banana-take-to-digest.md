<p>The digestive process starts in the mouth and ends in the large intestine. In the mouth, saliva begins to break down the banana, and it is further broken down by the stomach acid. <p>The type of banana can affect the digestion time. For example, ripe bananas are softer and easier to digest, while unripe bananas are difficult to digest and can take longer to digest.
