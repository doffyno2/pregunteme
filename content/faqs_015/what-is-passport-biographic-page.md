Passport biographic page refers to the page in a passport that contains the personal information of the passport holder. The information on the biographic page includes the holder's full name, date of birth, place of birth, nationality, and gender. Additionally, a passport biographic page also contains the passport number, issuing authority, and the date of issue and expiration. The biographic page is usually located towards the back of the passport and includes a photo of the passport holder.

<h2>What Information is on the Passport Biographic Page?</h2>

The biographic page of a passport typically includes the following information:

<h3>Full Name</h3>

The full name of the passport holder is listed on the biographic page of the passport. This includes the holder's first, middle, and last name. Depending on the country of origin, different naming conventions are followed. For example, in some countries the last name is listed first and the first name is listed last. 

<h3>Date of Birth</h3>

The date of birth of the passport holder is usually included on the biographic page. This can take the form of a full date (day, month, and year) or just a year. 

<h3>Place of Birth</h3>

The place of birth of the passport holder is also listed on the biographic page. This can include the city, state, or country of birth. 

<h3>Nationality</h3>

The nationality of the passport holder is listed on the biographic page. This is usually the same as the country of origin, but in some cases, it may differ. 

<h3>Gender</h3>

The gender of the passport holder is also listed on the biographic page. This is usually listed as male or female, but in some countries, a third gender may be listed. 

<h3>Passport Number</h3>

The passport number is a unique identification number assigned to each passport holder. This number is usually listed on the biographic page. 

<h3>Issuing Authority</h3>

The issuing authority is the government agency or organization responsible for issuing the passport. This information is typically included on the biographic page. 

<h3>Date of Issue and Expiration</h3>

The date of issue and expiration of the passport are also printed on the biographic page. This includes the date that the passport was issued and the date that it will expire. 

<h2>What is the Purpose of the Passport Biographic Page?</h2>

The passport biographic page serves several purposes. First, it provides the government with information about the passport holder. This can help with verifying the identity of the passport holder when they are traveling or applying for visas. Additionally, the biographic page can be used to help prevent passport fraud. By verifying the information on the biographic page, the government can make sure that the passport is legitimate.

The passport biographic page is usually protected by a layer of laminate. This helps to protect the information from being tampered with or damaged. Additionally, some passports also include a RFID chip that is used to store the information electronically. This provides an additional layer of security.
