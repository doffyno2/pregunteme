<h2>What Causes Pregnant Dogs to Shiver?</h2>

<p>There are several reasons why a pregnant dog may shiver more than usual. Hormone fluctuations that occur during pregnancy can cause a dog to shiver due to the increased levels of progesterone, estrogen, and other hormones. Additionally, pregnant dogs may shiver due to the physical discomfort they experience, such as cramps, bloating, and an enlarged abdomen. Pregnant dogs may also shiver due to the psychological stress of pregnancy, such as the fear of labor and delivery.</p>

<h2>When Do Pregnant Dogs Shiver?</h2>

<p>Pregnant dogs may shiver more often than usual throughout their pregnancy. This is most common during the last trimester of pregnancy, when hormone levels are at their highest and the physical discomfort is more intense. It is also common for pregnant dogs to shiver around the time of labor and delivery, due to the fear and anxiety associated with this process. Some dogs may even shiver after giving birth, due to the sudden drop in hormones and the stress of raising a litter.</p>

<p>If your dog is shivering more than usual during their pregnancy, it is important to take note of the frequency and duration of the shivering.
