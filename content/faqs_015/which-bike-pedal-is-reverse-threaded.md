<h2>What Is Reverse Threading on Bike Pedals?</h2>

Reverse threading on bike pedals is a threading pattern that is opposite of the traditional threading pattern. The traditional threading pattern is clockwise, while the reverse threading pattern is counterclockwise. This is an important distinction, as it affects how the pedal is attached to the crank. 

Reverse threading was invented to improve the safety and security of the pedal on the crank. By reversing the threading pattern, the pedal is more securely attached and more difficult to accidentally unscrew. This makes it safer for cyclists, as the pedal cannot come loose while riding.
