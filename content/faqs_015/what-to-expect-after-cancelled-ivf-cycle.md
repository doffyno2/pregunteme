<h2>Understanding the Cancellation Process</h2>

When a cycle is cancelled, there are several steps that need to be taken to ensure that the patient is safe and healthy. The first step is to determine why the cycle was cancelled. This will involve a discussion between the patient and the doctor to discuss the risks and complications associated with the cycle. If a medical condition or complication is identified, the patient will need to be monitored closely and any necessary treatments will be discussed.

The next step is to discuss the options available to the patient. This may include the option of continuing with the cycle, or to wait and try again in the future. Depending on the situation, the doctor may also suggest that the patient consider an alternative treatment option, such as adoption or using donor eggs.

<h2>What to Expect Physically After a Cancelled IVF Cycle</h2>

Once the cycle is cancelled, it is important to understand the physical effects that may be experienced. For some patients, the physical side effects can be quite severe. These may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and headaches. Other physical effects may include fatigue, soreness, and abdominal cramping.

The most common side effect of a cancelled IVF cycle is the development of ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS). This is a condition that occurs when the ovaries become overstimulated, leading to a build-up of fluid in the abdomen, chest, and sometimes, even the lungs. If this occurs, it is important to be monitored closely and to seek immediate medical attention if symptoms worsen.
