<h2>Jack Russell Terriers: An Overview</h2>

The Jack Russell terrier is a small, energetic breed of dog originally bred in England in the 1800s. Also known as the Parson Russell terrier, the Jack Russell is a working breed that was bred to hunt foxes and other small animals. Jack Russells have a white coat with black, brown, or tan markings, and their short, stubby legs give them a distinctive silhouette. They are generally friendly and social, but can also be quite stubborn and strong-willed. 

<h2>Characteristics of Jack Russell Terriers</h2>

Jack Russells are active and intelligent dogs that require plenty of exercise and mental stimulation. The breed is known for its independent streak, and requires consistent and clear training from an early age. 

<h2>Jack Russells as Family Pets</h2>

Jack Russells make excellent family pets, as long as their exercise and mental stimulation needs are met. They are generally good with children, although their rough-and-tumble play style means they may not be the best option for very young children. Jack Russells are also known for their strong prey drive and can sometimes be difficult to train, so they may not be the best option for first-time dog owners.
