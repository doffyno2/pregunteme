<h2>What Causes a Dog's Tongue to Turn White?</h2>

<p>The most common cause of a white tongue in dogs is a medical condition known as leukoplakia. This condition is caused by an overgrowth of cells on the surface of the tongue, which can lead to a white, smooth coating. Leukoplakia is most commonly seen in older dogs, but it can also occur in younger dogs who have had dental disease or trauma to the mouth.</p>

<p>Other causes of a white tongue in dogs include dietary deficiencies, which can lead to a lack of essential nutrients in the body. A lack of iron, calcium, and zinc can all lead to a white tongue in dogs. Additionally, certain medications, such as antibiotics, can also cause a white tongue in dogs.</p>

<h2>What Are the Symptoms of a White Tongue in Dogs?</h2>

<p>The most common symptom of a white tongue in dogs is a white coating on the tongue. This coating may be smooth or rough and can range in color from light pink to dark gray. Additionally, a white tongue in dogs may also be accompanied by other symptoms such as bad breath, excessive salivation, and difficulty eating or drinking.</p>

<h2>How Is a White Tongue in Dogs Diagnosed?</h2>

<p>If your dog has a white tongue, it is important to take them to the vet for a diagnosis. The vet will likely perform a physical examination and take a sample of your dog’s saliva or blood to determine the cause. In some cases, an x-ray or ultrasound may be necessary to rule out other possible causes.</p>

<p>The treatment for a white tongue in dogs depends on the underlying cause. If the cause is a dietary deficiency, your vet may recommend supplements to correct the deficiency.
