<h2>Why Does My Dog Keep Sniffing My Older Dog's Mouth?</h2>

Sniffing is a common behavior among dogs and it’s one of the ways they use to explore and gain more information about their environment. When a dog sniffs another dog’s mouth, it’s likely that they are trying to get a better understanding of the other dog. Dogs have an incredible sense of smell, and they can pick up on a wide variety of scents, including those associated with the other dog’s health, diet, and even mood. The information dogs can learn from sniffing another dog’s mouth can help them better understand the other dog and even help them make better decisions about how to interact with them.

<h2>What Does It Mean If My Dog Sniffs My Older Dog’s Mouth?</h2>

Sniffing is a natural behavior for dogs, and it’s usually nothing to be concerned about. If your dog is sniffing your older dog’s mouth, it could be a sign of curiosity and a desire to learn more about the other dog. If the sniffing persists, it could be a sign of dominance or attention-seeking behavior, so it’s important to keep an eye on the situation and make sure your older dog is comfortable.

<h2>How Do I Stop My Dog From Sniffing My Older Dog’s Mouth?</h2>

The best way to stop your dog from sniffing your older dog’s mouth is to redirect their attention to something else. Give your dog something else to focus on, such as a toy or a treat, and make sure to reward them for focusing on the new object. If the behavior persists, consider speaking with a dog behaviorist who can give you more personalized advice.
