<h2>What is a Cliff?</h2>

A cliff is a steep slope or drop of land, often composed of rock or soil, that connects two higher areas. Cliffs can be found in many places, including on coastlines, mountain ranges, and in valleys. Cliffs are usually formed by the erosion of waves or by the collapse of a large rock or earth formation.

<h2>Types of Cliffs</h2>

There are many different types of cliffs, each with its own unique characteristics. Some of the most common types of cliffs are:

<h3>Sea Cliffs</h3>

Sea cliffs are found along the coastlines of oceans and seas. These cliffs are usually created by the erosion of waves, which gradually erode away the edges of the land. Sea cliffs can be made of limestone, sandstone, shale, or other sedimentary rocks.

<h3>Mountain Cliffs</h3>

Mountain cliffs are found in mountain ranges and are usually created by the collapse of a large rock or earth formation. These cliffs are often made of granite and other igneous rocks, and can be quite steep.

<h3>Valley Cliffs</h3>

Valley cliffs are found in valleys and canyons. They are usually made of sedimentary rocks like sandstone and limestone. Valley cliffs can be quite steep and are often used for rock climbing.
