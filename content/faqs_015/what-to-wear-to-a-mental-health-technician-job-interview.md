Interviewing for a Mental Health Technician role can be an intimidating experience, especially if you’re unsure of what to wear. The right attire can help you exude confidence and make a great first impression. Read on to discover what to wear to a Mental Health Technician job interview. 

<h2>Research the Company Dress Code</h2>

Before you head to the interview, take some time to research the company dress code. This will give you an idea of what to expect and how to dress for the interview. 

Some companies may require a more formal attire, such as a suit and tie. Others may allow more casual dress. If the company dress code is not specified, you can opt for business casual. 

<h2>Choose the Right Clothing</h2>

If you’re not sure what to wear to a Mental Health Technician job interview, err on the side of caution and dress for the job you want. Choose clothing that is appropriate, neat, and clean. Avoid anything too flashy, revealing, or casual. 

<h3>Women</h3>

Women should stick to a skirt or pantsuit, along with a blouse or collared shirt. Make sure your skirt is an appropriate length. Avoid overly tight clothing, and opt for a pair of low-heeled shoes. 

<h3>Men</h3>

Men should stick to a suit and tie, or a collared shirt and slacks. Avoid jeans, t-shirts, and shorts. Opt for a pair of dress shoes rather than sneakers. 

<h2>Accessorize Carefully</h2>

Accessorizing may help you stand out, but choose your accessories carefully. Avoid wearing too many accessories, or anything that may be distracting. Stick to a conservative watch or bracelet, and a professional-looking belt. 

<h2>Prepare for the Interview</h2>

Once you’ve chosen the right clothing, it’s time to prepare for the interview. Make sure you arrive early, wear a smile, and give a firm handshake. Practice making eye contact and speaking clearly.
