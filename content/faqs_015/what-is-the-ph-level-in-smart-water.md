<h2>What is pH and Why Does it Matter?</h2>

pH stands for "potential of hydrogen," and it is a measure of how acidic or basic a solution is. The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14, with 7 being neutral. Solutions with a pH of less than 7 are considered acidic, and solutions with a pH of greater than 7 are considered basic. 

The pH of a given solution is important because it can affect the solubility of certain substances, as well as their reactivity. For example, in the human body, the pH of the blood needs to remain within a narrow range in order for the body to function properly.
