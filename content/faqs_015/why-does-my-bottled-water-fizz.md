<p>Water can naturally contain dissolved gases, including carbon dioxide (CO2). When these gases come into contact with air, they can form tiny bubbles. <p>Carbon dioxide dissolves very easily in water. This is because CO2 molecules are polar and can form electrostatic bonds with water molecules. This results in a very stable solution, where the CO2 molecules are evenly distributed throughout the water.</p> 

<p>The gas is usually present in water in equilibrium with the air. This means that the amount of CO2 in the water is constantly changing to match the amount in the air. This is why the amount of CO2 in bottled water can vary so much. If the water is exposed to air for a long time, more of the CO2 will bubble out and the water will become less fizzy.</p>
