<h2>What Are Fleas?</h2>

<p>Fleas are small, wingless parasitic insects that feed on the blood of mammals, birds, and even reptiles. They are small and dark, measuring only about 1.5mm in length, making them difficult to spot. Fleas can live for up to 100 days, which means that if left untreated, their numbers can quickly multiply.</p>

<h2>Flea Life Cycle</h2>

<p>Fleas go through four stages of development: egg, larva, pupa, and adult. The eggs are laid directly on the animal or in its environment, such as carpets and furniture. The eggs hatch into larvae after about two days, which then feed on organic material in the environment, such as dead skin cells, feces, and other debris. After about four days, the larvae spin a silken cocoon and enter the pupal stage. The pupal stage is when the flea is fully formed and ready to emerge as an adult. Adult fleas can live for up to 100 days and can lay up to 50 eggs per day.</p>

<h2>Risks of Fleas</h2>

<p>Fleas are not just a nuisance; they can also pose a threat to our pets’ health. As blood-sucking parasites, fleas can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and skin infection. Additionally, fleas can cause anemia in puppies, kittens, and other animals with weaker immune systems.</p>
