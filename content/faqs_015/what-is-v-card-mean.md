What is a vCard?


A vCard is an electronic business card, also known as a virtual business card. It is essentially a digital representation of a person's or business's contact information, including name, address, phone number, email address, and other contact information. It is a format developed by the Versit consortium, which was formed in 1995 by Apple, IBM, Novell, and Siemens. The vCard format was created to standardize contact information exchange, and it has been widely adopted by many companies and organizations.

vCards are commonly used to exchange contact information between individuals and businesses. They are also used in email signatures, on websites, and in social media profiles. They are a convenient way to share contact information in a standardized format.

<h2>How Does a vCard Work?</h2>

A vCard is a digital representation of a person's or business's contact information. It contains data fields that are formatted according to the Versit consortium standard. These data fields include name, address, phone number, email address, website URL, photo, and other contact information.

When a vCard is sent, the data fields are encoded into a text-based file format, typically with a .vcf extension. The person or business receiving the vCard can then import the file into their contact management software, such as an address book application. The contact information is then stored in their address book, and can be accessed whenever needed.
