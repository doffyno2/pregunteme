<h2>Check the Serial Number</h2>
<p>Many bicycles have a serial number stamped on the bottom bracket, which is the part of the frame that holds the crank arms in place. This serial number can usually be seen from the side of the bike when looking down at the frame. The serial number will usually start with a letter, followed by a series of numbers. The first letter of the serial number can tell you the year of the bike. For example, if the serial number starts with an "H", it could mean that the bike was made in 2019.</p>

<h2>Look at the Components</h2>
<p>The components on a bicycle can give you a good indication of its age. For example, many older bikes have components made of steel, while newer bikes have components made of aluminum or carbon fiber. Additionally, the design of the components can often help you to determine the year of the bike. For example, if the bike has a Shimano Deore XT groupset, it was likely made in the late 1990s or early 2000s.</p>
