<p>Have you ever experienced an involuntary shaking of your arm when you lean on it? If yes, then you're not alone. This phenomenon is known as "essential tremor" and it is a relatively common condition. It is a neurological disorder that affects the muscles and causes them to shake or tremble involuntarily. In this article, we will discuss what essential tremor is, what causes it, the symptoms associated with it, and how it can be treated.</p>

<h2>What is Essential Tremor?</h2>

<p>Essential tremor is a neurological disorder that affects the muscles. It causes them to tremble or shake involuntarily. The condition is usually identified by the trembling of the hands, arms, head, and sometimes the legs and feet. It is usually most noticeable when the person is attempting to do a task such as writing or holding a cup.</p>

<p>Essential tremor is a common condition and it affects people of all ages. It is more common in people over the age of 65, but it can affect children as well. It can be a very frustrating and embarrassing condition, as it can make it difficult to do everyday tasks.</p>

<h2>What Causes Essential Tremor?</h2>

<p>The exact cause of essential tremor is still unknown. It is believed to be related to the malfunctioning of certain parts of the brain that control muscle movement. It is also believed to be related to genetics, as it is often seen to run in families. Other factors that may contribute to essential tremor include certain medications, stress, caffeine, and alcohol.</p>

<h2>Symptoms of Essential Tremor</h2>

<p>The most common symptom of essential tremor is shaking or trembling of the hands, arms, head, and sometimes legs and feet. It usually occurs when the person is attempting to do a task such as writing or holding a cup. Other symptoms may include difficulty speaking, difficulty with fine motor tasks, and difficulty walking.</p>

<h2>How is Essential Tremor Diagnosed?</h2>

<p>Essential tremor is usually diagnosed through a physical examination and a medical history. Your doctor may also use tests such as an MRI or CT scan to look at the brain and rule out other conditions. In some cases, the doctor may also order a blood test to rule out other conditions.</p>

<h2>Treatment for Essential Tremor</h2>

<p>The main treatment for essential tremor is medication. There are several types of medications that can be used to treat the condition. These include beta blockers, anticonvulsants, and botulinum toxin injections. In some cases, surgery may be an option to reduce the severity of the tremor.</p>

<h2>Living With Essential Tremor</h2>

<p>Essential tremor can be a difficult condition to live with, but there are steps you can take to manage the condition. It is important to stay active and participate in regular physical activity. This can help to reduce the severity of the tremor. Additionally, avoiding caffeine and alcohol can also help to reduce the tremor.</p>

<p>It is also important to find ways to cope with the stress associated with the condition. This can include talking to a therapist or joining a support group. Finally, it is important to discuss all treatment options with your doctor to determine which one is right for you.</p>
