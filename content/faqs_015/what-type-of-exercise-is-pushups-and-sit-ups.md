<h2>What Muscles do Pushups and Situps Target?</h2>
<p>The primary muscles targeted by pushups and situps are the chest, triceps, and core muscles. Pushups primarily target the pectoral muscles, triceps, and anterior deltoids. Situps primarily target the rectus abdominis and the obliques. </p>

<h2>Why are Pushups and Situps Important?</h2>
<p>Pushups and situps are important exercises because they are simple, effective, and can be done anywhere. They require no equipment and can be done anywhere with minimal space. They are also excellent for improving cardiovascular health and for burning calories. </p>

<h2>How to Perform Pushups and Situps Properly</h2>
<p>To perform pushups and situps properly, it is important to have proper form. Proper form will ensure that the exercise is performed correctly and that the maximum benefit is achieved from the exercise. </p>

<h3>Pushups</h3>
<p>To perform the pushup correctly, start by lying on your stomach on the floor. Place your hands on the floor slightly wider than shoulder width apart. Push your body up off the floor, keeping your elbows close to your sides. Straighten your arms and hold your body in a straight line from your head to your toes. Lower your body back to the starting position. </p>

<h3>Situps</h3>
<p>To perform the situp correctly, start by lying on your back on the floor with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor. Place your hands behind your head and tuck your chin in towards your chest. Push your upper body up towards your knees. Hold for a few seconds, then lower your upper body back to the starting position. </p>
