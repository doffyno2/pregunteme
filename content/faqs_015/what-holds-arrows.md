What Holds Arrows Together, and How Does It Work? 



When shooting an arrow, the shooter has to ensure that the arrow is correctly put together, as the components need to be safely and securely attached to each other. The components that make up an arrow are shaft, fletching, nock, and point. Each of these components needs to be joined together in order for the arrow to fly correctly. This article will discuss what holds arrows together, and how it works.

<h2>What Are the Components of an Arrow?</h2>

An arrow is made up of four main components: the shaft, the fletching, the nock, and the point. The shaft is the main body of the arrow and is usually made of either wood or fiberglass. The fletching is the feathers or plastic vanes that are attached to the rear of the arrow and help stabilize it in flight. The nock is the groove or notch at the end of the arrow that allows it to be attached to the bowstring. The point is the sharp tip of the arrow that is responsible for penetrating the target.
