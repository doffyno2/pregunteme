<h2>What is BPA?</h2> 

<p>BPA, or bisphenol A, is an industrial chemical used in the production of certain plastics, such as polycarbonate plastics and epoxy resins. BPA is known to have an estrogen-like effect in the body, and can be found in some food and beverage containers, such as certain canned foods, plastic bottles, and water bottles. BPA can leach into food and beverages, and has been linked to a variety of health concerns, including cancer, reproductive issues, and behavioral problems.</p>

<h2>What Kind of Bottled Water is BPA Free?</h2> 

<p>The simple answer is that not all bottled water is BPA free. However, there are several types of bottled water that are BPA free. The most common type of BPA free bottled water is spring water. Spring water is collected from an underground source, and is not treated with any chemicals or other additives. Spring water is also tested for contaminants and is generally considered to be safe for consumption.</p> 

<p>Another type of BPA free bottled water is reverse osmosis water. Reverse osmosis is a process in which water is forced through a membrane, which removes toxins and impurities. Reverse osmosis water is often purified and free from many contaminants, and is often BPA free.</p> 

<p>The last type of bottled water that is BPA free is distilled water. Distillation is a process in which water is boiled and the steam is then condensed and collected. The resulting water is free from many contaminants, including BPA. Distilled water is often used in medical facilities, and is considered to be a safe option for drinking.</p>
