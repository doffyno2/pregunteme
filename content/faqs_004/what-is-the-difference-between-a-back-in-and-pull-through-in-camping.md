<h2>What is a Back In Campsite?</h2>

A back in campsite is a camping spot that requires you to back your vehicle into the spot. This type of camping spot is often found in more rural areas and is typically less expensive than a pull through spot. The advantage of a back in spot is that it often provides more privacy and is often more secluded than a pull through campsite.

<h2>What is a Pull Through Campsite?</h2>

A pull through campsite is a camping spot that allows you to drive straight through it without having to back your vehicle into the spot. This type of campsite is often found in more developed campgrounds, such as those found in state and national parks. These campsites are often more expensive than a back in spot, but they provide more convenience and often have more amenities.
