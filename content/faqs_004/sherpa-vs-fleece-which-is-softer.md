<h2>What is Sherpa?</h2>

<p>Sherpa is a 100% polyester fabric that was originally used as a lining for jackets, vests and other winter wear. The fabric was named after the people of the Himalayan region, who are also called Sherpas, because it was designed to mimic the warm and cozy feel of their traditional clothing. Today, Sherpa is commonly used to line the interior of jackets, hats and other outdoor apparel.</p>

<p>Sherpa is usually made from two different types of fibers. The first is a soft, smooth synthetic fiber that provides warmth and comfort. The second is a textured, looped fiber that traps air and helps to keep the wearer warm in cold weather.</p>

<h2>What is Fleece?</h2>

<p>Fleece is a synthetic fabric made from polyester fibers. It is typically used for outdoor apparel, such as jackets, hats and gloves, as well as for linings, blankets and other items. Fleece is a popular choice for outdoor wear because it is lightweight, breathable and warm.</p>

<p>Fleece is made by interlocking two or more fibers. This creates a fabric that is soft, lightweight and warm. Fleece can also be made from other materials, such as wool or cotton, but these fabrics tend to be much heavier and less comfortable.</p>

<p>Fleece is still a very soft fabric, but it pales in comparison to the softness of Sherpa. The fibers of fleece are much coarser than those of Sherpa, making it slightly less comfortable to wear. Additionally, fleece fabrics tend to pill and shed more than Sherpa fabrics, which can also make them less comfortable.</p>
