<p>TB, or tuberculosis, is a serious, highly contagious, and potentially fatal illness that affects the lungs. It is caused by a bacterial infection, and is one of the most common infectious diseases worldwide. As such, health care workers and those who may be exposed to TB, such as those working in healthcare, are routinely tested for the infection. But how long does a TB test last?</p>

<p>A TB test is usually administered through a two-step process. The first step is a skin test, also known as the tuberculin test, which is used to detect whether or not a person has been exposed to the TB bacteria. This test involves injecting a small amount of the TB bacteria into the skin, and then observing the test area for any reaction. If a reaction is observed, it is considered a positive result, indicating that the person has been exposed to the TB bacteria. The second step is a blood test, which is used to confirm whether or not the person has an active TB infection. This test is also referred to as the QuantiFERON-TB test, and is typically done in conjunction with the skin test.</p>

<h2>How Long Does a TB Test Take to Give Results?</h2>

<p>The results of a TB test can usually be obtained within 48 hours of the skin test being administered. The results of the blood test, however, can take up to two weeks to be returned. During this time, the person being tested should continue to practice good hygiene and social distancing, as the TB bacteria can be spread through close contact.</p>

<h2>What Should I Do If My TB Test is Positive?</h2>

<p>If the results of a TB test come back positive, it does not necessarily mean that the person has an active TB infection. It simply means that the person has been exposed to the TB bacteria at some point in their life. In order to confirm whether or not the person has an active TB infection, further testing and treatment may be necessary. If a person’s TB test results are positive, they should immediately contact their healthcare provider to discuss their treatment options.</p>

<h2>What Are the Risks of a False Positive TB Test?</h2>

<p>A false positive TB test result is when the test indicates that a person has an active TB infection, when in fact they do not. This can occur for a variety of reasons, including if the person has been recently vaccinated, or if they have been exposed to a different strain of the TB bacteria. It is important to note that a false positive TB test result does not necessarily mean that the person is infectious or at risk of developing an active TB infection.</p>

<h2>How Can I Reduce the Risk of False Positive TB Tests?</h2>
