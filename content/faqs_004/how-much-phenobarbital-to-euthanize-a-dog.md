<h2>What Is Phenobarbital?</h2>

Phenobarbital is a barbiturate drug used to control seizures in both humans and animals. It is also used as a sedative and to induce coma in patients who are undergoing surgery. In veterinary medicine, it is the most commonly used drug in the euthanasia process.

<h2>How Is Phenobarbital Used in Euthanasia?</h2>

This causes the pet to become deeply relaxed and sleepy. This is followed by an injection of an overdose of phenobarbital, which quickly induces a peaceful death.

<h2>What Is the Dosage of Phenobarbital in Euthanasia?</h2>

The dosage of phenobarbital used in euthanasia is dependent on the size of the animal. For dogs, the standard dosage is between 30 and 90 mg per pound of body weight. It is important to remember that the dosage should not exceed 90mg per pound of body weight in order to avoid any potential complications.
