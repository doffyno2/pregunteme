<h2>What is Navy Bike PRT?</h2>

The Navy Physical Readiness Test, or PRT, is a physical fitness test that all members of the United States Navy are required to take twice a year. The Navy Bike PRT is a specific version of the PRT that is conducted on a stationary bike. The Navy Bike PRT is designed to test the muscular endurance and cardiovascular fitness of Navy personnel.

<h2>What are the Requirements for Navy Bike PRT?</h2>

The Navy Bike PRT consists of a series of exercises that must be completed within a specified time limit. The exercises include a five-minute warmup, five minutes of cycling at a moderate pace, five minutes of cycling at a vigorous pace, and five minutes of cool down. The goal of the Navy Bike PRT is to complete as many revolutions as possible during the vigorous portion of the test.

<h2>How is Navy Bike PRT Score Calculated?</h2>

The Navy Bike PRT score is calculated by taking the total number of revolutions completed during the vigorous portion of the test and then subtracting the number of revolutions completed during the moderate portion of the test. The score is then divided by the total number of minutes spent on the bike. The final score is then multiplied by 100 to get the final Navy Bike PRT score.

<h2>What is a Good Navy Bike PRT Score?</h2>
