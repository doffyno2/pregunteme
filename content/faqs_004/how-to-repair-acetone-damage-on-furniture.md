<h2>What is Acetone?</h2>

Acetone is a colorless, flammable liquid with a characteristic odor. It is a powerful solvent that can dissolve and remove many materials, including paint, grease, and other substances. Acetone is often used as a solvent for cleaning purposes, as well as in the manufacture of paint and varnish.

<h2>How Does Acetone Damage Furniture?</h2>

Acetone is a strong solvent that can weaken or damage the finish of wood surfaces. It can also leave behind a residue that can discolor the wood. If acetone is not used properly, it can cause permanent damage to furniture.

<h2>How to Avoid Acetone Damage on Furniture</h2>

When using acetone on furniture, it is important to take the proper precautions to prevent damage. Before applying acetone, it is important to test it on an inconspicuous area of the furniture to make sure it will not damage the finish. It is also important to use a well-ventilated area, as acetone can be hazardous if inhaled in large amounts.

<h2>How to Repair Acetone Damage on Furniture</h2>
