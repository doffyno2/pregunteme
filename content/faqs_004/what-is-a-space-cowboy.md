Space cowboys are a type of astronaut who specializes in long-term space exploration. They are usually highly trained and experienced astronauts who have a deep understanding of space technology and the environment of outer space. Space cowboys are often associated with the early exploration of space, when much of space exploration was done by a few brave individuals who were willing to take on the risks of space exploration. 

Today, space cowboys are still around, but they are much more rare. They typically work on long-term space missions, such as those to Mars or other planets. Space cowboys are also frequently consulted for their expertise on space-related matters, such as spacecraft design, navigation, and mission planning.

The term "space cowboy" originated in the late 1950s and early 1960s, during the beginnings of the space race between the United States and the Soviet Union. It was used in reference to the brave and pioneering astronauts of the time, who were the first to take on the risks of long-term space exploration.

The term was popularized by the popular movie "The Right Stuff," which featured a group of astronauts who were considered to be space cowboys. The movie also popularized the term "right stuff," which is used to describe someone who is brave, courageous, and willing to take on risks.
