<h2>What Are Carbohydrates?</h2>

<p>Carbohydrates are one of the three macronutrients that provide the body with energy. They are found in foods such as bread, pasta, rice, potatoes, and other starchy foods, as well as in fruits, vegetables, and dairy products. Carbohydrates are broken down into glucose, which is then used by the body for energy. When the body does not have enough glucose, it will turn to other sources of energy, such as fat and protein.</p>

<h2>How Long Do Carbohydrates Stay In the Body?</h2>

<p>The answer to this question depends on the type of carbohydrate consumed. Simple carbohydrates, such as those found in sugary foods and drinks, are quickly broken down and absorbed into the bloodstream. This means that they are quickly used for energy and are no longer present in the body after a few hours. Complex carbohydrates, such as those found in whole grains, take longer to break down and can stay in the body for longer periods of time.</p>
