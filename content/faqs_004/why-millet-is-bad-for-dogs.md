<h2>What Type of Millet is Bad for Dogs?</h2>

The type of millet that is bad for dogs is the processed millet that is often used in commercial dog food. Processed millet has been processed to reduce its nutritional value and increase its shelf life. This type of millet can be difficult to digest and can cause digestive problems in dogs.

<h2>Why is Processed Millet Bad for Dogs?</h2>

Processed millet is bad for dogs because it has been processed to reduce its nutritional value. This type of millet is also high in carbohydrates, which can cause weight gain in dogs. Additionally, processed millet does not contain the same essential fatty acids and other nutrients that are found in unprocessed millet.
