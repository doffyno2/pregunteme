<h2>What Is American Wire Gauge (AWG)?</h2>

American Wire Gauge (AWG) is a standard that was developed in the United States in 1857. It is used to measure the diameter of electrical wires and cables. It is also used to refer to the thickness of the insulation that surrounds the wire. The higher the AWG number, the smaller the wire diameter and the thinner the insulation.
