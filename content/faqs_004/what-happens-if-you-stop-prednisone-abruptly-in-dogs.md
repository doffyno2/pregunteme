<h2>What is Prednisone?</h2>

Prednisone is a steroid that is used to reduce inflammation in the body. It is a synthetic version of cortisol, a hormone naturally produced by the adrenal glands. Prednisone can be used to reduce inflammation caused by many different conditions, including arthritis, allergies, asthma, and autoimmune diseases. <h2>What Are the Benefits of Taking Prednisone?</h2>

<h2>What Are the Risks of Taking Prednisone?</h2>

Although prednisone has many benefits, there are also some risks associated with taking prednisone. Prednisone can cause a variety of side effects, including increased appetite, weight gain, increased thirst, increased urination, increased panting, and increased risk of infection. Prednisone can also cause more serious side effects, such as an increased risk of developing an ulcer, an increased risk of developing osteoporosis, and an increased risk of developing diabetes. 

<h2>When Should Prednisone Be Used?</h2>

<h2>What Happens If You Stop Prednisone Abruptly in Dogs?</h2>

Stopping prednisone abruptly in dogs can have serious consequences. When prednisone is stopped suddenly, the body can no longer produce enough cortisol to keep up with the body's needs.
