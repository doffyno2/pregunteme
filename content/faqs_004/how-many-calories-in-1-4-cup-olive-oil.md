<h2>Types of Olive Oil</h2>

There are several different types of olive oil available, each with its own unique flavor and nutritional profile. The most common types of olive oil are extra-virgin, pure, and light.

<h3>Extra-Virgin Olive Oil</h3>

Extra-virgin olive oil is the most popular and widely available type of olive oil. It is made from the first pressing of the olives and has a strong, fruity flavor. It is also the most nutritious, with the highest levels of antioxidants and polyphenols. Extra-virgin olive oil is also the most expensive type of olive oil.

<h3>Pure Olive Oil</h3>

Pure olive oil is made from the second and third pressing of the olives. It is lighter in color and flavor than extra-virgin olive oil and has a milder taste. Pure olive oil is the most versatile type of olive oil and can be used for both cooking and baking.

<h3>Light Olive Oil</h3>

Light olive oil is a blend of extra-virgin and pure olive oil. It is lighter in color and flavor than pure or extra-virgin olive oil and is often used for cooking and baking. Light olive oil is not as nutritious as extra-virgin or pure olive oil, but it is still a healthier alternative to many other cooking oils.
