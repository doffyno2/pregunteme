<h2>What is Deposition?</h2>

Deposition is the process of water vapor condensing into a liquid or a solid in the atmosphere and then falling to Earth. It is the opposite of evaporation, which is the process of liquid water turning into a gas and then traveling back into the atmosphere. Deposition is a crucial part of the water cycle because it is the way that water is returned to the Earth's surface. 

<h2>How Does Deposition Work?</h2>

Deposition occurs when the temperature and humidity levels of the atmosphere reach a certain point. At this point, water vapor in the atmosphere condenses and falls to the ground as either rain or snow. The temperature and humidity levels at which this occurs is known as the dew point.

<h2>How Does Deposition Affect the Water Cycle?</h2>

<h2>What Are The Different Types of Deposition?</h2>

There are several different types of deposition. The two main types are precipitation and sublimation. Precipitation is the most common type of deposition and is the process of water vapor condensing into a liquid and then falling to the ground as rain or snow. Sublimation is the process of water vapor condensing directly into a solid and then falling to the ground as snow or ice.
