<h2>Time Zones in France</h2>

<p>France is divided into two time zones: Central European Time (CET) and Central European Summer Time (CEST). CET is used in winter, from the last Sunday in October to the last Sunday in March. This corresponds to GMT+1, and is the same as the UK and Ireland. CEST is used in summer, from the last Sunday in March to the last Sunday in October. This corresponds to GMT+2, the same as most of the rest of Europe.</p>

<p>In France, the clocks go forward one hour at 2am on the last Sunday in March, and back one hour at 3am on the last Sunday in October. This is known as 'Daylight Saving Time'. The time in France is usually displayed on digital clocks as 'CET' or 'CEST', depending on the time of year.</p>
