<h2>What Are the Benefits of Delta Preferred Seats?</h2>

Delta Preferred Seats offer passengers a number of benefits that are not available with other seating options. These include greater legroom, priority boarding, and access to pre-boarding lounges.

<h3>More Legroom</h3>

Delta Preferred Seats are some of the most spacious available on Delta flights. Depending on the aircraft, these seats offer up to five inches more legroom than standard economy seats. This additional legroom makes it easier to relax during the flight, allowing passengers to recline and stretch out.

<h3>Priority Boarding</h3>

Delta Preferred Seats also come with priority boarding. This means that passengers with these seats get to board the plane before other passengers. This can be especially helpful if you need to get settled quickly, or if you have carry-on items that need to be stowed away.

<h3>Access to Pre-Boarding Lounges</h3>

Finally, Delta Preferred Seats come with access to the airline's pre-boarding lounges. These lounges are typically located at the gate, and offer passengers a comfortable place to relax before their flight. The lounges usually offer refreshments and snacks, as well as a selection of newspapers and magazines.

Delta Preferred Seats come with a variety of benefits, but they also come with a higher cost. Depending on the flight, these seats can cost up to double the price of a standard economy seat. As a result, travelers should carefully consider whether or not the added benefits are worth the additional cost.
