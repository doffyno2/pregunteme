<p>In order to determine a good bowling score, it's important to first understand the scoring system of the game. In most forms of bowling, the goal is to knock down as many pins as possible with each turn. Depending on the type of game being played, each pin knocked down is worth a certain number of points, and the total score is determined by the total number of pins that are knocked down in a given frame. For example, in a standard ten-pin game, each pin knocked down is worth one point, and the total score is determined by the total number of pins knocked down in ten frames.</p>

<p>Now that you know the basics of the bowling scoring system, let's discuss what a good bowling score is. Generally speaking, a good bowling score is anything over 150 points. This means that if you knock down 15 pins in each frame, you will have achieved a good bowling score. Of course, this is just the minimum requirement for a good bowling score. For those who want to be competitive, the goal is usually to score higher than 200 points.
