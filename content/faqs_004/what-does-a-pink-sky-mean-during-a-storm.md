<h2>Why is the Sky Pink During a Storm?</h2>

The reason the sky turns pink during a storm is because of a phenomenon known as “scattering.” This occurs when sunlight passes through turbulent air, such as during a storm, and is scattered by the atmosphere. The light is broken up into its component parts, which are the colors of the rainbow.

The pink color is caused by the combination of red and violet. When light is scattered by the atmosphere, the shorter wavelengths (violet) are scattered more than the longer wavelengths (red). This is why the sky appears to be a pinkish color.

<h2>What Does Pink Sky Mean for the Weather?</h2>

Although a pink sky can be a beautiful sight, it can also be a sign of an impending storm. The pink sky is usually the result of a storm front, which is a line of thunderstorms that is moving in. The turbulence of the air behind the storm front is what causes the sky to turn pink.

The pink sky is usually accompanied by strong winds, lightning, and heavy rain. So if you see a pink sky during a storm, it is best to take cover and stay safe.
