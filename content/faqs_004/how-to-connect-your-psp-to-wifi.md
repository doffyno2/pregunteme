<h2>What You'll Need</h2>

<p>Before you set up your PSP to connect to Wi-Fi, you’ll need to make sure you have the correct equipment. You’ll need:</p>

<ul>
  <li>A PlayStation Portable (PSP)</li>
  <li>A Wi-Fi network</li>
  <li>A computer or mobile device that is connected to the same Wi-Fi network</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps to Connect Your PSP to Wi-Fi</h2>

<p>Once you have all the necessary equipment, you can start the process of connecting your PSP to Wi-Fi. To do this, follow these steps:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Turn on Your PSP</h3>

<p>The first step is to turn on your PSP. To do this, press the power button located on the top of the device. Once your PSP is on, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Locate the Network Settings</h3>

<p>The next step is to locate the network settings on your PSP. To do this, press the Home button on your device. This will bring up a menu on the screen. From here, select the Settings option. This will bring up a list of options. Select the Network Settings option and then select the Wi-Fi Settings option.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Connect to the Wi-Fi Network</h3>

<p>Once you’ve selected the Wi-Fi Settings option, you’ll be taken to a list of available Wi-Fi networks. Select the network you want to connect to and enter the password if it is required. Once you’ve entered the password, your PSP will be connected to the Wi-Fi network.</p>

<h3>Step 4: Check Your Connection</h3>

<p>Once you’ve connected to the Wi-Fi network, you can check your connection by opening a web browser on your PSP. If the connection is successful, you’ll be able to browse the web. If the connection is not successful, you may need to check the settings and try again.</p>
