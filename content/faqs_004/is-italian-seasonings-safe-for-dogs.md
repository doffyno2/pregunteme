<h2>What is Italian Seasoning?</h2>

Italian seasoning is a blend of herbs, usually including basil, oregano, marjoram, rosemary, and thyme. This seasoning is often used in Italian dishes, but can also be used in a variety of other recipes. Italian seasoning is available in both fresh and dried forms, and can be used as a rub or added to sauces, soups, and casseroles.

<h2>Is Italian Seasoning Safe for Dogs?</h2>

The good news is that Italian seasoning is generally considered safe for dogs. In fact, many veterinarians recommend adding small amounts of Italian seasoning to your dog’s food in order to add flavor and improve their overall nutrition. However, it is important to remember that Italian seasoning should only be used in moderation. Too much of this seasoning can cause digestive upset and could even be toxic to your dog if consumed in large amounts.

If you want to add Italian seasoning to your dog’s food, you should start off by using small amounts.
