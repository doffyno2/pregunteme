<p>Netflix is one of the most popular streaming services in the world, offering a wide variety of films, TV shows, and documentaries. As with any streaming service, Netflix pays production companies for the rights to stream their content. But how much does Netflix pay for a documentary?</p>

<p>The answer to this question depends on several factors, such as the length of the documentary, the production company's reputation, and the popularity of the documentary. Netflix typically pays production companies a fixed fee for the rights to stream their content, but some companies may also receive a portion of the revenue generated from streaming the documentary.</p>

<p>The length of the documentary is one of the most important factors when it comes to Netflix's payment. The longer the documentary, the more money Netflix will typically pay. Generally speaking, Netflix pays more for feature-length documentaries than they do for short films. The production company's reputation is also a factor in how much Netflix pays for a documentary. If the production company is well-known and has a strong reputation, Netflix may be willing to pay more for the rights to stream their content.</p>

<p>The popularity of the documentary is also a factor in how much Netflix pays. Netflix may be willing to pay more for a documentary that has a proven track record in terms of viewership, as they know that more people will be likely to watch the documentary on their platform. Netflix also takes into consideration the awards and nominations that the documentary has received.</p>

<p>In general, Netflix pays production companies around $3,000 to $20,000 for the rights to stream their documentary. However, the exact amount depends on the factors mentioned above. Netflix also pays production companies a portion of the revenue generated from streaming the documentary, which typically ranges from around 10% to 50%.</p>
