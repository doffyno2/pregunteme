<h2>What is MSG?</h2>

<p>MSG stands for monosodium glutamate, and it is a flavor enhancer made from glutamic acid, which is naturally found in many foods like tomatoes, mushrooms, and Parmesan cheese. When MSG is added to food, it gives it a savory, umami flavor. It is commonly used in Asian cuisine, and it is also found in many processed foods like snacks and instant noodles.</p>

<h2>How Does MSG Taste?</h2>

<p>When added to food, MSG has a mild, pleasant taste that can be described as "savory" or "umami." This flavor is often described as a combination of salty, sweet, and sour. It is often used to enhance the flavor of dishes that are otherwise bland, such as Chinese stir-fries or fried rice. MSG is also used to reduce the bitterness of some foods, such as broccoli and cauliflower.</p>
