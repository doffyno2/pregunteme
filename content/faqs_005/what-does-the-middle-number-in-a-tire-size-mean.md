<h2>What Is The Middle Number In A Tire Size?</h2>

The middle number in a tire size is the aspect ratio, which is the ratio of the sidewall height to the width of the tire. It is expressed as a percentage, and is usually followed by a letter which indicates the construction of the tire. For example, a tire with a size of 215/55R17 would have an aspect ratio of 55. This means that the sidewall height of the tire is 55% of the width of the tire. 

<h2>What Does The Aspect Ratio Mean?</h2>

The aspect ratio of a tire is important because it indicates how much cushioning the tire provides. The higher the aspect ratio, the more cushioning the tire will have. This is because a higher aspect ratio will result in a taller sidewall and more air being contained inside the tire, which increases the cushioning effect. Conversely, a lower aspect ratio will result in a shorter sidewall and less air being contained inside the tire, which reduces the cushioning effect.

The aspect ratio of a tire has a significant impact on the performance of a vehicle. Generally, tires with a higher aspect ratio will provide better handling and a more comfortable ride.
