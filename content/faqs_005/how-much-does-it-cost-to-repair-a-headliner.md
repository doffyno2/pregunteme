<h2>What Is a Headliner?</h2>

A headliner is a material lining the roof of a vehicle. In most cars, it’s a fabric material that covers the interior roof and is held in place with a foam backing adhesive. Headliners are meant to absorb sound, give the interior of a car some texture and color, and help to insulate the cabin from noise and heat.

<h2>Signs You Need to Repair or Replace Your Headliner</h2>

The most common sign that you need to repair or replace your car’s headliner is when it starts to sag or come loose from the roof of your vehicle. It may also start to show signs of discoloration or mildew due to age or water exposure. If you notice any of these signs, it’s important to take action as soon as possible to prevent further damage to the headliner.

<h2>Common Causes of Headliner Damage</h2>

The most common cause of headliner damage is age. As the years go by, the fabric covering the headliner can start to fade and tear due to wear and tear. Another common cause of headliner damage is water exposure. If your car has been left in the rain or exposed to high humidity, the headliner can start to sag and come loose. Additionally, if you’ve had any roof work done on your vehicle, this can also cause headliner damage.

<h2>Repair or Replace?</h2>

When it comes to repairing or replacing a headliner, it’s important to assess the extent of the damage. If the damage is minor, such as a few small holes or tears, it may be possible to repair it. However, if the damage is extensive, it’s usually best to replace the entire headliner.

<h2>Average Cost of Headliner Repair or Replacement</h2>

The cost of repairing or replacing a headliner depends on the type of vehicle, the extent of the damage, and the labor costs of the repair shop. In general, the cost of repairing a headliner can range from $150 to $400, while the cost of replacing a headliner can range from $250 to $800.
