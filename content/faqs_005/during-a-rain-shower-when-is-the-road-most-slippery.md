<h2>What Causes the Roads to be Slippery in the Rain?</h2>

<p>One of the primary factors that cause the roads to become slippery in the rain is the presence of oil and other substances that have been deposited onto the roads. These substances, such as oil and gasoline, create a slippery surface when mixed with water. The other factor that can cause the roads to become slippery is the amount of water on the road. When there is more water on the road, there is more potential for the road to become slick and slippery.</p>

<h2>When is the Road Most Slippery During a Rain Shower?</h2>

<p>The roads are most slippery during the beginning of the rain shower, when the oil and other substances are still on the surface of the road. This is because the rainwater mixes with the oil, creating a slippery surface. Additionally, the roads are also more slippery when there is a significant amount of water on the road, such as during a heavy rain shower. It is important to be aware of these factors and to adjust your driving accordingly.</p>

<h2>What Can Drivers Do to Stay Safe During a Rain Shower?</h2>

<p>There are several steps that drivers can take to stay safe during a rain shower.
