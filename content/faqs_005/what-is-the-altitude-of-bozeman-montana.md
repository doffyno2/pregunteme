<h2>What is Altitude?</h2>

Altitude is the height of an object or place in relation to sea level. It is measured in feet or meters, and is used to determine the amount of air pressure and oxygen at the given location. Altitude is an important factor for many activities, including skiing, climbing, and hiking, and is also beneficial for those living in high-altitude areas, as the air pressure can provide more oxygen to their lungs.

<h2>How is Altitude Measured?</h2>

Altitude is typically measured using three different methods. The first is through ground-based measurements, which are taken using a barometer or altimeter. The second is air pressure measurements, which are taken with a sensitive instrument called an aneroid barometer. The third method is through satellite measurements, which measure the angle between a satellite and the Earth's surface to calculate the altitude.
