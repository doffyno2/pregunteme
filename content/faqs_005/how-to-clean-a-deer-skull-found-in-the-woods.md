How to Clean a Deer Skull Found in the Woods 
The beauty of a deer skull is something to behold, but it's not something you'd want to keep in its natural state. As with any animal remains, a deer skull can become infested with bacteria and other organisms unless it is properly cleaned. Thankfully, cleaning a deer skull is a relatively easy process that can be done with just a few simple steps.


Before you start cleaning a deer skull, you'll need to gather the following supplies:

<ul>
	<li>A large container</li>
	<li>Bleach or a strong degreaser</li>
	<li>A brush or scrub pad</li>
	<li>A long-handled brush or scrubber</li>
	<li>A hose or sprayer</li>
	<li>Gloves</li>
</ul>


Now that you have all the supplies you need, you can begin cleaning the deer skull. Here is a step-by-step guide to the process:

<h2> Step 1: Prepare the Container</h2>
The first step is to prepare the container. Fill the container with warm water and add a few drops of bleach or a strong degreaser. This will help to kill any bacteria or organisms living on the skull.

<h2> Step 2: Soak the Skull</h2>
Once the container is prepared, submerge the skull in the mixture and let it soak for several hours or overnight. This will help to loosen any dirt or debris that may be stuck to the skull.

<h2> Step 3: Scrub the Skull</h2>
Once the skull has had a chance to soak, it's time to scrub it. Use a brush or scrub pad to remove any stubborn dirt or debris. Be sure to wear gloves to protect your hands from the harsh chemicals. If necessary, use a long-handled brush or scrubber to reach any hard-to-reach areas.

<h2> Step 4: Rinse the Skull</h2>
Once you have finished scrubbing, it's time to rinse the skull. Use a hose or sprayer to rinse away any remaining dirt or debris.

<h2> Step 5: Dry the Skull</h2>
Once the skull is clean, it's time to dry it. Place the skull in a sunny spot and let it air dry. This may take several hours or overnight.

<h2> Step 6: Whiten the Skull</h2>
Once the skull is dry, you can whiten it. You can use a whitening solution or a whitening toothpaste to whiten the skull. This will help to give it a clean, polished look.


<h2>How long should I soak the skull?</h2>
It is recommended that you soak the skull for several hours or overnight to allow the mixture to loosen any dirt or debris.

<h2>What kind of brush should I use?</h2>
It is best to use a brush or scrub pad that is specifically designed for cleaning skulls. This will help to ensure that the skull is not scratched or damaged.

<h2>What should I do if the skull is still dirty?</h2>

<h2>How long should I let the skull air dry?</h2>
It is recommended that you let the skull air dry for several hours or overnight. This will help to ensure that the skull is completely dry before whitening.

Yes, it is safe to use bleach or a strong degreaser on the skull. However, it is important to wear gloves to protect your hands from the harsh chemicals.
