<h2>What Causes Bad Weather?</h2>

<p>Bad weather is typically caused by a combination of factors, including conditions in the atmosphere and the movement of air masses. Atmospheric conditions, such as temperature and humidity, can cause clouds to form and storms to develop. Air masses, which are large areas of air with similar characteristics, can also affect the weather. When air masses move, they can cause changes in temperature, humidity, and pressure, which can create storms.</p>

<h2>Which Direction Does Bad Weather Come From?</h2>

<p>Bad weather can come from any direction, but there are some general patterns that can help you determine which direction it is likely to come from. In general, storms tend to move from west to east, which means that bad weather is more likely to come from the west. This is because storms are typically created when warm, moist air meets cold air, and the warm air is typically found on the western side of a storm system.</p>

<h2>What Is an Air Mass?</h2>

<p>An air mass is a large area of air with similar characteristics, such as temperature and humidity. Air masses are created when air from a certain region of the Earth is heated and cooled by the Sun, and these air masses can move around and affect the weather. When two different air masses meet, this can cause changes in temperature, humidity, and pressure, which can create storms.</p>
