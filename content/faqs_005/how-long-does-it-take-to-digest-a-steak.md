<h2>What Is Digestion?</h2>

Digestion is the process of breaking down food into smaller particles, which can then be absorbed by the body and used for energy and nutrients. Digestion is carried out by the digestive system, which is made up of the stomach, small intestine, and large intestine. 

The process of digestion starts in the mouth, where chewing begins to break down the food. As it is chewed, saliva is released, which helps to break down the food and begin the digestion process. When the food reaches the stomach, it is mixed with digestive juices and enzymes, which help to break down the food into even smaller particles. The food then moves into the small intestine, where additional enzymes and juices are added to further break down the food. Finally, the food reaches the large intestine, where it is further broken down and absorbed into the bloodstream.
