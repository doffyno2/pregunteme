<p>When fireworks are set off, the explosive material inside the shell creates a loud blast of sound. In flat terrain with clear skies, the sound wave will travel further than it would in hilly terrain with overcast skies.</p>

<p>The sound wave also needs a medium through which to travel. In the case of fireworks, this medium is the air. The higher the temperature or the denser the air, the further the sound will travel before it's dissipated.</p>
