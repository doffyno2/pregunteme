<p>Heat shields are essential components of a car’s exhaust system that protect the vehicle from the intense heat of the exhaust gases. Heat shields are made of metal and are designed to reflect the heat away from the engine and other components of the car. Heat shields can become damaged over time due to age, corrosion, or even a collision. When this happens, it’s important to get the heat shield repaired as soon as possible to ensure the safety of the vehicle and its passengers. But how much does it cost to repair a heat shield?</p>

<h2>What Causes Heat Shield Damage?</h2> 

<p>Heat shields can become damaged for a variety of reasons. The most common cause of heat shield damage is age. Over time, the metal of the heat shield can corrode due to exposure to the intense heat of the exhaust gases. Another common cause of heat shield damage is a collision or other type of accident. In some cases, the heat shield may also be damaged due to improper installation.</p>

<h2>What Are the Signs of Heat Shield Damage?</h2> 

<p>The most common sign of heat shield damage is a rattling noise coming from the exhaust system. This noise is caused by the loose pieces of the heat shield rattling against the frame of the car. Other signs of heat shield damage include visible corrosion on the metal of the heat shield and increased exhaust fumes entering the vehicle’s cabin.</p>
