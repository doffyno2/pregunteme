<p>Lysol is a brand name of a cleaning and disinfecting product manufactured by Reckitt Benckiser. It is a combination of several compounds, including ethanol, isopropanol, and a surfactant. <h2>Is Lysol Toxic to Dogs?</h2>

<p>The short answer is, it depends. Lysol products are generally safe to use around pets, as long as they are not ingested. However, Lysol can be hazardous to dogs if they ingest it. Ingestion can lead to irritation of the gastrointestinal tract, as well as vomiting, diarrhea, and difficulty breathing. If you suspect your pet has ingested Lysol, you should contact your veterinarian immediately.</p>

<h2>How Can I Keep My Dog Safe From Lysol?</h2>

<p>The best way to keep your dog safe from Lysol is to store it in a secure place out of reach of your pet. Alternately, you can store it in a locked area that your pet cannot access. It is also important to keep Lysol away from food and water dishes, as ingestion can be deadly. Finally, always use Lysol in well-ventilated areas, as inhalation of its fumes can cause respiratory irritation.</p>
