<p>Tire sidewall thickness is measured in millimeters (mm). The sidewall thickness of a tire is usually indicated by a three-digit number. The first two digits represent the width of the tire and the last digit indicates the sidewall thickness. For example, a tire with a sidewall thickness of 75 would have a sidewall thickness of 7.5 mm.</p>

<p>The sidewall thickness of a tire can vary depending on the type of tire and the size of the wheel. Generally, tires with lower aspect ratios (the ratio of sidewall height to tread width) will have thicker sidewalls. For example, a tire with an aspect ratio of 65 would have a thicker sidewall than a tire with an aspect ratio of 55.</p>
