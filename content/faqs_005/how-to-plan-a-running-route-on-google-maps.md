Google Maps has become one of the most popular online mapping services, offering directions and points of interest for destinations around the world. The Google Maps app is available for Android and iOS devices, as well as the web version. One of its great features is the ability to plan running routes. With the help of the Google Maps app, you can easily plan your running routes and get directions to wherever you want to go.

<h2>Step 1: Find Your Starting Point</h2>

The first step to planning a running route on Google Maps is to determine your starting point. You can either enter an address or use your current location. To use your current location, you will need to turn on your location settings in the app. Once you have set your starting point, you can start planning your route.

<h2>Step 2: Set Your Destination</h2>

The next step is to set your destination. You can search for an address or landmark, or you can draw your route on the map. You can choose to search for a specific address or you can use the map to draw your route and get directions to your destination.

<h2>Step 3: Choose Your Route Options</h2>

Once you have set your starting point and destination, it's time to choose your route options. Google Maps offers a range of route options, such as walking, cycling, and running. You can choose to avoid highways, tolls, and ferries if you wish. You can also choose to avoid certain roads or areas that you don't want to pass through.

<h2>Step 4: Set Your Route</h2>

Once you have chosen your route options, you can set your route. Google Maps will generate a route based on your chosen options and your starting and destination points. You can adjust the route as you wish, by dragging the route to the roads that you want to take. You can also add waypoints along your route, if you wish to make stops or visit certain points of interest.

<h2>Step 5: Save and Share Your Route</h2>

Once you have set your route, you can save it for future use. You can also share it with your friends or family if you wish. This is very useful if you are going on a long run with someone else and you want to make sure that you are both on the same page.

<h2>Step 6: Start Your Run</h2>

Once you have saved your route, it's time to start your run. You can start your run at any time, as long as you have your device with you. Google Maps will provide you with turn-by-turn directions, so you can easily follow your route. The app also has a feature that will let you know when you are nearing your destination.
