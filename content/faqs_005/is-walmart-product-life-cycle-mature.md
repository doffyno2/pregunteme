<h2>What is the Product Life Cycle?</h2>

The product life cycle is the process by which products are introduced, developed, marketed, and eventually discontinued. This cycle is important for companies as it helps them to identify the stages in the life of a product, and to plan effectively for the future. 

<h2>What is the Product Life Cycle of Walmart?</h2>

Walmart follows a typical product life cycle when introducing, marketing, and discontinuing products. Walmart typically starts by introducing a new product to the market. This can be done through advertising, promotions, and other forms of marketing. Once the product has been introduced, Walmart will then focus on developing and marketing the product. This can include introducing new features or variations of the product, as well as providing customer service and support. As the product matures, Walmart will transition to a more mature stage of the product life cycle, where they focus on maintaining the product and ensuring customer satisfaction. Finally, when the product has reached its maturity, Walmart will start to consider discontinuing the product, either due to lack of customer demand or to make room for more innovative products.

<h2>What Makes a Product Life Cycle Mature?</h2>

A mature product life cycle is one that is well-planned and managed, allowing a company to maximize its returns and minimize its losses.
