<h2>Ingredients and Supplies Needed</h2>

<p>The one thing you’ll need most of all is marshmallows. You can use any type of marshmallows you like, including mini marshmallows. You’ll also need a stove, a pot, a rubber spatula, and a bowl to melt the marshmallows in. If you’re making any recipes where you need to spread the melted marshmallows, you’ll also need a baking sheet.</p>

<h2>Step-by-Step Instructions</h2>

<p>The first step is to heat the pot on the stove over medium heat. Once the pot is heated, add the marshmallows and stir them often with the rubber spatula. The marshmallows will start to melt and you’ll want to keep stirring them to prevent them from sticking to the bottom of the pot. Keep stirring until all of the marshmallows are melted and then remove the pot from the heat.</p>

<p>Once the marshmallows have melted, you can transfer them to a bowl. If you’re making any recipes with melted marshmallows, now is the time to spread the marshmallows onto the baking sheet. You can do this with the rubber spatula. If you’re just melting marshmallows for snacking, you can serve them directly from the bowl. Enjoy!</p>

<h2>Tips and Tricks for Melting Marshmallows</h2>

<p>There are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind when melting marshmallows. First, it’s important to use low heat. If you use too high of a heat, the marshmallows can burn. Second, make sure to stir the marshmallows constantly to keep them from sticking to the bottom of the pot. Finally, make sure to remove the pot from the heat as soon as the marshmallows are melted. This will prevent them from burning.</p>
