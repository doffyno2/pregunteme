<h2>Geography of the Rocky Mountains</h2>

The Rocky Mountains are a major mountain range in western North America, stretching from the northernmost part of British Columbia, Canada, in the south to the northern border of New Mexico, USA, in the north. They extend from the Canadian provinces of British Columbia, Alberta, and Saskatchewan, to the US states of Montana, Wyoming, Colorado, Idaho, Utah, and New Mexico.

The Rocky Mountains are divided into three main sections: the Southern Rockies, the Central Rockies, and the Northern Rockies. The Southern Rockies include the states of Montana, Wyoming, Colorado, and New Mexico, while the Central Rockies include Idaho, Utah, and parts of Wyoming, Montana, and Colorado. The Northern Rockies include the provinces of British Columbia and Alberta.

The highest peak in the Rocky Mountains is Mount Elbert, located in Colorado, with an elevation of 4,401 meters (14,440 feet). The longest river in the Rocky Mountains is the Fraser River, which flows through British Columbia and into the Pacific Ocean.
