<p>Going through the application process for the TSA Global Entry program can be a time-consuming and cumbersome task. So it's important to make sure you have everything you need when you attend your Global Entry interview. Whether you're a first-time applicant or a returning applicant, there are certain items you'll need to bring with you to your interview.</p>

<h2>Required Documents</h2>

<p>When you attend your Global Entry interview, you'll need to bring some important documents with you. These documents include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Valid passport or other government-issued ID</li>
	<li>Proof of residence (utility bills, mortgage, or rental agreement)</li>
	<li>Proof of address (driver's license or other government-issued ID)</li>
	<li>Proof of U.S. citizenship or lawful permanent resident status (birth certificate, naturalization certificate, or green card)</li>
</ul>

<h2>Fingerprinting</h2>

<p>At your Global Entry interview, you'll also be asked to provide fingerprints for a background check. Be sure to bring your fingerprints with you to the interview. You can obtain fingerprints from a law enforcement agency or a fingerprinting service.</p>

<h2>Additional Materials</h2>

<p>In addition to the documents and fingerprinting, you may also be asked to provide additional information or materials to the interviewer. This may include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>A photo ID</li>
	<li>Any supporting documents or materials, such as employment verification, school transcripts, or other documents</li>
	<li>A list of countries visited in the past five years</li>
	<li>A copy of your most recent tax return</li>
	<li>Any other documents or materials requested by the interviewer</li>
</ul>

<h2>What to Wear</h2>

<p>When attending a Global Entry interview, it's important to dress appropriately. Wear business casual attire, such as a collared shirt, slacks, or dress. Avoid wearing anything that could be considered inappropriate or offensive. In addition, remove any hats or sunglasses when entering the interview room.</p>
