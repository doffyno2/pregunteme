<p>The anion gap is typically calculated by subtracting the total serum chloride level from the total serum bicarbonate level. Low anion gap levels below 8 mmol/L are generally considered to be abnormal and indicative of a medical condition. </p>

<p>When the anion gap is low, it is often associated with electrolyte imbalance.
