<h2>What is a Mountain?</h2>

A mountain is a large natural elevation of the Earth's surface that usually has steep sides and a pointed or rounded top. Mountains can be found on every continent and range in height from a few hundred feet to several miles. Mount Everest, located in the Himalayas range, is the highest mountain on Earth, standing at an impressive 8,848 meters (29,029 feet).

<h2>How are Mountains Formed?</h2>

Mountains are formed by a variety of processes. The most common type of mountain formation is through tectonic plates colliding and pushing up the Earth’s crust. This is why most mountain ranges are found near the edges of continents. Other mountain formation processes include volcanic activity and erosion.

<h2>What is the Average Weight of a Mountain?</h2>

The average weight of a mountain is difficult to determine, as this is dependent on the size and composition of the mountain itself. Generally, the larger the mountain is, the higher its weight. For example, the weight of Mount Everest is estimated to be around 6,000,000,000 tons.

<h2>What Factors Affect the Weight of a Mountain?</h2>

The weight of a mountain is affected by several factors, including its size, composition, and the amount of material it contains. The size of a mountain is determined by its height, width, and depth. The composition of a mountain is determined by the type of rock and soil it is formed from. Lastly, the amount of material a mountain contains is determined by the amount of snow, ice, and vegetation present on the mountain.

<h2>What is the Lightest Mountain in the World?</h2>
