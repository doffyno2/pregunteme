The answer is yes, you can glue metal sunglass frames, but you need to be aware of the risks involved.

<h2>What Adhesive is Best for Sunglass Frames?</h2>

When it comes to gluing metal sunglass frames, there are a few things to consider. The first is the type of adhesive you should use. The best adhesive for sunglass frames is epoxy. Epoxy is an adhesive that is specifically designed for metal and plastic, making it perfect for glasses frames. It is also strong enough to hold the frame together and durable enough to last a long time.

<h2>How to Glue Metal Sunglass Frames?</h2>

When you are ready to glue the metal sunglass frames, there are a few steps you need to take. First, you need to clean the frame and make sure it is free of dirt and dust. Then, use a cotton swab and rubbing alcohol to remove any oils or residue. Once the frame is clean and dry, it is time to apply the epoxy.

For best results, it is important to apply the epoxy evenly. Start by applying a small amount of epoxy around the edges of the frame. Then, use a toothpick to spread the epoxy around. Make sure to spread it evenly and avoid applying too much in one area. Allow the epoxy to dry for 24 hours before wearing the glasses.

<h2>What Can You Do If the Glue Does Not Hold?</h2>

If the glue does not hold the frame together or the frame continues to become loose over time, it is important to take the glasses to a professional for repair. A professional will be able to assess the damage and determine the best course of action. In some cases, the frame may need to be replaced, but in other cases, a simple repair may be all that is needed.
