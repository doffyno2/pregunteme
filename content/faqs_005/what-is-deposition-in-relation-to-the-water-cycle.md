<h2>What is Deposition?</h2>

<p>Deposition is the process of water vapor turning into a solid or liquid. It is the last stage of the water cycle before the water returns to the atmosphere. During deposition, the water vapor cools and condenses, forming tiny droplets of liquid water or ice crystals. The tiny droplets of water or ice crystals then fall to the ground or sea as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.</p>

<h2>How Does Deposition Occur?</h2>

<p>Deposition occurs when the air is no longer able to hold the water vapor, and the vapor condenses into tiny droplets of liquid water or ice crystals. This is usually due to a decrease in temperature, an increase in pressure, or a combination of both.
