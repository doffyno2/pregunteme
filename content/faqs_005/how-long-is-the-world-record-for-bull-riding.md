<h2>What is Bull Riding?</h2>

Bull riding is a rodeo event in which a cowboy (or cowgirl) attempts to stay mounted on a bucking bull for as long as possible. The bull is typically wild and unbroken, and it is the rider’s job to stay atop the bull as it bucks and twists. Riders use a bull rope to stay in the saddle, but the most important factor is their own skill and balance.

<h2>History of Bull Riding</h2>

Bull riding has a long history in the United States and dates back to the late 19th century. It originated in the American West, where cowboys would often attempt to ride wild bulls for fun or as a way to prove their courage. Later, professional rodeos began to incorporate bull riding as an event, and it has become a staple of the sport ever since.

<h2>How is Bull Riding Scored?</h2>

Bull riding is scored based on a combination of factors. Judges look at the rider’s ability to stay in the saddle, the bull’s strength and performance, and the length of time the rider stays on the bull. The maximum score for bull riding is 100 points, and the highest possible score is a perfect score of 100 points.
