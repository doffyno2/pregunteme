<h2>What is Expanding Foam?</h2>

Expanding foam is a type of foam used for insulation and other construction projects. It is a polyurethane foam that is sprayed onto surfaces and expands when it comes in contact with air. It is usually used to fill small cracks and gaps in walls, ceilings, and floors and can also be used to insulate pipes, windows, and other parts of the home.

<h2>How to Remove Dried Expanding Foam from Clothes?</h2>

If the expanding foam has already dried, you’ll need to take a few extra steps to remove it. Here are some tips for removing dried expanding foam from clothes:

<h3>1. Scrape Away the Foam</h3>

The first step is to scrape away as much of the dried foam as possible. Use a butter knife or a credit card to scrape away the foam. You should be careful not to damage the fabric of the clothing.

<h3>2. Apply Acetone</h3>

Once you have scraped away as much of the foam as possible, you can use acetone to help loosen the remaining foam. Apply a small amount of acetone to the foam and use a soft cloth to rub it in.

<h3>3. Use a Vacuum Cleaner</h3>

Once the foam has been loosened, use a vacuum cleaner to suck up the remaining foam. Be sure to use a vacuum cleaner with a brush attachment so that you can reach into all the nooks and crannies of the fabric.

<h3>4. Wash the Clothing</h3>

Once the foam has been removed, you can wash the clothing as usual. Be sure to use a mild detergent and warm water.
