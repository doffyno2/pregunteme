<h2>What Are the TSA Rules for Bringing a Water Bottle on a Plane?</h2>

The TSA (Transportation Security Administration) has specific rules on what items can be brought on a plane. According to the TSA, you can bring a reusable water bottle on a plane as long as it is empty when passing through security. You can fill it up after passing through security, as long as it does not exceed the 3.4 ounce limit for liquids and gels.

<h2>What Types of Water Bottles Are Allowed on a Plane?</h2>

Any type of collapsible or insulated water bottle is allowed on a plane, including metal, plastic, and silicone water bottles. However, the bottle must be empty when passing through security. The TSA also requires that all liquid containers, including water bottles, must fit in a single, quart-sized clear plastic bag.
