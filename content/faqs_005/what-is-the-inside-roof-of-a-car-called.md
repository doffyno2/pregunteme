What is the Inside Roof of a Car Called?


The inside roof of a car is an important component of the body of the vehicle. It is the part that separates the interior of the car from the outside. It is usually made of metal, plastic, or fiberglass and is typically coated with a finish that helps to protect the material from the elements. The inside roof of the car is also referred to as the headliner and is often decorated with fabric or other materials to give the interior of the car a more luxurious look.

The inside roof of a car can be made from a variety of materials including metal, plastic, or fiberglass. Metal roofs are typically the most durable and are available in both steel and aluminum. Plastic roofs are lighter in weight and typically less expensive than metal roofs. Fiberglass roofs are often used for luxury vehicles and are available in a range of colors and finishes.
