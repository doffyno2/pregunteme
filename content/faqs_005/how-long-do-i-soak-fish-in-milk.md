<h2>Factors That Influence Soaking Time</h2>

The type of fish you are soaking is one of the most important factors when it comes to determining how long you should soak the fish in milk. For example, if you are soaking a thin-fleshed fish, such as a trout or a mackerel, you should only need to soak the fish for a few minutes. On the other hand, if you are soaking a thick-fleshed fish, such as a salmon or a tuna, you will need to soak the fish for longer, usually up to an hour.

The thickness of the fish also plays an important role in determining how long you should soak the fish in milk. Thinner fish, such as trout or mackerel, will not require as much soaking time as thicker fish, such as salmon or tuna. For thinner fish, you should aim for a soaking time of about 15 minutes, while for thicker fish, you should aim for a soaking time of about 45 minutes to an hour.
