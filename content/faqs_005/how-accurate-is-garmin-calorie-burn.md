<h2>What is Garmin Calorie Burn?</h2>

Garmin's calorie burn tracking feature is designed to track the number of calories burned during various activities. It uses a combination of factors such as heart rate, activity type, and duration to estimate the amount of calories burned during an activity. This feature is available on a variety of Garmin fitness trackers, including the Vivoactive, Vivofit, and Forerunner series.

<h2>How Accurate is Garmin Calorie Burn?</h2>

Garmin's calorie burn tracking feature is generally accurate, but it is not perfect. It relies on a combination of factors to estimate the amount of calories burned, which can lead to some discrepancies between the actual amount of calories burned and the amount that is estimated by the tracker. Additionally, the accuracy of the calorie burn tracking feature can vary depending on the type of activity being tracked. For example, activities that involve more intense movements, such as running or cycling, are likely to be more accurately tracked than activities that involve slower, less intense movements, such as walking or yoga. 

<h2>How to Improve Accuracy of Garmin Calorie Burn Tracking</h2>

There are several steps you can take to improve the accuracy of your Garmin calorie burn tracking. First, make sure you are wearing the tracker correctly. If the tracker is not worn correctly, it can lead to inaccurate readings. Additionally, make sure you are entering your body statistics accurately. Entering your height, weight, and age correctly can help the tracker track your calorie burn more accurately. Finally, if you are engaging in a more intense activity, such as running or cycling, consider using a heart rate monitor to track your calorie burn.
