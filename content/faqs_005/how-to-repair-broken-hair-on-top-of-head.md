<p>If you have suffered from severe hair breakage on the top of your head, it can be a difficult situation to deal with. The good news is that there are steps you can take to repair the damage and restore the health of your hair. In this article, we'll discuss some of the most effective methods for repairing broken hair on top of your head.</p>

<h2>Why Does Hair Break on Top of the Head?</h2>

<p>Hair breakage on the top of your head is often caused by several factors, such as using harsh products, over-styling, and heat damage. Other causes of hair breakage can include chemical treatments and styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons. If you are experiencing hair breakage on the top of your head, it is important to identify the underlying cause in order to repair the damage.</p>

<h2>Tips for Repairing Broken Hair on Top of Head</h2>

<p>There are a few simple steps you can take to help repair broken hair on top of your head. Here are some tips to get you started:</p>

<h3>Use a Deep Conditioner</h3>

<p>Using a deep conditioner can help to repair damaged hair. Look for a deep conditioner that is specifically designed for damaged hair, as this will help to replenish the moisture that has been lost due to breakage. Leave the conditioner on for at least 15 minutes before rinsing it out.</p>

<h3>Cut Back on Heat Styling</h3>

<p>Heat styling can cause severe damage to hair, especially when used on the top of your head. Try to cut back on the amount of heat styling you do, and if you do use a heated tool, make sure to use a heat protectant spray beforehand.</p>

<h3>Use a Wide-Tooth Comb</h3>

<p>Brushing your hair with a wide-tooth comb can help to reduce breakage and prevent further damage. Start at the ends and work your way up to the top of your head, taking care not to tug or pull at the strands.  Additionally, avoid brushing your hair when it is wet, as this can cause more breakage.</p>

<h3>Protect Your Hair at Night</h3>

<p>When sleeping at night, it is important to protect your hair from further breakage. Try wearing a satin or silk scarf to bed, as these fabrics are less likely to snag and cause breakage. Additionally, avoid using tight elastic bands or ponytails, as these can cause more damage to the hair.</p>

<h3>Trim Your Hair Regularly</h3>

<p>Trimming your hair regularly can help to keep it healthy and reduce breakage. Try to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair looking its best. Additionally, avoid over-trimming, as this can cause the hair to become thin and brittle.</p>

<p>When brushing damaged hair, it is best to use a wide-tooth comb. This will help to reduce breakage and prevent further damage. Start at the ends and work your way up to the top of your head, taking care not to tug or pull at the strands.</p>
