A switchblade is a type of knife that can be quickly deployed with the press of a button, spring-loaded lever, or switch. It is typically double-edged and has a blade that is 4 to 6 inches long. The blade is usually stowed in the handle, which makes the knife easily concealable.

<h2>How Does a Switchblade Work?</h2>

Switchblades are designed to quickly deploy a blade with the press of a button. This is accomplished by a combination of two components: a locking mechanism and a spring-loaded release. 

The locking mechanism is typically a small lever that holds the blade in place when it is closed. This lever is connected to a spring-loaded release, which is connected to a trigger. When the trigger is pressed, the release is activated and the lever is disengaged, allowing the blade to be deployed.
