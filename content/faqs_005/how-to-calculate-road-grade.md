<h2>What Is Road Grade?</h2>

Road grade, also known as grade or gradient, is the measure of the degree of slope of a road or other surface. It is typically expressed as a percentage, with a higher number indicating a more steep incline. <h2>How to Calculate Road Grade?</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Measure the Elevation Change</h3>

The first step in calculating the grade of a road is to measure the elevation change between two points. This can be done using a variety of tools, such as a clinometer, an electronic level, or a GPS unit. The elevation change between two points is the difference in height between the two points.

<h3>Step 2: Calculate the Grade</h3>

Once the elevation change has been measured, the grade can be calculated by dividing the elevation change by the horizontal distance between the two points. This is typically expressed as a percentage, with a higher number indicating a more steep incline.
