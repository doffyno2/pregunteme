<h2>What is Light in Minecraft?</h2>

Light, in the context of Minecraft, is the illumination generated from various sources. These sources include torches, lava, glowstone, and daylight. Light is important in Minecraft, as it helps to create a more immersive experience, and can provide players with an advantage in certain situations.

<h2>Which Blocks Can Light Pass Through?</h2>

Light can pass through certain blocks in Minecraft, and these blocks are known as “transmissive” blocks. Transmissive blocks are those that allow light to pass through them, much like a window or a pane of glass.

There are several transmissive blocks in Minecraft, including glass, ice, sea lanterns, and glowstone. The most common transmissive block is glass, and it can be obtained by smelting sand in a furnace.

<h2>Which Blocks Cannot Pass Light?</h2>

Not all blocks in Minecraft are transmissive, and some blocks completely block the passage of light. These blocks are known as “opaque” blocks, and they cannot be illuminated by any light source.

Opaque blocks are those that do not allow light to pass through them, and they include blocks such as stone, dirt, and obsidian. Opaque blocks are useful for creating cave systems, as they will block out any light sources.
