<h2>What is a Maintenance Light?</h2>
A maintenance light is a warning indicator that alerts you when your vehicle is due for service or maintenance. When the maintenance light comes on, it indicates that your vehicle is due for an oil change, air filter replacement, or other service. It's important to reset the light after any service or maintenance is performed, to ensure that the maintenance light will come on again at the appropriate time.

<h2>What Does the Maintenance Light Look Like?</h2>
The maintenance light appears as a yellow or orange light on the dashboard of your vehicle. The exact location of the light varies by model and make, so refer to your vehicle’s owner’s manual to find out exactly where it is.

<h2>Why Should You Reset the Maintenance Light?</h2>
It's important to reset the maintenance light after any service or maintenance is performed on your vehicle. This ensures that the light will come on again at the appropriate time, alerting you that it's time for another oil change or other service.

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light on a 2019 Toyota Corolla</h2>
Resetting the maintenance light on a 2019 Toyota Corolla is easy and can be done in a few simple steps.

<h3>Step 1: Turn the Ignition On</h3>
Turn the ignition on, but don’t start the engine. Make sure the gearshift is in “P” or “N”.

<h3>Step 2: Locate the Maintenance Reset Button</h3>
The maintenance reset button is located on the instrument panel, near the speedometer. It may be labeled as “RESET” or “MAINT REQD”.

<h3>Step 3: Press and Hold the Reset Button</h3>
Press and hold the reset button for at least five seconds. During this time, the maintenance light will flash on and off, and then turn off completely when the reset is complete.

<h3>Step 4: Turn the Ignition Off and On Again</h3>
Turn the ignition off and then on again. This will ensure that the reset has been successful.
