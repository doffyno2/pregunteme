<h2>What is a Carbohydrate?</h2>

Carbohydrates are organic molecules composed of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen. They are the main source of energy for our cells and are found in a variety of foods, including fruits, grains, legumes, and dairy products. Carbohydrates can be further broken down into three groups: monosaccharides, disaccharides, and polysaccharides. Monosaccharides are the simplest form of carbohydrate and consist of single sugar molecules. Disaccharides are two sugar molecules bound together, while polysaccharides are long chains of sugar molecules.

<h2>How Many Carbs Are in a Medium-Sized Orange?</h2>

A medium-sized orange contains approximately 16.7 grams of carbohydrates. Of this, 11.3 grams are sugars and 5.4 grams are dietary fiber. The sugars present in an orange are mainly fructose, glucose, and sucrose. Fructose is the main type of sugar found in oranges, and is a simple monosaccharide. Glucose is also a monosaccharide, while sucrose is a disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose molecules.
