Have you ever experienced a bright flash of light when you close your eyes? This phenomenon is known as ‘photopsia’ or ‘stroboscopic vision’ and it can be a very disconcerting experience. In this article, we’ll discuss what causes this to happen and what treatments are available. 

<h2>What Is Photopsia?</h2>

Photopsia is the medical term for the sensation of seeing flashes of light when you close your eyes. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including underlying medical conditions, medications, or lifestyle factors. 

The flashes of light can range from brief flashes to longer-lasting, continuous light. The light may be white, yellow, or blue and can be accompanied by a feeling of pressure behind the eyes. 

In some cases, the sensation of photopsia can be accompanied by other symptoms such as headaches, migraines, blurred vision, and dizziness. 

<h2>What Causes Photopsia?</h2>

There are several different potential causes of photopsia. Some of the most common causes include: 

<h3>Eye Conditions</h3>

Certain eye conditions, such as macular degeneration, can cause flashes of light when the eyes close. If you have any underlying eye conditions, it is important to get them diagnosed and treated as soon as possible. 

<h3>Medications</h3>

Certain medications, such as antibiotics and anti-seizure medications, can cause photopsia. If you suspect that your medication is causing the flashes of light, it is important to speak to your doctor as soon as possible. 

<h3>Migraines</h3>

Migraines can also cause photopsia. If you experience photopsia along with other migraine symptoms, such as sensitivity to light and sound, it is important to get your migraines treated as soon as possible. 

<h3>Stress and Anxiety</h3>

Stress and anxiety can also cause the sensation of photopsia. If you are feeling stressed or anxious, it is important to get the necessary help and support.
