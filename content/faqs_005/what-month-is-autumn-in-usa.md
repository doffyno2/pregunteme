<h2>What Is Autumn?</h2>
<p>Autumn is one of the four seasons that make up the year. It is the transition between summer and winter, and is typically a time of year when the leaves change color and the temperatures start to cool down. In the United States, autumn is also known as "fall" and typically takes place between the months of September and November. During this time, days become shorter, nights become cooler, and the leaves begin to change color from green to red, yellow, and orange. </p>

<h2>When Does Autumn Begin?</h2>
<p>In the United States, autumn usually begins in September and ends in November. In the northernmost parts of the country, the first day of autumn is usually celebrated on September 22, the autumnal equinox. This is the day when the length of daylight and nighttime are equal. In the southernmost parts of the country, autumn begins around October 22. </p>

<h2>What Are The Key Features Of Autumn?</h2>
<p>The key features of autumn in the United States include cooler temperatures, shorter days, and changing leaves. During this season, the average temperature begins to decline, with the average high temperatures in most areas ranging between 60 and 65 degrees Fahrenheit. In addition, the number of hours of daylight also starts to decrease, with the length of the day gradually diminishing until the winter solstice in December. Finally, during autumn, the leaves of deciduous trees typically change color from green to yellow, orange, and red, and eventually fall to the ground. </p>

<h2>What Are The Activities To Do During Autumn?</h2>
<p>Autumn is a great season for outdoor activities. Some popular activities include taking a walk in the woods to admire the changing leaves, visiting a pumpkin patch to pick out pumpkins for Halloween, going apple picking, and taking a hayride. Additionally, autumn is also a great time to spend time with family and friends by having a bonfire, roasting marshmallows, and making s'mores. </p>

<h2>What Are The Holidays During Autumn?</h2>
<p>The United States celebrates several holidays during autumn, including Labor Day, Columbus Day, Halloween, and Thanksgiving. Labor Day, which takes place on the first Monday in September, celebrates the contributions of workers to the U.S. economy. Columbus Day, which takes place on the second Monday in October, commemorates Christopher Columbus' arrival in the Americas in 1492. Halloween, which takes place on October 31, is a holiday celebrated with costumes, parties, and other festivities. Finally, Thanksgiving, which takes place on the fourth Thursday of November, is a holiday for giving thanks for the blessings of the harvest and of the preceding year. </p>
