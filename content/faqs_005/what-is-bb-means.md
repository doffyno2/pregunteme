<h2>What is Big Brothers Big Sisters of America?</h2>

Big Brothers Big Sisters of America (BBBS) is a non-profit organization that pairs mentors with children in need of positive role models and friendship. Through this program, adult volunteers are paired with children for a period of 1-2 years, depending on the situation. During this time, mentors and children spend time together going on outings, sharing meals, and doing activities that help build relationships and trust. 

<h2>What is the Purpose of Big Brothers Big Sisters?</h2>

The mission of BBBS is to provide children facing adversity with strong and enduring, professionally supported one-on-one relationships that change their lives for the better, forever. The organization seeks to help children reach their potential through positive experiences, friendship, and guidance. This helps improve the child’s self-confidence, communication skills, relationships with peers, and overall sense of wellbeing. 

<h2>What is the Process for Becoming a Big Brother/Big Sister?</h2>

The process to become a Big Brother/Big Sister starts with an application. After the application is approved, the organization pairs the applicant with a child and sets up an initial meeting. During this meeting, the mentor and the child get to know each other and decide if they want to move forward with the program. If both parties agree, then the mentor and the child can begin meeting once or twice a month. The mentor and the child can decide what activities to do together such as going to the movies, playing sports, sharing meals, or doing a project. The mentor also provides guidance and support to the child.
