<h2>What Are Stitches for Dogs?</h2>
<p>Stitches for dogs, also known as sutures, are a type of medical treatment that involves the use of a needle and thread to close a wound or repair a damaged or torn area of tissue. Stitches can be used to close deep wounds and prevent infection, hold together torn tissue, or close a hernia. They can be made of various materials, such as silk, nylon, or stainless steel.</p>

<h2>When Are Stitches Necessary for Dogs?</h2>
<p>Stitches are typically necessary when a wound or tear is deep enough that it won’t close on its own. In some cases, stitches may be necessary to prevent infection or further damage to the affected area. Stitches may be needed for a variety of reasons, including wounds caused by a bite, a broken bone, surgery, or a laceration. <h2>What Is the Process for Putting Stitches in Dogs?</h2>
<p>If stitches are necessary for your dog, your vet will first determine the type of suture material needed for the procedure. Depending on the size and location of the wound, the vet may use dissolvable sutures or non-dissolvable sutures. After the type of suture material is determined, your vet will use a needle and thread to close the wound and may use a topical anesthetic to reduce pain.</p>

<h2>How Long Do Stitches Stay in Dogs?</h2>
<p>The length of time that stitches stay in dogs depends on the type of suture material used. Dissolvable sutures will naturally break down over time, usually within seven to 14 days. Non-dissolvable sutures must be removed by your vet after the wound has healed. Your vet will determine when the sutures should be removed.</p>
