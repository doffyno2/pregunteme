Are Bicycle Helmets DOT Approved?

The Department of Transportation (DOT) has regulations for bike helmets to ensure that cyclists are properly protected. While the DOT does not approve bicycle helmets, they do require that all helmets meet the Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard (FMVSS) 218. This standard specifies the design, construction, performance, and labeling requirements for motorcycle helmets sold in the United States.

<h2>What is FMVSS 218?</h2>
FMVSS 218 is a safety standard issued by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA). The standard requires that all helmets manufactured for use on public roads in the United States meet a minimum set of safety requirements. These requirements include a minimum impact absorbing capability, a minimum retention system strength, and a minimum field of vision. In addition, the helmet must have a permanent label attached to the outside of the helmet that states that the helmet complies with the FMVSS 218 standards.

<h2>What is the Purpose of FMVSS 218?</h2>
The purpose of FMVSS 218 is to provide a minimum level of protection for motorcycle, moped, and bicycle riders in the event of an accident. The standard helps ensure that helmets sold in the United States provide riders with a reasonable level of protection from impacts and other injuries that could be sustained in a crash. 

<h2>Who Is Required to Comply with FMVSS 218?</h2>
All manufacturers, distributors, and retailers of helmets intended for use on public roads in the United States must comply with FMVSS 218. In addition, all helmets must include a label indicating that the helmet meets the FMVSS 218 standards.

<h2>Do Bicycle Helmets Need to Be DOT Approved?</h2>
No, bicycle helmets do not need to be DOT approved. However, they must meet the safety requirements specified in FMVSS 218. All bicycle helmets sold in the United States must include a label indicating that the helmet meets the FMVSS 218 standards.

<h2>Are Non-DOT Approved Helmets Legal?</h2>
Yes, non-DOT approved helmets are legal for use on public roads in the United States. However, it is important to note that non-DOT approved helmets may not provide the same level of protection as helmets that comply with FMVSS 218.
