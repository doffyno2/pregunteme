<h2>What is a Presta Valve?</h2>

<p>A Presta valve is a type of valve commonly used on bicycle tires and inner tubes. Unlike the Schrader valve, which is found on many car tires, the Presta valve is much narrower and requires an adapter for inflation. <h3>Step 2: Find the Valve Core</h3>

<p>Once you have identified your valve, the next step is to locate the valve core. The valve core is the small metal plunger that releases air from the tire. It is usually located at the top of the valve.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Unscrew the Valve Core</h3>

<p>Once you have located the valve core, the next step is to unscrew it. If you have a threaded valve, you will need to use an adjustable wrench to unscrew the valve core. If you have a non-threaded valve, you can simply grip the valve core with your fingers and unscrew it. Be sure to unscrew the valve core all the way, as this will ensure that air can flow freely into the tire.</p>

<h3>Step 5: Replace the Valve Core</h3>

<p>Once the tire is inflated to the desired pressure, the last step is to replace the valve core. Simply screw the valve core back into place and you are done!</p>
