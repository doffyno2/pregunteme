<h2>What Are Split Peas?</h2>

Split peas are the dry, mature form of green or yellow peas. The pea is harvested when it is mature, dried, and then split along the natural seam. This process creates split peas, which are small, round, and pale yellow or green in color. Split peas are much faster to cook than whole peas, and they also have a slightly nuttier taste.

<h2>Nutritional Information for Split Peas</h2>

Split peas are a great source of nutrition. They are high in protein, fiber, and low in fat and calories. One cup of cooked split peas provides about 12 grams of protein, 16 grams of fiber, and only 0.4 grams of fat. Split peas are also a good source of important vitamins and minerals, including folate, iron, magnesium, and potassium.

A pound of split peas is equal to approximately 2.5 to 2.75 cups of split peas. The exact amount of split peas in a pound will vary slightly depending on the size of the peas and how they have been split.
