<h2>What Is an Alignment?</h2>

An alignment, also known as wheel alignment, is a service that ensures that your vehicle's tires are positioned correctly relative to each other. Properly aligned tires will help to improve the vehicle's performance, fuel economy, and handling. An alignment also helps to reduce tire wear, as tires that are out of alignment will wear unevenly. 

<h2>Why Do You Need an Alignment?</h2>

There are a few reasons why you might need an alignment. The most common reasons are: 
<ul>
  <li>Hitting a pothole or curb</li>
  <li>Frequent hard braking</li>
  <li>Regularly driving on rough surfaces</li>
</ul>

If your vehicle is experiencing any of these issues, it might be time for an alignment. Other signs that you need an alignment include uneven tire wear, steering wheel vibration, or the vehicle pulling to one side when driving.
