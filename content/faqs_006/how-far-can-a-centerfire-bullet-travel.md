<h2>What is a Centerfire Bullet?</h2>

<p>A centerfire bullet is a type of ammunition that is used in firearms that employ a centerfire primer. The primer is a small disc or tube that is filled with a compound that is sensitive to impact or heat. When struck by the firing pin of a firearm, the primer ignites the propellant powder, which then ignites the bullet and propels it out of the barrel of the gun.</p>

<p>Centerfire bullets are typically used in rifles and handguns, as opposed to shotguns, which use shotgun shells. Centerfire bullets come in a variety of sizes, shapes, and weights, and are typically made from lead or copper. The most common type of centerfire bullet is the full metal jacket (FMJ), which is a bullet encased in a metal jacket that helps to reduce the amount of friction between the bullet and the barrel of the gun.</p>
