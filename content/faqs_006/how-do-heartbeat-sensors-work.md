<h2>What Is a Heartbeat Sensor?</h2>

A heartbeat sensor (also known as an electrocardiograph or ECG) is a device that measures the electrical signals generated by your heart. It can be used to monitor your heart rate, detect arrhythmias (irregular heartbeats), and diagnose heart conditions.

The most common type of heartbeat sensor is an electrocardiograph (ECG). This device is used to measure the electrical activity of your heart by attaching electrodes to your chest, arms, and legs. The ECG then records the electrical signals generated by your heart and displays them on a graph.

<h2>How Does an ECG Work?</h2>

An ECG measures the electrical activity of your heart by detecting the small electrical signals generated when your heart's chambers contract. These electrical signals are then displayed on a graph, which is called an electrocardiogram.

An ECG works by attaching electrodes to your chest, arms, and legs. As your heart beats, it produces tiny electrical signals. These signals are picked up by the electrodes and sent to the ECG machine, which then displays them on a graph. The ECG graph shows your heart rate and rhythm, and can be used to detect any irregularities.

<h3>2. Pulse Oximeter</h3>

A pulse oximeter is a small device that measures the amount of oxygen in your blood. It works by attaching a clip to your finger or earlobe and measuring the oxygen content in your blood. This type of heartbeat sensor is typically used in medical settings and can be used to detect changes in oxygen levels in your blood.

<h3>3. Wearable Heartbeat Sensors</h3>

Wearable heartbeat sensors are small devices that can be worn on the wrist or other areas of the body. These sensors measure your heart rate and can be used to monitor your activity levels. They are becoming increasingly popular as a way to track fitness and health.
