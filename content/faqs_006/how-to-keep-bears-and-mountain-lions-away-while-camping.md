## How to Keep Bears and Mountain Lions Away While Camping
It can be quite a challenge to protect yourself and your camping gear from wild animals while camping in the wilderness. Bears and mountain lions are two of the most dangerous creatures that can make your camping experience a nightmarish one. Fortunately, there are several ways to keep bears and mountain lions away while camping.

### How Does a Bear Behave? 
Bears are usually timid animals, and they rarely attack humans unless provoked. However, if you are camping in bear country, it is important to understand how bears behave so you can take the necessary precautions. Bears are omnivores, meaning they will eat both vegetation and meat. Therefore, they may be attracted to the smell of food in your camping area. Bears are also highly curious and they may investigate any unfamiliar objects or smells that strike their interest.

### How Does a Mountain Lion Behave?
Mountain lions are much more dangerous than bears, as they are predators and can view humans as prey. It is important to understand how mountain lions behave so that you can take the necessary precautions when camping in mountain lion territory. Mountain lions are solitary animals and typically hunt at night. They are also strong and powerful animals and can easily overpower a human if provoked.

### What Are the Best Ways to Keep Bears and Mountain Lions Away from Camp?
The best way to keep bears and mountain lions away from your camp area is to identify potential hazards and take the necessary precautions to reduce the risks. Here are a few tips for keeping bears and mountain lions away from your camp:

* Do not store food or other items that have a strong scent in or near your camp.
* Keep your camp area well lit at night.
* Avoid camping in areas known to have a large population of bears or mountain lions.
* Make noise while in the camp area to let the animals know that humans are present.
* Carry bear spray or pepper spray with you while in the camp area.
* Avoid sleeping in or near areas with a lot of vegetation or foliage, as this can attract bears or mountain lions.
