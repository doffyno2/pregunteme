Pressure cooking chicken bones for dogs is a quick and easy way to make sure your pup gets the most out of their meal. Pressure cooking chicken bones helps to break down the bones, making them easier to digest and absorb the nutrition. It also helps to keep the bones from splintering, which can be a choking hazard for our furry friends.

But how long do you need to pressure cook chicken bones for your pup? That depends on the type of bones you’re using, the size of the bones, and the pressure cooker you’re using. In this article, we’ll discuss all the factors that go into determining the right cooking time for chicken bones for your dog. 

<h2>What Kind of Bones Are Best for Pressure Cooking?</h2>

When it comes to pressure cooking chicken bones for your pup, it’s important to choose the right kind of bones. The best type of bones for pressure cooking are raw chicken necks, backs, and wings. These are the softer bones that are easier to break down in the pressure cooker. It’s also important to avoid bones that have been cooked in other ways. Boiled, fried, or baked bones are much more difficult to break down in the pressure cooker and can be a choking hazard for your pup. 

<h2>The Size of the Bones</h2>

The size of the bones also plays an important role in determining the cooking time. Smaller bones will require less cooking time, while larger bones will need more time. If you’re using large bones, it’s best to break them down into smaller pieces before pressure cooking them. This will help to ensure they cook evenly and will help to reduce the cooking time.

<h2>The Pressure Cooker You’re Using</h2>

The type of pressure cooker you’re using also plays a role in determining the cooking time. Most electric pressure cookers will have a maximum cooking time of 30 minutes, so if you’re using one of these models, it’s best to keep the cooking time to a minimum. Stovetop pressure cookers, on the other hand, can cook for up to an hour, so if you’re using one of these, you can increase the cooking time. 

<h2>How Long to Pressure Cook Chicken Bones for Dogs?</h2>

So, how long should you pressure cook chicken bones for your pup? The answer will depend on the factors we’ve discussed above. Generally speaking, you should pressure cook chicken bones for 15-20 minutes for small bones and up to 30 minutes for larger bones. It’s important to keep an eye on the pressure cooker while it’s cooking and make sure the bones are cooked through before serving them to your pup. 

<h2>What to Do with the Leftover Bones?</h2>

Once the bones have been cooked, it’s important to discard any remaining bones that your pup hasn’t eaten.
