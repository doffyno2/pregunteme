<p>Master Lock is one of the most popular and widely used locks in the world. It is known for being secure and reliable, making it a great choice for both residential and commercial applications. However, as with any lock, there are times when you may need to cut a Master Lock. Fortunately, with the right tools and some patience, you can easily cut through a Master Lock with bolt cutters.</p>

<h2>Things You Will Need</h2>
<p>Before you begin cutting your Master Lock, you will need the following items:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Bolt cutters</li>
  <li>Safety glasses</li>
  <li>Work gloves</li>
</ul>

<h2>Instructions</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Put on your safety glasses and work gloves. Bolt cutters are sharp and can cause serious injury if not handled properly.</li>
  <li>Open the bolt cutters so that the blades are fully extended. Place the bolt cutters around the shackle of the Master Lock.</li>
  <li>Squeeze the handles of the bolt cutters together. Keep squeezing until the shackle is cut through.</li>
  <li>Once the shackle is cut through, the Master Lock should be open.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Tips</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Bolt cutters can be purchased at most hardware stores.</li>
  <li>Make sure the bolt cutters you purchase are the right size for the job. For example, if you are cutting a Master Lock with a 5/8-inch shackle, you will need bolt cutters with a 5/8-inch cutting capacity.</li>
  <li>If you do not have access to bolt cutters, you can use a hacksaw to cut through a Master Lock. This will take longer, but it will still get the job done.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Warnings</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Always wear safety glasses and work gloves when using bolt cutters.</li>
  <li>Never use bolt cutters on a Master Lock without first unlocking it.</li>
  <li>Never place your fingers in between the blades of the bolt cutters.</li>
</ul>
