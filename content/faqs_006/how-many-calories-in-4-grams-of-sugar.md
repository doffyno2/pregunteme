<h2>What Is Sugar?</h2>

Sugar is a type of carbohydrate found naturally in many fruits and vegetables. It is also added to many processed foods and drinks to enhance their flavor. Common types of sugar include white granulated sugar, brown sugar, honey, and high-fructose corn syrup. Sugar is composed of two molecules, glucose and fructose, which are both forms of simple carbohydrates.

<h2>How Many Calories in 4 Grams of Sugar?</h2>

Four grams of sugar is equivalent to 16 calories. This amount of sugar is found in a teaspoon of granulated sugar, a tablespoon of honey, or a tablespoon of high fructose corn syrup. Consuming four grams of sugar is equivalent to one large banana or one and a half glasses of orange juice.

<h2>How Much Sugar Should You Have Per Day?</h2>

The American Heart Association recommends that adults limit their added sugar intake to no more than 36 grams per day for men and 25 grams per day for women. This equates to nine teaspoons of added sugar per day for men and six teaspoons of added sugar per day for women. It is important to note that naturally occurring sugars found in fruits and vegetables do not count towards this limit.
