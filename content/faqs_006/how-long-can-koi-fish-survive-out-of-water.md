<h2>Understanding the Dangers of Being Out of Water</h2>

Koi fish, like all fish, need to be in water to survive. Without water, the fish cannot absorb oxygen or get rid of waste products. This quickly leads to death, as the fish's cells become starved for oxygen and toxic waste builds up in their bodies.

Koi fish can also suffer from dehydration if they are out of water for too long. This is because their skin and gills are not able to retain water when they are not submerged. The exact amount of time that a koi can survive out of water will depend on the temperature, humidity, and other environmental factors. 

<h2>Factors That Affect How Long Koi Can Survive Out of Water</h2>

Koi can survive out of water for a few minutes to a few hours, depending on the environmental conditions. The most important factor is temperature: the higher the temperature, the shorter the time that the fish can survive without water. Koi can also survive longer in dry conditions, as dry air helps to slow down the rate of dehydration.

The size of the koi also plays a role in how long it can survive outside of water. Larger koi have more body mass and can therefore retain water for a longer period of time.

<h2>How to Keep Your Koi Safe</h2>

If you need to move your koi from one aquarium to another, it's important to keep the fish out of water for as short a time as possible. Here are some tips for keeping your koi safe during a move:

<ul>
<li>Keep the fish in a container filled with water to minimize their time out of water.</li>
<li>Keep the water at the same temperature as the aquarium they are coming from and going to.</li>
<li>Keep the container in a cool, dark place to reduce the rate of dehydration.</li>
<li>If possible, move the fish during the cooler hours of the day.</li>
