<h2>What is Voltage?</h2>

Voltage, or electrical potential difference, is the amount of energy available to move a unit of electrical charge from one point to another. It is measured in volts, which is the potential difference between two points of an electrical circuit. Voltage can be generated by many means, including batteries, generators, transformers and rectifiers.

<h2>What is the Difference Between Voltage and Amperage?</h2>

Voltage and amperage are two different ways to measure electricity. Voltage is measured in volts, while amperage is measured in amperes. Voltage is the difference in electrical potential between two points in an electrical circuit, while amperage is the amount of electric current flowing through a circuit.

<h2>How Much Voltage is Dangerous?</h2>

For AC, a voltage of greater than 1,000 mV is considered dangerous.
