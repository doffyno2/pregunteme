<h2>What are the Big Red Balls on Power Lines?</h2>

The big red balls that often hang from power lines are called insulators. They are used to keep electricity from flowing between two power lines. The insulators are made of porcelain or glass and are filled with air. The air insulates the electricity and prevents it from flowing between the two power lines. 

<h2>How do Insulators Work?</h2>

Insulators work by trapping the electricity and preventing it from flowing to other parts of the power lines. The insulators are attached to the power lines using metal clamps, which create a barrier between the two lines. The electricity is then trapped in the insulators and unable to flow to other parts of the power lines. 

<h2>Why Are Insulators Used?</h2>

Insulators are used to keep electricity from flowing between two power lines. This is important for safety reasons, as electricity can be dangerous when it flows through the wrong parts of a power line. Insulators also help to reduce power loss, as electricity is unable to escape the insulators and enter other parts of the power lines. 

<h2>Are Insulators the Only Thing Used on Power Lines?</h2>

No, insulators are not the only thing used on power lines. Other components are also used to help keep electricity from flowing between two power lines. These components include grounding rods, lightning arresters, stay rods, cross arms, and surge arresters. Each of these components helps keep electricity from flowing between two power lines. 

<h2>What Other Safety Measures are Taken?</h2>

Lightning arresters help to protect the power lines from lightning strikes, while grounding rods help to ensure that electricity is properly grounded. Surge arresters help to protect the power lines from power surges.
