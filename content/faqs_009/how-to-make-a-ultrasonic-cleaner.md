<p>Ultrasonic cleaners are devices that use sound waves to clean objects. They are used in a variety of industries, from jewelry cleaning to medical equipment sterilization. Making a homemade ultrasonic cleaner is a simple project that requires basic tools and materials. It can be built in an afternoon and will save you money.</p>

<h2>Gather the Materials</h2>

<p>To make an ultrasonic cleaner you will need the following materials:</p>

<ul>
  <li>A container such as a plastic bucket or metal pan.</li>
  <li>An ultrasonic transducer.</li>
  <li>A power supply.</li>
  <li>An electrical cord.</li>
  <li>A piezoelectric crystal.</li>
  <li>A soldering iron and solder.</li>
  <li>Two pieces of metal such as copper or aluminum.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Build the Transducer</h2>

<p>The transducer is the part of the ultrasonic cleaner that creates the sound waves. To build the transducer, you will need to solder the piezoelectric crystal to the two pieces of metal. Once the crystal is soldered to the metal, it will create a vibration when it is exposed to electricity, which will create the sound waves.</p>

<h2>Attach the Transducer to the Container</h2>

<p>Once the transducer is built, it needs to be attached to the container. This is done by drilling a hole in the container for the transducer to fit into. Then, the transducer is inserted into the hole and secured with screws or bolts. The transducer should fit snugly in the hole and should be able to move without being loose.</p>

<h2>Connect the Wiring</h2>

<p>The next step is to connect the wiring. The power supply will need to be connected to the transducer and the electrical cord should be connected to the power supply. Make sure to connect all the wires properly to avoid any electrical shock.</p>

<h2>Test the Ultrasonic Cleaner</h2>

<p>Once all the wiring is connected, it is time to test the ultrasonic cleaner. Place a small object such as a coin or small jewelry piece in the container and turn on the power supply. The object should vibrate in the container as the sound waves move through it. If the object does not vibrate, check the wiring and make sure it is properly connected.</p>
