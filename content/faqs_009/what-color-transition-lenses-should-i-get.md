<h2>What are Transition Lenses?</h2>

Transition lenses are lenses that automatically adjust to the level of light that is present. They are also known as photochromic lenses and are available in both prescription and non-prescription forms. When indoors, they are clear and block 100 percent of UV light. When outdoors, they darken to a light tint, blocking 100 percent of UV rays and blue light, and providing a comfortable level of darkness.

<h2>Types of Transition Lenses</h2>

Transition lenses come in several colors, each providing a different level of darkness when in the sun. The most common colors of transition lenses are gray, brown, and green. 

<h3>Gray Transition Lenses</h3>

Gray transition lenses are the most popular choice. They provide the most natural vision, reducing glare while maintaining the color of your surroundings. Gray lenses provide an even darkness across all wavelengths of light, which makes them the most versatile for different lighting conditions. 

<h3>Brown Transition Lenses</h3>

Brown transition lenses are great for outdoor activities such as driving, as they are highly effective in blocking out glare from the sun. They provide a more intense, contrast-enhancing darkening of the lens and are ideal for those who are sensitive to bright light. 

<h3>Green Transition Lenses</h3>

Green transition lenses are great for recreational activities such as golf and fishing, as they provide a more subtle darkening of the lens. They provide a slight contrast enhancement, while still allowing you to see natural colors.
