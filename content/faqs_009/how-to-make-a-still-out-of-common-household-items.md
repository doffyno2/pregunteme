<h2>What Is a Still?</h2>

<p>A still is a device used to distill liquids, such as water, alcohol, and other liquids. It is composed of a container that holds the liquid to be distilled, a heat source, and a condenser. The heat source boils the liquid, and the condenser collects the vapors and condenses them back into liquid form. This process is used to purify liquids and remove any impurities.</p>

<h2>What Do You Need to Make a Still?</h2>

<p>To make a still, you will need a few basic supplies. The most important part of the still is the container, which should be able to hold the liquid to be distilled. You will also need a heat source, such as a stove or open flame, and a condenser. The condenser can be made from a variety of materials, such as copper tubing or aluminum foil. You will also need tubing to connect the container to the condenser, and some sort of collection vessel to collect the distilled liquid.</p>

<h2>How to Make a Still Out of Common Household Items</h2>

<p>Making a still out of common household items is relatively easy. First, you’ll need to find a suitable container to hold the liquid to be distilled. A large pot or a bucket will work well for this. You’ll also need a heat source and a condenser. For the heat source, you can use a stove or an open flame. For the condenser, you can use a length of copper tubing or aluminum foil. You will also need some tubing to connect the container to the condenser, and a collection vessel to collect the distilled liquid.</p>

<p>Once you have all the supplies, you can begin building your still. Start by attaching the tubing to the container and the condenser. Make sure the tubing is securely attached. Next, place the container on the heat source, and turn on the heat. The liquid in the container will begin to boil, and the vapors will be collected in the condenser. The condenser will cool the vapors and turn them back into liquid form, which will then be collected in the collection vessel.</p>

<p>Once the liquid has been collected, turn off the heat and allow the still to cool. Once cooled, you can carefully remove the still from the heat source. You can now enjoy the distilled liquid, which should be free of impurities.</p>
