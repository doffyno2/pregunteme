<p>Black plastic bumpers are a common feature on many modern cars but unfortunately, they can be prone to scratches. Whether it’s from a careless driver in a parking lot, a low-hanging branch, or a minor collision, it’s not always easy to avoid damage to your vehicle’s bumper. Fortunately, there are a few different methods you can use to repair the scratches in your black plastic bumper and make it look as good as new.</p>

<h2>What You'll Need</h2>

<p>Before you begin, you should assemble all the necessary materials. You’ll need a plastic polish, a clean cloth, a scrubbing pad or brush, and a wax or sealant. Make sure you choose a plastic polish specifically designed for repairing scratches in black plastic bumpers.</p>

<h2>Cleaning the Bumper</h2>

<p>The first step is to clean the bumper. Start by wetting the cloth with warm water and gently rubbing it over the entire bumper to remove any dirt and grime. If there are any stubborn areas, you can use a mild detergent or scrubbing pad. Once the bumper is clean and dry, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Polishing the Bumper</h2>

<p>Once the bumper is clean, you can begin polishing it. Start by applying the plastic polish to the cloth and then buff it into the bumper in a circular motion. Work the polish into the scratches and rub it until the scratches have disappeared. When you’re done, use the cloth to wipe off any excess polish.</p>

<h2>Waxing or Sealing the Bumper</h2>

<p>Once you’re finished polishing the bumper, you should apply a wax or sealant to protect it from further damage. Start by applying the wax or sealant to the cloth and then buff it into the bumper in a circular motion. Make sure you cover the entire bumper, including the scratches. When you’re done, use the cloth to wipe off any excess wax or sealant.</p>
