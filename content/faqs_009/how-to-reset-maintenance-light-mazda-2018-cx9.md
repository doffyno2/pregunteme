<h2>What Causes The Maintenance Light To Come On?</h2>

<p>The maintenance light will come on when the vehicle's onboard computer detects that the engine oil has reached a certain level of wear or degradation. It's important to note that the maintenance light does not indicate a problem with the engine or any other part of the vehicle. It's simply a reminder that it's time for the vehicle to receive its regular scheduled maintenance.</p>

<p>The maintenance light may also come on if there is a problem with the vehicle's exhaust system or if the vehicle has not been driven for an extended period of time. If the maintenance light is flashing, it is likely indicating a more serious problem and the vehicle should be brought in for service.</p>

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light in a Mazda 2018 CX9 </h2>

<p>Resetting the maintenance light in a Mazda 2018 CX9 is fairly simple. First, turn the ignition key to the "on" position and wait for the maintenance light to come on. Once the maintenance light is illuminated, press and hold the "reset" button (located on the instrument panel) for three seconds. The light should then go off, indicating that the maintenance light has been successfully reset.</p>

<p>It's important to note that resetting the maintenance light does not actually reset the maintenance schedule, it simply turns off the reminder light. The maintenance schedule should still be followed as recommended by the manufacturer. If you have any further questions about resetting the maintenance light in a Mazda 2018 CX9, it's best to consult your owner's manual or a qualified Mazda technician.</p>
