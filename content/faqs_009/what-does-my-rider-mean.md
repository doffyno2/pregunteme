<p>For those who are new to the world of horse riding, the term “rider” can be a little confusing. A rider is someone who is in control of a horse during a ride. Riders are typically experienced riders, as the horse needs to trust and obey the rider in order for the ride to be successful.</p>

<h2>What Does a Rider Do?</h2>

<p>A rider’s job is to control the horse while riding. This includes guiding the horse through turns, keeping the horse at the correct speed, and keeping the horse on the correct path. Riders must be aware of the horse’s reactions to their commands, and be able to adjust their commands accordingly. They must also be aware of their own body language and the signals they are sending to the horse. Riders must also be aware of their surroundings and the terrain they are riding on, in order to ensure the horse’s safety.</p>

<p>Riders typically wear specialized clothing for safety and comfort. This includes a helmet, boots, gloves, and a riding jacket.
