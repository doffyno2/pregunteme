<h2>What Causes Swallowing Noises in Dogs?</h2>

Swallowing noises in dogs can be caused by several factors. The most common cause is an obstruction in the throat or airway. This can be due to a foreign object, a tumor, or an inflammation of the throat. Another potential cause is an infection, such as kennel cough or tracheobronchitis. These infections can cause inflammation and swelling of the throat, leading to difficulty swallowing as well as the noises associated with it. 

<h2>Are Swallowing Noises in Dogs Serious?</h2>

Swallowing noises in dogs can range from a minor nuisance to a symptom of a more serious underlying condition. If your dog is exhibiting signs of difficulty breathing or is having difficulty swallowing, you should take them to the vet as soon as possible. In some cases, the noises may be a sign of a more serious obstruction, such as an object lodged in the throat or a tumor on the throat. If this is the case, it is important to get your dog to the vet immediately.
