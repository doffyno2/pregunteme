<h2>Types of Scabs</h2>

<p>There are several types of scabs that can form on a dog's body. These include:</p>

<ul>
  <li><b>Dry scabs</b> – These scabs are usually caused by a skin condition or irritation. They can form anywhere on the body, but are most common on the face, around the eyes, and around the ears. Dry scabs can be painful and can cause the dog to scratch or lick excessively. </li>
  <li><b>Infected scabs</b> – These scabs are caused by a bacterial or fungal infection. They are usually red, swollen, and filled with pus. Infected scabs can be very painful and should be examined by a veterinarian. </li>
  <li><b>Allergic scabs</b> – These scabs are caused by an allergic reaction to something that the dog has come into contact with, such as pollen, dust, or food. Allergic scabs can be itchy and can cause the dog to scratch or lick excessively. </li>
  <li><b>Traumatic scabs</b> – These scabs are caused by an injury or wound. They can occur anywhere on the body, but are most common on the legs. Traumatic scabs can be very painful and can take several weeks or months to heal. </li>
</ul>
