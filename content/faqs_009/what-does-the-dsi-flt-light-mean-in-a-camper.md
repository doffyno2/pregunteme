When you're out camping in your camper, you may find that the dashboard lights indicate something is wrong. One of these lights is the DSI FLT light, which stands for Direct Spark Ignition Fault. Here, we'll take a look at what this light means and what you can do if it comes on.

<h2>What Does the DSI FLT Light Mean?</h2>

The DSI FLT light is a warning light that indicates there is a fault in the direct spark ignition system. It appears when there is a problem with the electronic ignition or fuel system. Since the direct spark ignition system is responsible for controlling the ignition and fuel system, it's essential that you get this issue resolved as quickly as possible.

<h2>What Causes the DSI FLT Light to Come On?</h2>

There are several potential causes for the DSI FLT light to come on. These include:

<h3>Faulty Wiring</h3>

If the wiring in your camper is faulty or damaged, it can cause the DSI FLT light to come on. Check the wiring in your camper to make sure it is in good condition and that all connections are secure.

<h3>Malfunctioning Components</h3>

If any of the components in the direct spark ignition system are malfunctioning, it can cause the DSI FLT light to come on. This could include the spark plugs, ignition coils, fuel injectors, or other components.

<h3>Low Fuel Pressure</h3>

If the fuel pressure in your camper is too low, it can cause the DSI FLT light to come on. To fix this issue, you will need to check the fuel pressure in your camper and make sure it is within the correct range.

<h2>What Should You Do If the DSI FLT Light Comes On?</h2>

If the DSI FLT light comes on in your camper, you should take the following steps:

<h3>Check the Wiring</h3>

The first step is to check the wiring in your camper. Make sure all connections are secure and that there are no signs of damage or corrosion.

<h3>Check the Components</h3>

Next, you should check all of the components in the direct spark ignition system. Make sure they are functioning properly and that there are no signs of wear or damage.

<h3>Check the Fuel Pressure</h3>

Finally, you should check the fuel pressure in your camper. Make sure it is within the correct range and that there are no signs of leaks or other issues.
