How to Reset Maintenance Light 2018 Honda CR-V Touring



The 2018 Honda CR-V Touring is a powerful, reliable and fuel-efficient SUV. It has been designed to provide an enjoyable driving experience, while also keeping maintenance costs low. However, like most vehicles, it is important to stay on top of regular maintenance and repairs in order to keep it running in top condition. One way to do this is to pay attention to the maintenance light on the dashboard.

<h2>What is the Maintenance Light?</h2>

The maintenance light on any vehicle is a light that comes on when the vehicle needs regular maintenance and/or repairs. On the 2018 Honda CR-V Touring, the maintenance light is located on the dashboard, just to the left of the steering wheel. It is typically a yellow, amber or orange light, and will illuminate when the vehicle needs to be serviced.

<h2>What Does the Maintenance Light Mean?</h2>

When the maintenance light on the 2018 Honda CR-V Touring is illuminated, it means that the vehicle is due for regular maintenance. This could include oil changes, filter changes, tire rotations, brake checks, and other routine maintenance. It is important to have these services performed as soon as possible, as they can help extend the life of the vehicle and ensure it runs as efficiently and reliably as possible.

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light on the 2018 Honda CR-V Touring</h2>

Once the maintenance services have been completed on the 2018 Honda CR-V Touring, it is important to reset the maintenance light. This can be done by following a few simple steps:

<h3>Step 1: Locate the Maintenance Light</h3>

The first step is to locate the maintenance light on the dashboard. It is typically a yellow, amber or orange light located just to the left of the steering wheel.

<h3>Step 2: Turn the Ignition Key</h3>

The next step is to turn the ignition key to the “On” position and wait for the maintenance light to turn on. Once it is illuminated, turn the key back to the “Off” position.

<h3>Step 3: Press and Hold the Trip Odometer Button</h3>

Once the ignition key is in the “Off” position, press and hold the trip odometer button. While pressing and holding the button, turn the ignition key back to the “On” position. Continue to hold the button until the maintenance light begins to blink.

<h3>Step 4: Release the Trip Odometer Button</h3>

Once the maintenance light begins to blink, release the trip odometer button and turn the ignition key back to the “Off” position. The maintenance light should now be reset and will not illuminate until the vehicle needs to be serviced again.
