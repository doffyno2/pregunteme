<p>If you own a 2017 Nissan Altima, you may need to reset the maintenance light on the dashboard. The maintenance light is designed to remind you when it's time to take your Altima in for routine service, such as oil changes and tire rotations. Resetting the light is easy and only takes a few minutes. Here’s how to do it.</p>

<h2>Step 1: Locate the Maintenance Light on Your Dashboard</h2>

<p>The maintenance light on a 2017 Nissan Altima is located on the dashboard, near the speedometer. It looks like a small yellow light and is usually labeled “MAINT REQD” or “MAINTENANCE REQUIRED.”</p>

<h2>Step 2: Turn on the Ignition</h2>

<p>Once you’ve located the maintenance light, turn the ignition on. Do not start the engine. You should see the light illuminate on the dashboard.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Push and Hold the Trip Odometer Reset Button</h2>

<p>Push and hold the trip odometer reset button for at least five seconds. This button is located near the speedometer, on the left side of the dashboard. When you press the button, you should hear a beep. Release the button after five seconds.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Release the Trip Odometer Reset Button</h2>

<p>When you release the trip odometer reset button, the maintenance light should turn off. This indicates that the maintenance light has been reset and you can now start the engine.</p>
