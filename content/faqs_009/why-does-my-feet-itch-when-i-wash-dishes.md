This can be extremely uncomfortable, and it can make the task of washing dishes even more unpleasant. Fortunately, there are ways to identify the cause of the itching and find relief. 

<h2>What Causes the Itching?</h2>

The most common cause of itching in the feet while washing dishes is contact dermatitis. Contact dermatitis is a type of skin irritation that is caused by coming into contact with a certain substance. Common allergens that can cause contact dermatitis include detergents, soaps, and cleaning agents. These allergens can irritate the skin on the feet, leading to itching and other symptoms. 

It is also possible for the feet to become itchy due to an infection. Fungal infections, such as athlete’s foot, can cause itching and burning. Bacterial infections can also cause itching in the feet.

<h2>What Are The Symptoms of Contact Dermatitis?</h2>

The most common symptom of contact dermatitis is itching. The skin may also become red and swollen. Blisters may form, and the skin may become dry and scaly. In severe cases, the skin may develop an oozing discharge. 

<h2>How Is Contact Dermatitis Diagnosed?</h2>

Contact dermatitis is usually diagnosed based on the patient’s symptoms and a physical examination. A patch test may also be used to identify the cause of the itching. During a patch test, small amounts of various allergens are applied to the skin, and the patient is monitored for any reactions. 

<h2>How Is Contact Dermatitis Treated?</h2>

The treatment of contact dermatitis depends on the cause of the itching. If an allergen is identified, then avoiding contact with the substance should help to relieve the symptoms. If the itching is caused by an infection, then antifungal or antibacterial medications may be prescribed. 

<h2>Can Contact Dermatitis Be Prevented?</h2>

The best way to prevent contact dermatitis is to avoid contact with any known allergens. Wearing gloves when washing dishes can help to protect the skin from coming into contact with irritants. It is also important to keep the feet clean and dry, as moisture can make the skin more vulnerable to irritation.
