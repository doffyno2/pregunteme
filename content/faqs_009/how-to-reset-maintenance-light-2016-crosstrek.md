<p>Are you trying to reset the Maintenance Light on your 2016 Subaru Crosstrek? If so, then you’ve come to the right place! We’ll walk you through the steps you need to take in order to reset the Maintenance Light and get your Crosstrek back in proper running order.</p>

<h2>What is a Maintenance Light?</h2>

<p>A Maintenance Light is a warning light on your dashboard that lets you know that it’s time to have your vehicle’s oil changed, brakes checked, and other routine maintenance tasks performed. It is important to keep up with these tasks, as they help keep your vehicle in top condition and running smoothly.</p>

<h2>How to Reset Maintenance Light on a 2016 Subaru Crosstrek</h2>

<p>Resetting the Maintenance Light on your 2016 Subaru Crosstrek is easy, and only requires a few steps. Here’s what you need to do:</p>

<h3>Step 1: Turn On the Ignition</h3>

<p>The first step is to turn on the ignition. Make sure to press the “Start” button for two seconds, but do not start the engine.</p>

<h3>Step 2: Access the Maintenance Light Menu</h3>

<p>Next, you need to access the Maintenance Light menu. To do this, press and hold the “Select” button on your dashboard until the Maintenance Light menu appears.</p>

<h3>Step 3: Select “Reset”</h3>

<p>Once the menu appears, select the “Reset” option. This will reset the Maintenance Light on your 2016 Subaru Crosstrek.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)</h2>

<h3>Q: How often should I reset the Maintenance Light?</h3>

<p>A: It is recommended that you reset the Maintenance Light every 6 months or 5,000 miles, whichever comes first.</p>

<h3>Q: What happens if I don’t reset the Maintenance Light?</h3>

<p>A: If you don’t reset the Maintenance Light, the warning light will remain illuminated on your dashboard. This is a reminder that it’s time to have your vehicle’s oil changed, brakes checked, and other routine maintenance tasks performed.</p>

<h3>Q: What other maintenance tasks should I do?</h3>

<p>A: In addition to changing your oil and checking your brakes, it is also important to have your vehicle’s tires rotated, check your fluid levels, and inspect your air filter. These tasks should be done every 6 months or 5,000 miles, whichever comes first.</p>

<h3>Q: How do I know when I need to reset the Maintenance Light?</h3>

<p>A: The Maintenance Light will illuminate on your dashboard to indicate that it’s time to reset it. If the light does not turn off after resetting, then it’s time to have your vehicle serviced.</p>

<h3>Q: Is it easy to reset the Maintenance Light?</h3>

<p>A: Yes, it is easy to reset the Maintenance Light on a 2016 Subaru Crosstrek. All you need to do is follow the steps outlined above.</p>

<h3>Q: What other maintenance tasks should I be aware of?</h3>

<p>A: Aside from changing your oil and checking your brakes, it is also important to check your spark plugs, inspect your battery, and check your air filter. These tasks should be done every 12 months or 10,000 miles, whichever comes first.</p>

<h3>Q: Is it important to keep up with routine maintenance?</h3>

<p>A: Yes, it is very important to keep up with routine maintenance on your vehicle. Regular maintenance will help keep your vehicle running smoothly and help prevent costly repairs in the future.</p>
