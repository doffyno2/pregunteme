<h2> Introduction </h2>
Rust can be a major problem for the rotor hub of a vehicle, as it can cause major damage to the hub and lower the life of the vehicle. Rust can also cause damage to the brake discs, making them unreliable and unsafe. Fortunately, there are several methods and products that can be used to safely and effectively remove rust from rotor hubs and help restore them to their original condition.

<h2> Causes of Rust on Rotor Hubs </h2>
Rotor hubs are prone to rust due to their close proximity to the road. Road salt, sand, and other debris thrown up from the road can cause the rotor hub to corrode and cause rust. In addition, the rotor hub is in direct contact with the brake disc, which can also cause corrosion due to the high heat generated during braking.

<h2> Removing Rust from Rotor Hubs </h2>
The first step in removing rust from the rotor hub is to clean the area around the rust. This can be done with a wire brush, sandpaper, or an abrasive pad. Once the area is clean, use a rust remover product to remove the rust. This can be done with a spray-on product or a brush-on product. Once the rust has been removed, use a rust converter product to help protect the area from further rusting. 

<h2> Prevention of Rust on Rotor Hubs </h2>
Once the rotor hub is free of rust, it is important to take steps to prevent it from rusting again. This can be done by coating the rotor hub with a rust prevention product, such as an oil-based product or a wax-based product. These products will help to protect the rotor hub from further corrosion and rusting.
