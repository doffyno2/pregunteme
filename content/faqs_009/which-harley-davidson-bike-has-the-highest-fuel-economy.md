<h2>Factors that Affect Fuel Economy on Harley Davidson Bikes</h2>

<p>The fuel economy of a Harley Davidson bike is impacted by a variety of factors. These include engine size, the type of bike, the rider's weight, the type of terrain the bike is ridden on, and the type of riding. Let's take a closer look at each of these factors.</p>

<h3>Engine Size</h3>

<p>The engine size of a Harley Davidson bike will have a major impact on its fuel economy. Generally speaking, larger engines require more fuel to operate, resulting in lower fuel economy. On the other hand, smaller engines are more efficient and will get better fuel economy. </p>

<h3>Type of Bike</h3>

<p>The type of bike you are riding will also have an impact on its fuel economy. For example, touring bikes are heavier and require more fuel to operate than lightweight cruisers. Similarly, sport bikes are designed for speed, and as such they require more fuel than bikes designed for touring.</p>

<h3>Rider's Weight</h3>

<p>The rider's weight will also have an impact on fuel economy. Heavier riders will require more fuel to move their bike than lighter riders. This is especially true for light-weight cruisers.</p>

<h3>Type of Terrain</h3>

<p>The type of terrain the bike is ridden on will also have an impact on fuel economy. For example, riding on flat terrain will require less fuel than riding on hilly terrain. Similarly, riding on rough terrain will require more fuel than riding on smooth roads.</p>

<h3>Type of Riding</h3>

<p>Finally, the type of riding you do will also affect fuel economy. Touring bikes are designed for long-distance riding and require more fuel than bikes designed for short urban trips. Similarly, sport bikes are designed for speed and require more fuel than bikes designed for city riding.</p>
