When you apply for a new passport, you must provide a recent passport-style photograph of yourself. To ensure that the photograph remains attached to the application form, it must be stapled. This article explains how to correctly staple a photograph to a passport application.

<h2>What is Needed to Staple a Picture to Passport Application?</h2>

To staple a photograph to a passport application form, you will need a stapler and a pack of staples. You will also need a ruler and a pair of scissors.

<h2>Steps to Staple a Picture to Passport Application</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Measure and Cut the Photograph</h3>

Using a ruler, measure the photograph against the box marked ‘Photograph’ on the passport application form. Cut the photograph so that it fits within the box.

<h3>Step 2: Place the Photograph in the Box</h3>

Place the photograph in the box marked ‘Photograph’ on the passport application form. Make sure that the photograph is straight and that it is securely in the box.

<h3>Step 3: Place the Staples on the Photograph</h3>

Using the stapler, place two staples on the four corners of the photograph. Make sure to place the staples on the corners of the photograph and not on the edges.

<h3>Step 4: Secure the Photograph to the Application</h3>

Using the stapler, secure the photograph to the passport application form. Make sure that the staples are placed properly and that they are securely in the form.
