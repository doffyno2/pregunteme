<h2>What is Tap Water?</h2>

<p>Tap water is the water that comes out of your faucet and is usually treated with chlorine and other chemicals in order to make it safe for human consumption. It is not always safe for fish, however, as some of the chemicals can be harmful to their health. Therefore, it is important to be aware of the composition of your tap water and how it will affect the fish that you choose.</p>

<h2>Which Fish Can Live in Tap Water?</h2>

<p>The type of fish that can live in tap water will depend on the composition of your water. Generally speaking, it is best to choose fish that are native to the area as they are more likely to be adapted to the water conditions. Some of the most common fish that can live in tap water include goldfish, betta fish, guppies, and platies. These fish are all quite hardy and can handle the conditions of a regular aquarium with tap water.</p>
