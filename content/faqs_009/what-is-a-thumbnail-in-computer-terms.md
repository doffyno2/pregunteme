A thumbnail is a small, reduced-size version of a picture or other graphic file used to help identify and organize digital images. A thumbnail is typically a low-resolution version of a larger image, used to distinguish between two or more images. They are most commonly used to help identify and organize digital photos from a collection and are also used in online photo sharing sites, such as Flickr and Instagram.

<h2>What is the Purpose of Thumbnails?</h2>

The main purpose of thumbnails is to help users quickly identify and organize digital images. Thumbnails can be used to quickly identify and select a specific image from a larger collection. For example, when viewing a large set of digital photos, a thumbnail of each image can be used to quickly identify the desired photo without having to scroll through a long list of images.

Thumbnails are also used in online photo sharing applications such as Flickr and Instagram. When viewing a collection of photos online, thumbnails of the images are displayed to help users quickly identify the desired photo.
