<h2>What Is Water Pollution?</h2>

Water pollution is the contamination of fresh or salty water by any means. It can be caused by chemical, physical, or biological processes. Pollution can have a wide range of effects on the environment, from affecting the quality of drinking water to altering the composition of aquatic ecosystems. 

<h2>What Causes Water Pollution?</h2>

Water pollution can be caused by a variety of factors. These include industrial activities, agricultural runoff, and sewage discharge. Industrial activities can introduce pollutants such as heavy metals, oil and grease, and other toxic chemicals into water sources. Agricultural runoff can carry fertilizers, pesticides, and other harmful chemicals into water systems. Sewage discharge can introduce bacteria, viruses, and other microorganisms into water sources.
