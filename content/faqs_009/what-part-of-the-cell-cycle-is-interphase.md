Interphase is the first phase of the cell cycle, where a cell prepares to divide into two daughter cells. During interphase, the cell grows, replicates its DNA, and prepares for cell division. Interphase is the longest phase of the cell cycle and consists of three distinct stages: G1 phase, S phase, and G2 phase. 

<h2>G1 Phase</h2>

G1 phase is the first stage of interphase. During G1 phase, the cell grows in size and synthesizes proteins, organelles, and other cellular components. During this phase, the cell also produces enzymes and other molecules that are necessary for DNA replication. 

<h2>S Phase</h2>

S phase is the second stage of interphase. During S phase, the cell replicates its DNA in preparation for cell division. During S phase, the cell's DNA is copied, and each strand of DNA is separated into two identical strands. This process is known as DNA replication. 

<h2>G2 Phase</h2>

G2 phase is the third and final stage of interphase. During G2 phase, the cell synthesizes proteins and other molecules that are needed for cell division. G2 phase also provides the cell with a final opportunity to repair any errors that may have occurred during DNA replication. 

<h2>Conclusion</h2>

Interphase is the first stage of the cell cycle and is composed of three distinct stages: G1 phase, S phase, and G2 phase. Interphase is the longest phase of the cell cycle and is essential for the successful division of the cell.
