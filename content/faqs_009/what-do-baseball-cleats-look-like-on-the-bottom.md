<h2>Types of Baseball Cleats</h2>

The first step in understanding what baseball cleats look like on the bottom is to understand the different types of cleats available. Generally speaking, there are two main types of cleats: metal cleats and rubber cleats. 

Metal cleats are usually made of either aluminum or steel and have a flat, spike-like design. Metal cleats are designed to provide maximum grip and stability on the field, and they are the preferred choice for serious players. The downside is that metal cleats can be uncomfortable and can sometimes cause injuries.

Rubber cleats are usually made of a synthetic material like PVC or rubber and have a ridged, stud-like design. Rubber cleats provide less grip than metal cleats, but they are more comfortable and less likely to cause injury. Rubber cleats are the preferred choice for beginner players.
