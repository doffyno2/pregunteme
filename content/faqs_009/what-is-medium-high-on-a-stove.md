<h2>What is Medium High on a Stove?</h2>

<p>Medium-high on a stove is a temperature setting that falls between medium and high. It is usually a slightly higher temperature than medium and is designed to provide a faster cooking time. On some stoves, the medium-high setting is indicated by a symbol such as a flame or a number 3. On other stoves, the temperature is indicated by a specific temperature setting such as 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius).</p>

<p>When cooking on medium-high, the heat should be hot enough to cook foods quickly, but not so hot that it will burn the food. Many recipes call for medium-high heat, so it is important to understand how this setting works.</p>

<h2>What Should You Cook On Medium-High Heat?</h2>

<p>Medium-high heat is best for cooking foods that need to be cooked quickly, such as meats and vegetables. It is also ideal for stir-frying and sautéing. Foods that need to be cooked slowly, such as soups and stews, should be cooked on a lower heat setting such as medium.</p>

<p>When using medium-high heat, it is important to watch the food closely and stir or flip it often to prevent it from burning. Foods that are cooked on the medium-high setting should be cooked for a shorter period of time than foods cooked on lower settings.</p>
