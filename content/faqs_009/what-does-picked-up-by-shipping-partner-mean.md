<h2>What Does Picked Up By Shipping Partner Mean?</h2>

<p>When a shipment is “picked up by shipping partner,” it means that the shipping company has been contracted to transport the package from its current location to its final destination. The shipping partner is responsible for picking up the package from its origin, loading it onto their vehicle, and delivering it to its destination. </p>

<p>When a package is picked up by a shipping partner, it is important to note that the shipping company is not necessarily the same company that will be delivering the package. For example, if a package is picked up by UPS, but then delivered by USPS, the UPS is the shipping partner and USPS is the delivery partner. </p>

<h2>How Does It Work?</h2>

<p>When a package is picked up by a shipping partner, they will typically send a driver to the package’s origin. The driver will collect the package and load it onto their vehicle. The driver may also collect additional information, such as the recipient’s name and address. </p>

<p>Once the package is loaded onto the vehicle, the driver will transport it to its destination. Depending on the shipping company, the package may be delivered directly to the recipient or it may be dropped off at a shipping facility. In some cases, the package may be transferred to a different shipping company for final delivery. </p>
