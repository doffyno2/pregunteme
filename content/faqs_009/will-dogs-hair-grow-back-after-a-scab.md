<h2>Why Do Dogs Get Scabs?</h2>

<p>Scabs on dogs can be caused by a variety of things, including insect bites, skin irritations, and infections. In some cases, the scabs may be the result of an underlying medical condition, such as allergies, skin cancer, or even mange. In these cases, the scabs may be a sign of a more serious problem and should be investigated further by a qualified veterinarian.</p>

<p>In some cases, however, the scabs may be caused by something more mundane, such as rough handling or excessive scratching. For example, if your dog is constantly digging in the dirt, the dirt may get stuck in their fur and irritate their skin, causing them to scratch and develop scabs. In these cases, the problem can usually be solved by providing your dog with ample exercise and providing a cleaner environment for them to play in.</p>

<h2>Signs of Scabbing on Dogs</h2>

<p>If your dog is developing scabs, you'll likely notice the following signs:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Raised bumps on the skin</li>
  <li>Redness or discoloration around the bumps</li>
  <li>Itchy or dry skin</li>
  <li>Hair loss around the scabbed area</li>
  <li>An unpleasant smell emanating from the scabbed area</li>
</ul>

<p>In some cases, the scabbed area may become infected, which can lead to further complications. If you notice any of the above signs of scabbing, contact your veterinarian right away to have the area checked out.</p>

<p>The best way to help your dog's hair grow back after scabbing is to keep the area clean and moisturized.
