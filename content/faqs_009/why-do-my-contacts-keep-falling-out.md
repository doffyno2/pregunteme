<h2>What Causes Contacts to Fall Out?</h2>

<p>There are several causes of contacts falling out, and it’s important to be aware of them. The most common causes include:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Poorly Fitting Contacts – Contacts that don’t fit properly are more likely to fall out. If your contacts don’t fit correctly, they may not be able to stay in place.</li>
  <li>Tearing of the Contacts – If your contacts are torn, they will be more likely to come out. Contacts can tear if they’re not handled properly or if they’ve been worn too long.</li>
  <li>Dry Eyes – Dry eyes can cause your contacts to become loose, making them more likely to fall out.</li>
  <li>Inadequate Cleaning – If you don’t clean your contacts properly, they may become loose and more likely to fall out.</li>
  <li>Inadequate Lubrication – If you don’t use enough lubrication on your contacts, they may become loose and more likely to fall out.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What Can You Do To Prevent Contacts from Falling Out?</h2>

  <li>Clean and Replace Your Contacts Regularly – Make sure to clean your contacts regularly and replace them as needed. This will help keep your contacts in good condition and help prevent them from falling out.</li>
  <li>Use Enough Lubrication – Make sure to use enough lubrication when putting in and taking out your contacts. This will help keep them in place and reduce the risk of them falling out.</li>
  <li>Avoid Rubbing Your Eyes – Rubbing your eyes can cause your contacts to become loose and more likely to fall out.</li>
</ul>
