<h2>What is Guar Gum?</h2>

Guar gum is a natural polysaccharide derived from the guar plant, which is native to India and Pakistan. It is a white powdery substance that is used as a thickening and stabilizing agent in food processing. Guar gum is often used as a vegan alternative to gelatin in products such as ice cream, yogurt, and puddings. It is also used in products such as processed meats, bakery items, and dairy products.

<h2>Is Guar Gum Toxic to Dogs?</h2>

Guar gum is not toxic to dogs, but it can be dangerous if ingested in large amounts. Dogs can have difficulty digesting large amounts of guar gum, and it can cause a number of digestive issues, including vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. Additionally, guar gum can form an indigestible mass in the stomach, leading to an obstruction.
