<h2>What is Prednisone?</h2>

<p>Prednisone is a man-made steroid medication that mimics cortisol, a hormone naturally produced in the body. It is used to reduce inflammation and swelling, and to treat various medical conditions in dogs. Prednisone can also be used as an immunosuppressant drug to reduce the activity of the immune system and to treat autoimmune disorders.</p>

<h2>Risks of Prednisone</h2>

<p>While prednisone can be an effective treatment for many medical conditions in dogs, it can also cause serious side effects. Common side effects of prednisone can include increased thirst and urination, increased appetite, panting, vomiting, and diarrhea. Long-term use of prednisone can also lead to weight gain, muscle wasting, and a weakened immune system.</p>

<p>In addition, prednisone can cause serious complications such as pancreatitis, liver disease, and Cushing's disease. It is important to discuss the potential risks and benefits of prednisone with your veterinarian before starting your dog on the drug.</p>
