The Enhanced Drivers License (EDL) is a special type of driver’s license that allows Texas residents to enter the United States from Canada, Mexico, the Caribbean and Bermuda at land and sea ports of entry without the need for a passport or other documents. An Enhanced Drivers License is perfect for travelers who do not have a passport but need to travel frequently to any of these locations. 

<h2>What are the Requirements for an Enhanced Drivers License Texas?</h2>

In order to obtain an Enhanced Drivers License, you must meet the following requirements: 

<ul>
  <li>Be a United States citizen</li>
  <li>Be a Texas resident</li>
  <li>Present proof of identity and residency in Texas</li>
  <li>Pass a vision test</li>
  <li>Pass the knowledge portion of the driver's license exam</li>
  <li>Present proof of your legal presence in the United States (if applicable)</li>
  <li>Pay the applicable fee</li>
</ul>

<h2>What Documents do I need to Prepare for an Enhanced Drivers License Texas?</h2>

To apply for an Enhanced Drivers License, you must present the following documents: 

<ul>
  <li>Proof of identity (birth certificate, passport, etc.)</li>
  <li>Proof of residency in Texas (utility bill, lease agreement, etc.)</li>
  <li>Social Security Card</li>
  <li>Proof of legal presence in the United States (if applicable)</li>
  <li>A completed and signed application form</li>
</ul>
