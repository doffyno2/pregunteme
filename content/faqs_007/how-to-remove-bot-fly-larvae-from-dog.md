<h2>What are Bot Flies?</h2>

<p>Bot flies are a type of fly that belongs to the family of Oestridae. They are found in warmer climates, such as in the United States and Canada, and lay eggs on the skin of mammals, including dogs. The larvae hatch and feed on the animal's tissue, causing irritation and potentially leading to infection if left untreated.</p>

<h2>Signs of Bot Fly Infestation in Dogs</h2>

<p>One of the first signs of a bot fly infestation in dogs is small, raised bumps on the skin. These bumps are caused by the larvae burrowing into the skin. If left untreated, the larvae can cause an infection and even death.</p>

<h2>Removing the Larvae from Dogs</h2>

<p>There are a few methods for removing bot fly larvae from dogs. The first is to apply a thick layer of petroleum jelly to the affected area. The jelly will suffocate the larvae, causing them to come to the surface of the skin where they can be removed. Alternatively, a topical insecticide can be applied to the affected area. This will kill the larvae and should be used only after consulting with a veterinarian.</p>
