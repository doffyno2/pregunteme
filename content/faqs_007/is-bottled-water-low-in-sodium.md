<h2>What is Sodium?</h2>

<p>Sodium is an essential mineral that helps regulate blood pressure and fluid balance in the body. Too much sodium can lead to high blood pressure, which can increase the risk of stroke, heart attack, and other health problems. The American Heart Association recommends that people consume no more than 2,300 milligrams of sodium per day. </p>

<h2>How Much Sodium Is in Bottled Water?</h2>

<p>Most bottled waters contain very low levels of sodium, usually less than 10 milligrams per liter. This is significantly lower than the maximum daily recommended intake of 2,300 milligrams per day. Some brands of bottled water contain even lower levels of sodium, such as purified waters that are labeled “sodium free” or “zero sodium.”</p>

<h2>Are All Types of Bottled Water Low in Sodium?</h2>

<p>Not all types of bottled water are low in sodium. Mineral waters, for example, may contain naturally occurring levels of sodium that are higher than other types of bottled water. Additionally, some flavored waters may contain added sodium or salt, so it is important to read the label and check the sodium content before consuming. </p>
