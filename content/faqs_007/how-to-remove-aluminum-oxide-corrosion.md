<h2>What is Aluminum Oxide Corrosion?</h2>

Aluminum oxide corrosion is a type of corrosion that occurs when aluminum is exposed to water or other liquids. When the aluminum is exposed to the liquids, the oxygen present in the liquid combines with the aluminum to form a thin, invisible layer of aluminum oxide on the surface of the metal. This layer of aluminum oxide is very hard and durable, and it forms a protective barrier against further corrosion.
