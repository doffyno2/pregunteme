What Season Is September In USA?


September in the United States is a transition month, as summer and fall mix together to produce the first signs of autumn. Depending on the region, temperatures can vary significantly, as can precipitation levels, making September a unique month in terms of climate.

<h2>Climate in the Northern Hemisphere</h2>
In the northern part of the United States, September is a month of transition as summer slowly gives way to fall. Temperatures start to cool off, but they are still relatively mild compared to the colder months in winter. The average high temperature in September is typically in the low 70s Fahrenheit, while the average low is in the mid-50s.

In the northern United States, September is typically the second-driest month of the year. Rainfall is usually only about two inches, with snowfall only occurring in the highest elevations of the Rockies and other mountain ranges.

<h2>Climate in the Southern Hemisphere</h2>
In the southern part of the United States, September is usually the hottest month of the year. Average temperatures typically reach the low 90s during the day, with nighttime lows around the mid-70s. This makes September the ideal time of year for enjoying outdoor activities in the south.

Rainfall is also higher in September in the south, with an average of around four inches. Thunderstorms are common in the south during this time of year, so it's important to be prepared for sudden weather changes.
