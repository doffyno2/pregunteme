<h2>What Is Gatorade?</h2>

Gatorade is a sports drink that was first developed in 1965 by a team of scientists at the University of Florida. It was designed to replenish the electrolytes, such as sodium and potassium, that athletes lose through sweat. It is now manufactured and sold by PepsiCo worldwide. Gatorade comes in a variety of flavors, including lemon-lime, fruit punch, orange, and grape, and is commonly used to rehydrate during and after exercise as well as to help replace lost electrolytes.
