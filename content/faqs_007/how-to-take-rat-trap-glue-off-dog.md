<h2>What Is Rat Trap Glue?</h2>
Rat trap glue is a sticky adhesive used to catch and kill rats. It is typically made from a combination of rubber and adhesive, so it is incredibly sticky and difficult to remove. The glue is usually placed on a board or other flat surface and is meant to capture rats as they walk across it. Unfortunately, it can also be hazardous to other animals, including dogs, if they walk across it or come into contact with it.

<h2>What Is the Risk of Rat Trap Glue On Dogs?</h2>
The risk of rat trap glue on dogs is that it can cause skin irritation, hair loss, and even infection if the glue is left on the dog’s skin for too long. If the glue gets in the dog’s eyes or is ingested, it can also cause serious health problems. It is important to take prompt, effective action if your dog comes into contact with rat trap glue.

<h2>How To Remove Rat Trap Glue From a Dog</h2>
If your dog has come into contact with rat trap glue, there are a few steps you can take to safely and effectively remove it from your dog’s skin and fur.

<h3>Step 1: Gently Peel Off Any Visible Glue</h3>

<h3>Step 2: Use Mineral Oil or Baby Oil to Soften the Glue</h3>
Once any visible glue has been removed, use mineral oil or baby oil to soften any remaining glue on your dog’s skin and fur. Apply the oil directly onto the glue and leave it on for a few minutes. This will help to loosen the glue and make it easier to remove.

<h3>Step 3: Rinse the Area With Warm Water</h3>
Once the glue has been softened, rinse the area with warm water. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to remove any remaining oil and glue from your dog’s skin and fur.

<h3>Step 4: Apply a Gentle Shampoo or Soap</h3>
Once the area has been rinsed, apply a gentle shampoo or soap to the area. This will help to remove any remaining glue or oil. Be sure to rinse thoroughly after applying the shampoo or soap.

<h3>Step 5: Check for Irritation or Infection</h3>
