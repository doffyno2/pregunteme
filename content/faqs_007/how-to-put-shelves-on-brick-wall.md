<p>Putting shelves on a brick wall can be a great way to add decorative storage space to any room. It is also a relatively easy DIY project that can be completed without the help of a professional. With the right tools and supplies, you can create your own custom shelving system that looks great and is also practical. In this article, we will discuss how to put shelves on a brick wall, what supplies you will need, and some tips and tricks to make the job easier.</p>

<h2>What You Will Need</h2>

<p>Before you begin the project, you will need to gather the necessary tools and supplies. These include:</p>

<ul>
<li>Hammer</li>
<li>Masonry nails</li>
<li>Masonry drill bit</li>
<li>Level</li>
<li>Screws</li>
<li>Shelves</li>
<li>Stud finder</li>
</ul>

<h2>Preparation</h2>

<p>Before you begin the project, you should use a stud finder to locate the studs in the wall. This will help you determine where to place the shelves. You should also measure the wall to determine the size of the shelves that you will need. Once you have the measurements, you can purchase the shelves that you need for the project.</p>

<h2>Drilling the Holes</h2>

<p>Once you have the shelves and the necessary supplies, you can begin drilling the holes for the masonry nails. You should use a masonry drill bit that is slightly larger than the nails that you have purchased. This will ensure that the nails will fit into the holes without any difficulty. It is important to use a level to ensure that the holes are drilled in a straight line. This will help ensure that the shelves are properly secured to the wall.</p>

<h2>Attaching the Shelves</h2>

<p>Once the holes have been drilled, you can begin attaching the shelves to the wall. You should use masonry nails to secure the shelves to the wall. It is important to make sure that the nails are inserted into the holes at a slight angle. This will help ensure that the shelves are properly secured to the wall.</p>

<p>You should also use screws to secure the shelves to the wall. This will help ensure that the shelves are firmly attached to the wall. It is important to use screws that are the same size as the masonry nails. This will help ensure that the shelves are properly secured to the wall.</p>
