Health codes are essential regulations that protect the health, safety, and welfare of people. They apply to a wide range of businesses and organizations, from restaurants to hospitals, and are designed to protect everyone from harm. Health codes are created and enforced by local and state governments, and they cover a broad range of topics, from food handling to hazardous materials.

<h2>Overview of Health Codes</h2>

Health codes are regulations that are designed to protect people from harm. They are created and enforced by governments, typically by local and state governments. Health codes are applicable to a wide range of businesses and organizations, from restaurants to hospitals. Health codes are typically divided into sections, and each section covers a different topic, such as food handling or hazardous materials. Health codes are generally enforced by health inspectors, who visit businesses to make sure that they are in compliance with the regulations.

<h2>Food Handling</h2>

Food handling is a major component of health codes. These regulations are designed to ensure that food is safe for consumption. For example, health codes may require that food be cooked to a certain temperature, or that food workers wear gloves when handling food. Health codes may also require that food be stored at certain temperatures, or that food be labeled with expiration dates. 

<h2>Hazardous Materials</h2>

Health codes may also contain regulations regarding hazardous materials. These regulations are designed to protect people from exposure to dangerous substances. For example, health codes may require that businesses store hazardous materials in a safe manner, or that employees wear protective clothing when handling hazardous materials. Health codes may also require that businesses dispose of hazardous materials in a safe manner.

<h2>Infectious Diseases</h2>

Health codes may also include regulations regarding infectious diseases. These regulations are designed to protect people from the spread of infectious diseases. For example, health codes may require that businesses screen their employees for certain infectious diseases, or that businesses take certain precautions when handling certain infectious diseases. 

<h2>Safety Regulations</h2>

Health codes may also contain safety regulations. These regulations are designed to protect people from harm. For example, health codes may require that businesses have certain safety features, such as fire extinguishers or emergency exits. Health codes may also require that businesses take certain precautions, such as providing employees with safety equipment. 

<h2>Sanitation Regulations</h2>

Health codes may also contain sanitation regulations. These regulations are designed to protect people from illness or disease. For example, health codes may require that businesses maintain a certain level of cleanliness, or that businesses dispose of waste in a safe manner.
