<p>A spur is a prominent geographic feature on a map, often appearing as a narrow strip of land that juts out from the main body of a landmass. The term is derived from the Latin word spurs, which means “a sharp point.” On maps, spurs are typically marked as a series of points that extend outward from the main area of land. In some cases, they can be seen stretching across an entire continent.</p>

<p>Spurs are most commonly found in mountainous regions. They are created when two streams of water or two fault lines run in opposite directions. This causes the land to be pushed up in the middle, creating the spur. This feature is often seen in mountain ranges, such as the Alps and the Rocky Mountains.</p> 

<p>Spurs can also be found in deserts, where they form long ridges.
