<p>The cell cycle is a sequence of events that cells go through to divide and reproduce. It is divided into four phases: G1, S, G2, and M. The G2 phase is the third of the four stages of the cell cycle and is the period during which the cell prepares for mitosis. During G2, the cell begins to synthesize proteins and organelles, repair any damaged DNA, and replicate its centrioles.</p>

<h2>What Occurs During G2 Phase?</h2>

<p>During the G2 phase, the cell prepares for mitosis. During this period, the cell begins to synthesize proteins and organelles, repair any damaged DNA, and replicate its centrioles. The cell also undergoes a series of metabolic processes such as glycolysis, the Krebs cycle, and electron transport chain. This allows the cell to produce energy needed for the process of mitosis. Additionally, the cell's chromosomes are duplicated and the cell's spindle fibers are formed.</p>

<h2>What Is the Duration of G2 Phase?</h2>

<p>The duration of the G2 phase varies depending on the type of cell. In most cells, the G2 phase lasts anywhere from 2-6 hours. In cancer cells, however, the G2 phase can be very short, lasting as little as 10 minutes. This is because cancer cells have a much higher rate of cell division and can rapidly move through the cell cycle.</p>
