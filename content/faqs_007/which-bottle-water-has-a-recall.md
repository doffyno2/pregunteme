<p>Bottled water has become an increasingly popular choice for people looking to stay hydrated on the go. It’s convenient, affordable, and can be found in virtually any grocery store, gas station, or convenience store. However, there have been several bottled water recalls over the past few years, leaving many consumers confused about which brands are safe and which ones should be avoided. </p>

<p>In this article, we’ll take a look at some of the most recent bottled water recalls, and provide some tips on how to keep yourself safe when choosing a bottled water brand. </p>

<h2>What Brands Have Been Recalled?</h2>

<p>In the past few years, there have been several bottled water recalls. The most recent recall was for Aquafina water, which was voluntarily recalled by the company in July of 2019 due to potential contamination with algae. Other brands that have been recalled over the past few years include Arrowhead, Dasani, Evian, and Nestle Pure Life. </p>

<p>It’s important to note that the majority of these recalls are voluntary, meaning the company proactively recalled the product to ensure consumer safety. However, there is still a risk of contamination, so it’s important to be aware of the potential risks when purchasing bottled water. </p>

<h2>What Are the Risks of Contamination?</h2>

<p>The risks of contamination from bottled water can vary depending on the type of contamination present. In the case of the Aquafina recall, the company stated that the water was potentially contaminated with algae, which can cause gastrointestinal distress if consumed. Other recalls have been for potential bacterial contamination, which can cause more serious health issues. </p>

<p>It’s also important to note that contamination can also occur during the bottling process, which is why it’s important to make sure you buy from a reputable company that follows proper safety protocols. </p>

<h2>How Can You Avoid Contaminated Water?</h2>

<p>The best way to avoid contaminated water is to buy from reputable companies that follow proper safety protocols. Make sure you read the label to make sure the water has been properly filtered and treated. Additionally, check the expiration date to make sure the water is still safe to drink. </p>

<p>It’s also important to buy from a company that has a good track record when it comes to safety. Check online reviews to see what other consumers have to say about the company’s safety protocols and their customer service. </p>

<h2>What Should You Do If You Suspect Contaminated Water?</h2>

<p>If you suspect that your bottled water is contaminated, it’s important to take action immediately. Stop drinking the water and contact the company to let them know. Additionally, you should contact your local health department to report the potential contamination. </p>

<p>If you do become ill after drinking contaminated water, it’s important to seek medical attention immediately. Your doctor can provide the necessary care and treatment to ensure your health and safety. </p>

<h2>Conclusion</h2>

<p>Bottled water can be a convenient and affordable way to stay hydrated on the go. However, it’s important to be aware of potential contamination risks and take steps to ensure that you’re buying from a reputable company that follows proper safety protocols. By following these tips, you can help keep yourself safe from contaminated water and enjoy the convenience of bottled water without any worries. </p>
