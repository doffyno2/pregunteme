<p>If you own a PS3 game system, you know how frustrating it is when a game disc becomes scratched. Not only can the game no longer be played, but you also have to shell out money to buy a new copy of the game. Fortunately, there are several methods you can use to repair scratched PS3 discs, so you can get back to gaming as soon as possible.  </p>

<h2>Things You'll Need</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Toothpaste</li>
  <li>Soft cloth</li>
  <li>Water</li>
  <li>Vinegar</li>
  <li>Baking soda</li>
  <li>CD/DVD repair kit</li>
</ul>  

<h2>Using Toothpaste</h2>
<p>Toothpaste is one of the most popular methods for repairing scratched PS3 discs. It is a fairly simple process that can be done with items you already have in your home. </p>

<ol>
  <li>First, take a soft cloth and dampen it with water. </li>
  <li>Squeeze a small amount of toothpaste onto the cloth and rub it into the scratch. </li>
  <li>Continue rubbing the cloth over the scratch in a circular motion until the scratch has been filled in. </li>
  <li>Once the scratch has been filled in, wipe off any excess toothpaste with the damp cloth. </li>
  <li>Allow the disc to dry completely before attempting to play.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Using a CD/DVD Repair Kit</h2>
<p>CD and DVD repair kits are available for purchase at most electronics stores. They typically consist of a polishing compound and a buffing cloth. </p>

<ol>
  <li>Take a small amount of the polishing compound and apply it to the scratched area of the disc. </li>
  <li>Use the buffing cloth to rub the compound into the disc in a circular motion. </li>
  <li>Continue rubbing until the scratch has been filled in. </li>
  <li>Once the scratch has been filled in, use the damp cloth to wipe off any excess compound. </li>
  <li>Allow the disc to dry completely before attempting to play. </li>
</ol>

<h2>Using Water, Vinegar, and Baking Soda</h2>
<p>Another popular method for repairing scratched PS3 discs is to use water, vinegar, and baking soda. This method is also fairly simple and can be done with items you already have in your home. </p>

<ol>
  <li>Take a soft cloth and dampen it with water. </li>
  <li>Mix equal parts vinegar and baking soda together in a bowl. </li>
  <li>Dip the cloth into the mixture and rub it into the scratch. </li>
  <li>Continue rubbing the cloth over the scratch in a circular motion until the scratch has been filled in. </li>
  <li>Once the scratch has been filled in, wipe off any excess mixture with the damp cloth. </li>
  <li>Allow the disc to dry completely before attempting to play. </li>
</ol>
