<h2>What Causes Itching in Hands and Feet?</h2>

<p>Itching of the hands and feet can be caused by a number of conditions, including:</p>

<ul>

  <li><strong>Allergic Reactions:</strong> Allergic reactions can cause itching in the hands and feet. This could be due to an allergy to certain foods, plants, or other substances.</li>
  <li><strong>Infections:</strong> Infectious diseases, such as scabies or athlete's foot, can cause itching of the hands and feet.</li>
  <li><strong>Medical Conditions:</strong> Certain medical conditions, such as kidney or liver disease, can cause itching of the hands and feet.</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Relieve Itching of the Hands and Feet</h2>

<p>The best way to relieve itching of the hands and feet depends on the underlying cause. Here are some tips for relieving itchy hands and feet:</p>

<ul>

  <li><strong>Avoid Certain Foods:</strong> If you have an allergic reaction, it is important to identify the cause and avoid it. This may include avoiding certain foods, plants, or other substances.</li>
  <li><strong>Treat Infections:</strong> Infections such as scabies or athlete's foot should be treated with topical creams or medications prescribed by your doctor.</li>
  <li><strong>See Your Doctor:</strong> If your itching does not improve or if it is accompanied by other symptoms, it is important to see your doctor. Your doctor will be able to determine the underlying cause and provide treatment.</li>
