The sun is the star at the center of our Solar System and is composed of several layers. The temperature of the sun varies depending on which layer you are looking at, with the coolest layer being the photosphere and the hottest layer being the core. In this article, we will discuss which layer of the sun is the coolest and why.

<h2>The Photosphere</h2>

The photosphere is the layer at the surface of the sun that is visible to us on Earth. It is the coolest layer of the sun and it is where the visible light is emitted. The photosphere has an average temperature of 5,778 K (5,505 °C; 9,941 °F). This is relatively cool compared to the other layers of the sun, which are much hotter.

<h2>The Chromosphere</h2>

The chromosphere is the layer of the sun just above the photosphere. It is a thin layer that can be seen during a total solar eclipse. The chromosphere has a temperature ranging from 4,000 K (3,727 °C; 6,740 °F) to 20,000 K (19,727 °C; 35,640 °F). This is much hotter than the photosphere, but still cooler than other layers.

<h2>The Corona</h2>

The corona is the outermost layer of the sun and is the source of the solar wind. It is composed of extremely hot plasma and has a temperature of up to 2 million K (1.9 million °C; 3.4 million °F). This makes it much hotter than the photosphere and chromosphere. 

<h2>Conclusion</h2>

The photosphere is the coolest layer of the sun, with an average temperature of 5,778 K (5,505 °C; 9,941 °F). This is much cooler than the other layers of the sun, such as the chromosphere and corona, which can have temperatures up to 2 million K (1.9 million °C; 3.4 million °F).
