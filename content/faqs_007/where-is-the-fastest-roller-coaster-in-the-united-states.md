<h2>What is a Roller Coaster?</h2>

A roller coaster is a type of amusement ride that typically consists of a series of connected cars, cars that move along a winding track. The cars are usually propelled by gravity and are often designed to provide riders with a thrilling experience. The cars typically travel up and down hills and through sharp turns, loops and other elements that can provide riders with an exciting and unforgettable experience. 

<h2>What Makes a Roller Coaster Fast?</h2>

The speed of a roller coaster is determined by a variety of factors including the length of the track, the design of the cars and the way they are connected to the track, the angle of the hills and the location of the track. The angle of the hills is one of the most important factors in determining the speed of a roller coaster. The steeper the hills, the faster the cars can travel. Additionally, the length of the track and the number of hills can also affect the speed of a roller coaster.
