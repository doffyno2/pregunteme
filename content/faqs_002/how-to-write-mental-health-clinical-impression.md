<p>Writing mental health clinical impressions is an important skill for any mental health professional. A clinical impression is a concise statement that describes a patient’s mental health condition, as well as the professional’s plan for treatment. It is important to be as accurate and descriptive as possible when writing a clinical impression, as this information will shape the rest of the patient’s care. </p>

<h2>Identifying the Problem</h2>

<p>The first step in writing a clinical impression is to identify the patient’s presenting problem or problems. This may include mental health issues, medical issues, and social/environmental factors that are influencing the patient’s mental health. It is important to thoroughly and accurately assess the patient’s problem in order to develop an appropriate treatment plan. </p>

<h2>Assessing the Patient </h2>

<p>The next step is to assess the patient in order to gain an understanding of the patient’s mental health. This includes evaluation of the patient’s mental, emotional, and physical state, as well as any potential risk factors. This assessment should include an evaluation of the patient’s thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, as well as any external factors that may be influencing the patient’s mental health. </p>

<h2>Developing a Diagnosis </h2>

<p>Based on the assessment, the mental health professional should develop a diagnosis. This should be based on the patient’s symptoms, as well as any criteria from the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM). Depending on the complexity of the patient’s condition, the mental health professional may need to consult with other professionals or utilize additional resources in order to make an accurate diagnosis. </p>

<h2>Formulating a Treatment Plan </h2>

<p>Once the diagnosis has been made, the mental health professional should develop a treatment plan. This should include short-term goals, long-term goals, and strategies for achieving those goals. The treatment plan should also include any referrals to other professionals or services, as well as any tools or resources that the patient may need in order to achieve the goals. </p>

<h2>Writing the Clinical Impression </h2>

<p>Finally, the mental health professional should write the clinical impression. This should include a concise statement that describes the patient’s mental health condition, as well as the professional’s plan for treatment. This statement should be as accurate and descriptive as possible, in order to provide an accurate representation of the patient’s condition and the professional’s plan for treatment. </p>
