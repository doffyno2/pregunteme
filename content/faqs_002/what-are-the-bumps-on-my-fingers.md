<h2>Warts</h2>

<p>Warts are small, skin-colored growths that can appear on your fingers. They are caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), which is a virus that can be spread from person to person through skin contact. Warts can be painful or painless and may appear as one bump or in clusters.</p>

<p>Warts are typically treated with topical medications or by freezing them with liquid nitrogen. In some cases, warts may require surgical removal. It is important to see a doctor if you have warts that do not respond to home treatment.</p>

<h2>Cysts</h2>

<p>Cysts are fluid-filled bumps that can form on your fingers. They are usually benign (non-cancerous) but can become painful if they become infected or inflamed. Cysts can be caused by a variety of conditions, including bacterial or fungal infections, trauma, and even genetics.</p>

<p>Cysts are typically treated with topical medications, antibiotics, or surgery. It is important to see a doctor if you have a cyst that does not respond to home treatment.</p>

<h2>Corns</h2>

<p>Corns are small, raised bumps that can appear on your fingers. They are caused by friction or pressure and can become painful if left untreated. Corns typically form in areas of the finger where there is a lot of pressure, such as the tips of the fingers or the sides of the fingers.</p>

<p>Corns can be treated with over-the-counter medications or home remedies. In some cases, corns may require surgical removal. It is important to see a doctor if you have corns that do not respond to home treatment.</p>
