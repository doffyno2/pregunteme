<h2>What is Temperature?</h2>

Temperature is a measure of how hot or cold something is. It is measured in degrees on the Celsius, Fahrenheit, or Kelvin scales. Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of the molecules in a substance. So when we measure the temperature outside, we are actually measuring the average kinetic energy of the air molecules. 

<h2>How is Temperature Measured?</h2>

Temperature is measured using thermometers. These are devices that measure the temperature of a substance. The most common thermometers are liquid-in-glass thermometers, which use a liquid that expands or contracts with changes in temperature. The liquid is placed inside a glass tube, and the amount of expansion or contraction is measured using a scale. Other thermometers, such as digital thermometers, use electrical resistance to measure temperature.
