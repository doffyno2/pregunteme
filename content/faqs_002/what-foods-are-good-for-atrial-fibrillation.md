<h2>What Is Atrial Fibrillation?</h2>

Atrial fibrillation (AFib) is a disorder of the heart's electrical system that causes an irregular heartbeat. This irregular heartbeat can be either too fast or too slow, and it can be too regular or too irregular. When the heart beats too fast, it can cause palpitations, chest pain, and shortness of breath. When the heart beats too slow, it can lead to lightheadedness, dizziness, and fatigue. AFib affects approximately 2.7 million people in the United States alone.

<h2>The Benefits of Eating Healthy For Atrial Fibrillation</h2>

Eating a healthy diet can help reduce the severity of AFib symptoms, as well as reducing the risk of stroke and other complications. Eating healthy can also help lower cholesterol, reduce blood pressure, and improve overall health. The American Heart Association recommends a diet that is low in saturated fat, sodium, and trans fat. It also recommends eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy products. Additionally, limiting alcohol and caffeine consumption can help reduce the risk of AFib.
