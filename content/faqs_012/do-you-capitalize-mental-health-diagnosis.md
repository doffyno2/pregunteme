This article will explore what the correct capitalization rules are for mental health diagnosis, and explain why it is important to understand and use proper capitalization.</p>

<h2>What is Mental Health?</h2>

<p>Mental health is defined as a state of well-being in which an individual is able to cope with the normal stresses of life, work productively, and contribute to their community. Mental health conditions are illnesses or disorders that affect a person’s mood, thinking, and behavior. Mental health disorders can range from mild to severe, and can include depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia.</p>

<h2>Why is Proper Capitalization Important for Mental Health Diagnosis?</h2>

<p>Capitalizing mental health diagnosis is important because it helps to respect the seriousness of the disorder and the person affected by it. It is also important to understand that mental health is a legitimate medical condition, and should be treated with the same respect and understanding as any other medical condition. Capitalizing mental health diagnosis shows that the disorder is real and that it deserves to be taken seriously. It can also help to reduce the stigma that is sometimes associated with mental health diagnoses.</p>

<h2>When Should Mental Health Diagnosis Be Capitalized?</h2>

<p>When referring to a specific mental health diagnosis, it should always be capitalized. For example, if you are referring to major depressive disorder, it should be written as “Major Depressive Disorder”. Similarly, if you are referring to Post-traumatic Stress Disorder, it should be written as “Post-traumatic Stress Disorder”. Additionally, any adjectives used to describe a mental health diagnosis should also be capitalized. For example, if you are referring to “severe depression”, it should be written as “Severe Depression”.</p>

<h2>When Should Mental Health Diagnosis Not Be Capitalized?</h2>

<p>When referring to mental health in general, or when referring to a symptom of a mental health disorder, it is not necessary to capitalize the words. For example, if you are referring to “depression” in general, it should be written as “depression”, not “Depression”. Additionally, if you are referring to a symptom of a mental health disorder, such as “low self-esteem”, it should be written as “low self-esteem”, not “Low Self-Esteem”.</p>
