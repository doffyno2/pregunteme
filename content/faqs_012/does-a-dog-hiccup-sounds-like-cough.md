<h2>Why Do Dogs Hiccup?</h2>

<p>Just like humans, dogs can hiccup too. Hiccups are caused by a spasm of the diaphragm, the muscle that separates the chest from the abdomen. This spasm causes the vocal cords to briefly close, resulting in the distinctive “hic” sound. While hiccups are usually harmless, they can be a sign of an underlying medical condition. If your dog's hiccups are frequent and persistent, it's best to consult your veterinarian.</p>

<p>A dog's hiccup is similar to a human's, but with a slightly higher pitch. It's a short, sharp sound with a distinct “hic” at the end. <h2>What Is the Difference Between a Hiccup and a Cough?</h2>

<p>A hiccup is caused by a spasm of the diaphragm, while a cough is caused by irritation or inflammation of the throat or lungs. A cough is usually a dry, hacking sound and may be accompanied by a gag reflex. Coughing may also be a sign of an underlying health problem, so it's important to have your pup checked out by a veterinarian if the coughing persists. </p>
