The defining characteristic of gumbo is the roux, a mixture of flour and oil that is cooked until it is a deep brown color and gives the gumbo its distinct flavor. Gumbo can be served as a main course or a side dish, and is usually accompanied by rice.

Freezing gumbo is a great way to save time and money. You can prepare a large batch of gumbo and freeze it in individual portions. The first step to freezing gumbo is to make sure it is completely cooled before you freeze it. If you freeze it while it’s still hot, it can cause freezer burn and make the gumbo taste bad. Allow the gumbo to cool completely before you put it in the freezer.

Once the gumbo is cooled, you can portion it out into individual servings. Use freezer-safe containers or bags for the gumbo.
