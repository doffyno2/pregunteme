<h2>What Are Lifters?</h2>

<p>Lifters are important components of a vehicle’s engine system. They are small metal pieces that sit between the camshaft and the valves. Their main purpose is to open and close the valves, allowing air and fuel to flow in and out of the cylinders. The lifter connects the camshaft to the valves by means of a pushrod, which helps control the opening and closing of the valves.</p>

<h2>Signs of Lifters That Need Attention</h2>

<p>When lifters are functioning properly, they are almost silent. However, when there is a problem with the lifters, you may begin to notice some symptoms. These can include a ticking or tapping noise from the engine, a lack of power when accelerating, or a decrease in fuel efficiency. If you’re experiencing any of these symptoms, it’s important to have your lifters checked out as soon as possible. </p>

<h2>What Can Cause Lifters to Wear Out?</h2>

<p>Lifters can wear out over time due to several factors. One of the most common causes is a lack of lubrication. When oil doesn’t reach the lifters, they can become dry and start to wear out prematurely. Low-quality oil can also reduce the life of the lifters, as it may not be able to provide the necessary lubrication. Other causes include a buildup of dirt, debris, and carbon deposits, as well as a lack of maintenance. </p>
