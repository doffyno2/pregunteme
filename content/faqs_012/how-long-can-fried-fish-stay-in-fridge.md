<p>When it comes to fried fish, everybody loves it! Whether it’s a fish fry at a restaurant or a home-cooked meal, it's always a treat. But when it comes to leftovers, there is one burning question: How long can fried fish stay in the fridge?</p>

<h2>Types of Fried Fish</h2>

<p>There are many types of fried fish, and each one has its own storage requirements. For instance, deep-fried fish needs to be refrigerated within two hours of cooking. If it’s left out too long, it can spoil quickly and cause foodborne illness. On the other hand, shallow-fried fish can usually be left out for up to four hours before it needs to be refrigerated.</p>

<p>It’s also important to note that some types of fried fish, such as fish and chips, need to be refrigerated right away. This is because the chips are fried in oil, which can become rancid if left at room temperature for too long.</p>

<h2>How to Store Fried Fish</h2>

<p>Once you’ve determined how long your fried fish can stay out of the refrigerator, it’s time to learn how to store it properly. The best way to store fried fish is in an airtight container. This will help keep it fresh for longer and prevent it from absorbing any odors from other foods in the refrigerator.</p>

<p>It’s also important to make sure that you store the fried fish in the coldest part of your refrigerator. This will help keep it fresh and safe to eat. Additionally, you should always check the expiration date on the label before eating fried fish.</p>

<h2>How Long Can Fried Fish Stay in the Fridge?</h2>

<p>Now that you know how to store fried fish, you’re probably wondering how long it can stay in the fridge. If you’re unsure, it’s best to err on the side of caution and discard any leftovers after two days.</p>

<p>It’s also important to note that if the fried fish smells bad or looks strange, it should be thrown away immediately. This is because it can be a sign that the fish has gone bad and is no longer safe to eat.</p>

<p>If you have too much fried fish and don’t want to throw it away, you can always freeze it. This will help extend its shelf life and allow you to enjoy the leftovers later. To freeze fried fish, simply place it in an airtight container or a freezer bag and store it in the freezer.</p>
