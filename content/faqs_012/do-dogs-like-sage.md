<h2>What Is Sage?</h2>

<p>Sage, also called Salvia officinalis, is an aromatic herb from the mint family. It has been used for centuries for both culinary and medicinal purposes. The leaves are grey-green in color and have a distinctive flavor with a slightly bitter undertone. It is often used in stuffing and sausages, and is a popular ingredient in Italian, French, and Mediterranean cuisine.</p>

<h2>Do Dogs Like Sage?</h2>

<p>The short answer is yes, some dogs like sage. But not all dogs are created equal, so it is important to understand that not all dogs will enjoy the taste and smell of sage. <h2>Is Sage Safe for Dogs?</h2>

<p>Yes, sage is generally considered safe for dogs as long as it is given in moderation. Sage is a rich source of vitamins and minerals, and can be a beneficial addition to your dog’s diet when used in moderation. However, it is important to remember that sage is a strong-tasting herb, and can be too much for some dogs.</p>

<h2>How Should You Give Sage to Your Dog?</h2>

<p>The most common way to give sage to your dog is by adding it to their meals. Start by adding a small amount of sage to their food and gradually increase the amount as your dog gets used to the taste. You can also mix sage with other herbs, such as basil or oregano, to make the flavor more palatable for your dog. If your dog is a picky eater, try adding a few drops of sage essential oil to their food to give it a distinctive flavor.</p>
