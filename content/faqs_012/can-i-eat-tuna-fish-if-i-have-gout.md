<h2>What Is Gout?</h2>

Gout is a form of arthritis that is caused by an excessive buildup of uric acid in the blood. This uric acid forms crystals, which accumulate in the joints and cause severe pain and swelling. Gout tends to affect the big toe more often than other joints, but it can also affect the ankles, knees, elbows, wrists, and other parts of the body.

Gout is typically caused by a combination of factors, such as genetics, lifestyle, and diet. People who are overweight, consume high amounts of alcohol, or eat foods that are high in purines are more at risk of developing gout.

<h2>What Are Purines?</h2>
