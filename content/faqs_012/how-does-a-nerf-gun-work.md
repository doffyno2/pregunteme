<h2>The Anatomy of a Nerf Gun</h2>

At its core, a Nerf gun is a fairly simple machine. Most Nerf guns are made up of a few basic components, including a firing mechanism, a barrel, and a trigger. The firing mechanism is the heart of the blaster and is responsible for launching the foam darts. It typically consists of a spring-loaded plunger, a catch and release mechanism, and a trigger. When the trigger is pulled, the catch and release mechanism trips, releasing the plunger and launching the foam dart out of the barrel. 

<h2>Types of Nerf Guns</h2>

There are many different types of Nerf guns available, ranging from single-shot blasters to fully automatic rifles. Single-shot blasters are the most basic type of Nerf gun and are usually the least expensive. These blasters typically hold one foam dart at a time and must be manually reloaded after each shot. Fully automatic Nerf guns, on the other hand, can hold multiple darts at once and fire them off at a much faster rate. 

<h2>Power Sources for Nerf Guns</h2>

Nerf guns typically use one of two power sources: air or batteries. Air-powered Nerf guns use a pump-action mechanism to launch the darts, while battery-powered Nerf guns use a motorized mechanism. Both types of Nerf guns can launch darts at distances of up to 30 feet or more, depending on the model.
