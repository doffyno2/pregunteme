<h2>Mines in Fallout 4</h2>

<p>Mines are explosive devices that can be used to deal massive damage to enemies. They are placed on the ground and detonated when an enemy comes near. There are three types of mines in Fallout 4: frag mines, pulse mines, and plasma mines. Frag mines are the most basic type, dealing damage to anyone who walks near them. Pulse mines emit a powerful blast when triggered, dealing damage to anyone in the vicinity. Plasma mines are the most powerful type, dealing extremely high amounts of damage to anyone in the blast radius.</p>

<h2>How to Set Mines in Fallout 4</h2>

<p>Setting mines in Fallout 4 is fairly simple. First, you must have the necessary supplies to craft a mine. This includes materials like steel, circuitry, and rubber. You can find these materials by scavenging or looting. Once you have the necessary supplies, you can craft a mine at any of the game's workbenches. To craft a mine, select the “Craft” option from the workbench menu and then choose the type of mine you wish to craft.</p>

<p>Once the mine has been crafted, you can place it in the world by selecting “Place” from the workbench menu. You can place the mine anywhere you want, but keep in mind that it will only explode when an enemy comes into its detection radius. You can also set the mine’s detonation timer, allowing you to control when it explodes.</p>

<p>Once the mine has been placed, you can arm it by pressing the “Arm” button on the workbench menu. This will activate the mine and it will begin to detect enemies. When an enemy enters its detection radius, the mine will explode, dealing massive damage to anyone in the blast radius.</p>
