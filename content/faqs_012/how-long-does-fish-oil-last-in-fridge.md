<h2>What Is Fish Oil? </h2>

Fish oil is an oil derived from certain types of fish, such as salmon, cod, and herring. It is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which can help reduce inflammation and improve heart health, among other benefits. Fish oil can be taken in capsule form or as liquid oil.

<h2>How to Store Fish Oil </h2>

When it comes to storing fish oil, the key is to keep it cool and away from light. Fish oil should be stored in a dark, airtight container in the refrigerator. This will help keep the oil fresh and prevent it from spoiling.

<h2>How Long Does Fish Oil Last in the Fridge? </h2>

If stored properly in the fridge, fish oil can last up to six months. However, it's important to keep an eye on the expiration date on the bottle. If the expiration date has passed, the oil should be discarded.

<h2>How to Tell if Fish Oil Has Gone Bad </h2>

If the fish oil has gone bad, it will have a strong odor and may look cloudy or discolored. It's important to discard any oil that has gone bad, as it can be contaminated with bacteria or heavy metals.

<h2>How to Maximize the Shelf Life of Fish Oil </h2>

To maximize the shelf life of fish oil, it's important to store it properly. Keep it in a dark, airtight container in the refrigerator. Make sure to check the expiration date on the bottle and discard any oil that has passed its expiration date.

<h2>Are There Alternatives to Fish Oil? </h2>

If you're looking for an alternative to fish oil, there are a few options. Flaxseed oil and chia seed oil are both rich in omega-3 fatty acids and have a similar nutritional profile to fish oil. However, they may not have the same benefits as fish oil, so it's important to consult a healthcare professional before making any changes to your diet.
