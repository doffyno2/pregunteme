<h2>What Is a Cleft Lip?</h2>

<p>A cleft lip is a birth defect that affects the lip and mouth. It occurs when the tissue that forms the lip fails to join together properly during the early stages of pregnancy. As a result, the lip may appear split, or there may be a gap between the upper lip and the nose. In some cases, the cleft may also affect the gum line, causing further abnormalities. Cleft lip can be either unilateral, affecting only one side of the face, or bilateral, affecting both sides.</p>

<h2>What Causes Cleft Lip?</h2>

<p>The exact cause of cleft lip is still unknown. However, there are certain factors that may increase the risk of developing a cleft lip. These include genetics, environmental factors, and certain medications. Some studies have also suggested that certain lifestyle choices, such as smoking and drinking alcohol during pregnancy, may increase the risk of cleft lip.</p>

<h2>What Are the Symptoms of Cleft Lip?</h2>

<p>The most common symptom of cleft lip is a split or gap in the upper lip. This may be accompanied by other facial abnormalities, such as a flattened nose or a malformed jaw. In some cases, the cleft may also affect the interior of the mouth, causing a misalignment of the teeth.</p>

<h2>How Is Cleft Lip Diagnosed?</h2>
