<h2>Is Dried Fish Safe During Pregnancy?</h2>

The short answer is yes, you can eat dried fish during pregnancy—but it is important to pay attention to the type of fish you are eating as well as when and how it is prepared. Fish is a great source of protein, omega-3 fatty acids, and other important vitamins and minerals that are essential for a healthy pregnancy. However, some types of fish may contain high levels of mercury or other contaminants that can be harmful to both you and your baby.

<h2>Types of Fish to Avoid During Pregnancy</h2>

Certain types of fish contain high levels of mercury and should be avoided during pregnancy. These include king mackerel, swordfish, tilefish, and shark. Mercury can be especially harmful to a developing baby, so it is important to avoid these types of fish.

It is also important to be aware of any fish that has been caught in polluted waters. Contaminants such as PCBs and dioxins can be present in fish from polluted waters and can be harmful to both you and your baby.
