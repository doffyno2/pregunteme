<h2>What is a Gift Card?</h2>

A gift card is a prepaid card that can be used to purchase goods or services from a specific retailer. Gift cards can be purchased in-store or online, and they typically come with a set amount of money already loaded onto them. They can be used to make purchases up to the value of the card, and any remaining balance can be used towards future purchases. Gift cards are a great way to give someone a present without having to worry about picking out the right size or color.

<h2>How Do Gift Cards Get Activated?</h2>

There are several ways that gift cards can be activated. The most common method is for the card to be activated at the time of purchase. This is done by swiping the card through the retailer’s point-of-sale system, which will register the card and activate it for use. Gift cards can also be activated online, either by entering the card number and PIN code into the retailer’s website, or by using a special activation link provided by the retailer.

<h2>What Happens After a Gift Card is Activated?</h2>

Once a gift card is activated, it can be used to make purchases at the retailer’s store or website. The card can also be used to check the remaining balance and make additional purchases until the balance is depleted. Some retailers may also offer additional features such as the ability to track purchases, set spending limits, or set up automatic payments.

<h2>Are There Any Fees Associated with Gift Cards?</h2>

Most gift cards do not have any associated fees, but there may be a few exceptions. For example, some retailers may charge a fee for activating the card or for checking the remaining balance. It’s important to read the terms and conditions before buying a gift card, as these fees may vary from retailer to retailer.

<h2>Are Gift Cards Refundable?</h2>
