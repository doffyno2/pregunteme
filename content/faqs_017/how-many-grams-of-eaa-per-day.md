<h2>What Are Essential Amino Acids?</h2>

<p>Essential Amino Acids are the nine amino acids our bodies can’t produce on their own. These nine amino acids must be obtained from food sources, making them “essential”. Without them, our bodies wouldn’t be able to absorb and utilize proteins, so it’s important to ensure you’re getting enough. The nine essential amino acids are:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Histidine</li>
  <li>Isoleucine</li>
  <li>Leucine</li>
  <li>Lysine</li>
  <li>Methionine</li>
  <li>Phenylalanine</li>
  <li>Threonine</li>
  <li>Tryptophan</li>
  <li>Valine</li>
</ul>

<h2>What Are the Benefits of EAA?</h2>

<p>EAAs play an important role in muscle growth, repair, and maintenance. They are also essential for the production of hormones, enzymes, and other important molecules. Furthermore, they help to regulate the body’s metabolism and energy levels. Without EAAs, our bodies would struggle to absorb and make use of proteins.</p> 

<p>What’s more, EAAs are important for brain and cognitive function. They help to produce neurotransmitters such as dopamine and serotonin, which regulate mood and help to keep us feeling mentally alert.</p> 

<p>For all these reasons, it’s important to ensure you’re getting enough EAAs in your diet. And that brings us to the question of how much you need.</p>

<h2>How Much EAA Do You Need Per Day?</h2>

<p>The amount of EAAs you need per day depends on your age, weight, activity level, and other factors. Generally speaking, the recommended daily allowance (RDA) for adults is 0.36 grams per kilogram of body weight per day. However, this varies slightly depending on which essential amino acid you’re looking at.</p> 

<p>For example, the RDA for leucine is higher than the RDA for histidine. The RDA for leucine is 0.55 grams per kilogram of body weight per day, while the RDA for histidine is just 0.15 grams per kilogram of body weight per day.</p> 

<p>The best way to ensure you’re getting enough EAAs is to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of proteins from a variety of sources. Animal proteins such as beef, chicken, and fish are rich in EAAs, as are eggs, dairy products, and plant-based proteins such as legumes, tofu, nuts, and seeds.</p>
