<h2>Types of Cruise Ships</h2>

<p>Cruise ships come in all shapes and sizes, from the small and cozy river boats to the huge and luxurious ocean liners. The size and type of cruise ship will determine how many people can fit on it. The larger the cruise ship, the more passengers it can hold. Some of the most popular types of cruise ships include:</p>

<ul>
    <li>River boats: These small, cozy vessels are perfect for exploring rivers and smaller waterways. They typically accommodate up to 50 passengers.</li>
    <li>Large ocean liners: These are the largest type of cruise ship and can accommodate up to 6,000 passengers.</li>
    <li>Mid-size ocean liners: Mid-size cruise ships can accommodate up to 3,000 passengers.</li>
    <li>Small ocean liners: Smaller cruise ships are typically designed for more intimate voyages and can accommodate up to 1,500 passengers.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Factors That Affect Capacity</h2>

<p>The size of the cruise ship isn't the only factor that affects how many people can fit on a cruise ship. Other factors include:</p>

<ul>
    <li><b>Number of cabins:</b> The number of cabins on a cruise ship will determine how many people can be accommodated. Smaller cruise ships may have fewer cabins, while larger ships may have more.</li>
    <li><b>Cabin size:</b> The size of the cabins will also determine how many people can fit on a cruise ship. Standard cabins can typically accommodate two adults, while larger cabins may be able to accommodate more people.</li>
    <li><b>Deck space:</b> The amount of deck space available will determine how many passengers the cruise ship can hold. More deck space means more passengers.</li>
    <li><b>Passenger amenities:</b> The amenities available on the cruise ship will also affect its capacity. More amenities will require more space, limiting the number of passengers that can be accommodated.</li>
</ul>
