<h2>What Is Whiskey?</h2>

Whiskey is a distilled spirit made from fermented grain mash. The grains used to make whiskey vary depending on the type of whiskey being produced and can include barley, corn, rye, and wheat. The grains are mixed with water, yeast, and other ingredients, and then fermented and distilled. After the distillation process, the whiskey is aged in oak barrels for a period of time.

<h2>What Is the Difference Between Scotch, Bourbon, Rye, and Irish Whiskey?</h2>

Scotch is a type of whiskey that is made in Scotland and is made from malted barley. Bourbon is a type of whiskey that is made in the United States and is made from at least 51% corn. Rye is a type of whiskey that is made from at least 51% rye. Irish whiskey is a type of whiskey that is made in Ireland and is made from a combination of malted and unmalted barley. 

<h2>What Are the Different Types of Whiskey?</h2>

There are many different types of whiskey, including single malt, blended, and flavored whiskey. Single malt whiskey is made from one type of grain, usually barley, and is produced at one distillery.
