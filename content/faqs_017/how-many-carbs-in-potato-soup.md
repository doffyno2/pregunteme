<h2>What Are Carbs?</h2>

Carbohydrates, or carbs, are one of the three main macronutrients (along with protein and fat). They provide the body with energy and are found in a variety of foods. Some of the most common sources of carbs are grains, fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

Carbs are classified as either simple or complex. Simple carbs are found in foods like refined sugar, white flour, and fruit juice. Complex carbs are found in foods like whole grains, legumes, and vegetables. 

<h2>How Many Carbs Are in Potato Soup?</h2>

The amount of carbs in potato soup varies depending on the recipe and ingredients used. Generally speaking, potato soup is a relatively low-carb food.

A one-cup serving of homemade potato soup made with potatoes, butter, milk, salt, and pepper contains about 18 grams of carbs. This serving size provides about 12 percent of the recommended daily value for carbs.

If you're trying to reduce your carb intake, you can make a lower-carb version of potato soup. For example, you can use low-carb ingredients such as cauliflower or celery instead of potatoes. You can also reduce the amount of milk and butter used in the soup.

<h2>Tips for Making a Low-Carb Potato Soup</h2>

If you're looking to reduce the amount of carbs in your potato soup, here are some tips to help you out:

• Use cauliflower or celery instead of potatoes. This will reduce the amount of carbs in the soup while still providing a hearty texture.

• Use low-fat milk instead of whole milk. This will reduce the amount of fat and carbs in the soup.

• Use low-sodium broth or stock instead of water. This will add flavor to the soup without adding a lot of carbs.

• Add other vegetables such as bell peppers, carrots, or onions to the soup. This will add more flavor and nutrition while keeping the carb content low.

• Use herbs and spices instead of salt and pepper. This will add flavor to the soup without adding any carbs.

<h2>Low-Carb Potato Soup Recipes</h2>
