<h2>What is a K12 School?</h2>

<p>K12 schools are educational institutions that provide instruction in grades kindergarten through 12th grade. These schools are typically funded by local, state, and federal government agencies and may be run as public, private, or charter schools. K12 schools are generally smaller than traditional high schools and provide a more personalized learning experience. They often offer a variety of courses and programs that may not be available at larger schools, such as specialized language classes or hands-on activities.</p>

<h2>How Many K12 Schools Are in the United States?</h2>

<p>According to the National Center for Education Statistics, there were approximately 98,000 K12 schools in the United States in 2020. This includes public, private, and charter schools. Of these, there were 67,000 public schools, 27,000 private schools, and 4,000 charter schools. The majority of these schools are located in urban areas, with the largest concentration of K12 schools in California, Texas, and Florida.</p>

<h2>What Types of K12 Schools Are There?</h2>

<p>K12 schools in the United States are divided into four main types: public, private, charter, and virtual. Private schools are funded by tuition and donations and may have more freedom to choose their own curriculum and standards. Charter schools are publicly funded but operate independently from the local school district. Virtual schools are online schools that offer courses and instruction to students in all grade levels.</p>
