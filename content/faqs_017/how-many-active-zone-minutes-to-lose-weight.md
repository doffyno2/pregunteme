<p>The number of active zone minutes you need to lose weight depends on many factors. However, the general consensus is that most people need at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity per week to stay healthy and lose weight. This equates to about 30 minutes of active zone minutes per day.</p>

<p>Achieving this number of active zone minutes per week is a great way to start a program of healthy weight loss. The active zone is the heart rate range where you are working out at an intensity that allows your body to burn fat and calories. This is the range that you should aim to stay in to get the best results from your workout. If you are new to exercise, start with 15-20 minutes of moderate intensity exercise and gradually increase your active zone minutes over time.</p>

<p>When you are in the active zone, your body is working hard. Your heart rate should be between 50 and 85% of your maximum heart rate. The higher your heart rate, the more intense your workout is. To find out your maximum heart rate, subtract your age from 220. For example, if you are 40 years old, your maximum heart rate would be 180. To calculate your active zone minutes, multiply your maximum heart rate by 0.5 (50%) and 0.85 (85%). For the 40 year old example, the active zone would be 90-153 beats per minute (bpm).</p>

<p>To ensure that you are working out in the active zone, use a heart rate monitor or a fitness tracker such as Fitbit. This will allow you to track your heart rate and make sure you are working out at the right intensity. You can also use the ‘talk test’ to check if you are in the active zone. If you are working out and can still hold a conversation without getting out of breath, you are in the active zone.</p>

<p>Once you know your maximum heart rate and active zone, you can begin to set goals and track your progress. Aim to work out for 30 minutes at least five days a week.
