<h2>What Is the Heat Output of Wood Coals?</h2>

Wood coals are a type of solid fuel source and produce heat through a process of combustion, or burning. The heat output of wood coals is measured in British Thermal Units (BTUs). One BTU is equal to the amount of energy needed to raise one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit. The average heat output of wood coals is between 12,000 and 15,000 BTUs per pound. This is slightly less than the heat output of natural gas, which typically produces around 20,000 BTUs per pound.

When it comes to the heat output of different fuel sources, wood coals are among the least efficient. For example, natural gas produces around 20,000 BTUs per pound, while propane can produce up to 25,000 BTUs per pound. Electricity is even more efficient, with some sources producing up to 100,000 BTUs per pound. However, wood coals can still be an efficient fuel source when used in a properly constructed and maintained wood stove.
