<h2>What Are Spring Rolls?</h2>

Spring rolls are a type of rolled appetizers that originated in China. In Chinese cuisine, spring rolls are usually made with pork, shrimp, and vegetables, then wrapped in a thin flour wrapper. The spring rolls are then deep-fried for a few minutes until golden brown.

In Western cuisine, spring rolls are often filled with a combination of vegetables, such as cabbage, carrots, and mushrooms. The wrappers are usually made from wheat flour or rice paper.

<h2>How Many Carbs in Spring Rolls?</h2>

The exact carb content of spring rolls varies depending on the ingredients used, as well as the size of the spring rolls. Generally speaking, one large spring roll contains around 15 to 20 grams of carbs. A medium-sized spring roll typically contains around 10 to 15 grams of carbs.

It's also important to note that the carbs in spring rolls come mostly from the wrapper and not from the filling. The wrappers are usually made from wheat flour, which is high in carbs.
