October is full of holidays, both religious and secular. Some of the most popular holidays celebrated in October include Halloween, Thanksgiving Day, Columbus Day, and National Boss’s Day. 

<h3>Halloween</h3> 
Halloween is one of the most popular holidays in October. It is celebrated on October 31st and is marked by dressing up in costumes, carving pumpkins, and trick-or-treating. On Halloween, children go from house to house in search of candy, and adults often host costume parties.

<h3>Thanksgiving Day</h3> 
Thanksgiving Day is a federal holiday observed on the fourth Thursday of November. It is a day of thanks and celebration, marked by family gatherings and the traditional feast of turkey and all the trimmings. 

<h3>Columbus Day</h3> 
Columbus Day is a federal holiday observed on the second Monday of October. The holiday celebrates the legacy of Christopher Columbus, who is credited with discovering the New World in 1492.

<h3>National Boss’s Day</h3> 
National Boss’s Day, also known as National Boss Appreciation Day, is observed on October 16th. This day is dedicated to thanking bosses and supervisors for their hard work and dedication.
