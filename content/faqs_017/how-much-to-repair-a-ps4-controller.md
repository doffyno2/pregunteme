<p>A broken PlayStation 4 (PS4) controller can be one of the most frustrating experiences for gamers. Not only are you unable to play your favorite games, but you also have to worry about costly repairs. But how much does it cost to repair a PS4 controller?</p>

<p>The cost of repairing a PS4 controller can vary, depending on the type of repair and the parts needed. However, it can cost anywhere from $20 to $120 for a basic repair, such as replacing a joystick or a button. For more complex repairs, such as replacing the motherboard, the cost can be up to $250.</p>

<p>If your controller is still under warranty, you may be able to get it repaired for free. Otherwise, you may need to pay for the repair out of pocket. It's important to note that the cost of the repair will depend on the specific model of your controller and the parts needed.</p>

<h2>How to Tell If a PS4 Controller Needs Repair?</h2>

<p>If your PS4 controller isn't working properly, it may need to be repaired or replaced. Here are some common signs that your controller needs to be repaired:</p>

<ul>
<li>The controller isn't responding to button presses or joystick movements.</li>
<li>The controller is not connecting to the console.</li>
<li>The controller is making strange noises when you press buttons or move the joystick.</li>
<li>The controller is unresponsive or sluggish.</li>
<li>The controller is displaying an error message.</li>
</ul>

<p>If you notice any of these signs, it's a good idea to have your controller checked by a professional. They can diagnose any potential problems and advise you on the best course of action.</p>

<h2>Common PS4 Controller Repairs</h2>

<p>The most common repairs for a PS4 controller include:</p>

<ul>
<li>Replacing the joystick or buttons.</li>
<li>Replacing the battery.</li>
<li>Cleaning and lubricating the joystick.</li>
<li>Replacing the motherboard.</li>
<li>Replacing the charging port.</li>
</ul>

<p>Depending on the type of repair, the cost can vary significantly. It's important to note that some repairs may require specialized tools and expertise, so it's best to have a professional do the repair.</p>
