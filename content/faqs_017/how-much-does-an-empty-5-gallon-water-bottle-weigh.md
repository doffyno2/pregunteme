<h2>How Much Does an Empty 5 Gallon Water Bottle Weigh?</h2>

<p>The weight of an empty 5 gallon water bottle varies, depending on the type of plastic it is made from. A standard 5 gallon water bottle made from polyethylene terephthalate (PET) plastic typically weighs 1.3 to 1.5 pounds (0.59 to 0.68 kg). A 5 gallon water bottle made from high-density polyethylene (HDPE) plastic typically weighs 1.7 to 2 pounds (0.77 to 0.91 kg).</p>

<h2>Factors That Affect the Weight of a 5 Gallon Water Bottle</h2>

<p>The weight of a 5 gallon water bottle is affected by a number of factors, including:</p>

<ul>
  <li>The type of plastic used to make the bottle.</li>
  <li>The thickness of the plastic.</li>
  <li>The shape of the bottle.</li>
  <li>The presence of any handles or other features.</li>
</ul>

<p>The type of plastic used to make a 5 gallon water bottle will have the biggest impact on its weight. PET plastic is lighter than HDPE plastic, so a 5 gallon water bottle made from PET plastic will weigh less than one made from HDPE plastic.</p>

<p>The thickness of the plastic can also affect the weight of the bottle. Thicker plastic will weigh more than thinner plastic. This is because thicker plastic is more dense and thus heavier.</p>

<p>The shape of the bottle can also have an impact on its weight. A bottle with a round shape will typically weigh more than a bottle with a flat shape, because it has more material. The presence of handles or other features can also affect the weight of the bottle, as these features add additional material.</p>

<h2>The Weight of a Full 5 Gallon Water Bottle</h2>

<p>A full 5 gallon water bottle will weigh significantly more than an empty one. A full 5 gallon water bottle made from PET plastic typically weighs about 11.4 pounds (5.2 kg). A full 5 gallon water bottle made from HDPE plastic typically weighs about 13.2 pounds (6 kg).</p>

<p>The weight of a full 5 gallon water bottle can vary depending on the type of water inside the bottle. For example, the weight of the bottle will be slightly higher if the water is cold, as cold water is denser than warm water. The weight of the bottle can also be affected by the presence of any dissolved solids in the water, such as minerals or salts.</p>
