<h2>What is Rust?</h2>

Rust is a type of corrosion that occurs when iron or steel is exposed to oxygen and moisture. The process of rusting causes the metal to break down, which can result in serious damage to the structure of your car.

<h2>What are the Different Types of Rust Repair?</h2>

When it comes to rust repair, there are several methods that can be used to fix the problem. The most common methods include:

<h3>Surface Rust Repair</h3>

Surface rust repair is the simplest and most cost-effective way to repair rust damage. The process involves removing the rust and then applying a protective coating to the area. This method is ideal for small areas of rust or for vehicles that are not exposed to moisture on a regular basis.

<h3>Welding Rust Repair</h3>

Welding rust repair is a more complex and expensive process than surface rust repair. This method involves welding a new piece of metal to the affected area to replace the corroded metal. This type of repair is typically used on larger areas of rust or for vehicles that are exposed to moisture on a regular basis.

<h3>Body Panel Replacement</h3>

Body panel replacement is a more extreme method of rust repair and should generally be used as a last resort. This method involves completely replacing the affected area of the vehicle with a new piece of sheet metal. This type of repair is typically used for major rust damage or for vehicles that are extensively corroded.

<h2>How Much Does Rust Repair Cost?</h2>
