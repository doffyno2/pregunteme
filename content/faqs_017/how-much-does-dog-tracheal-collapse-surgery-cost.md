<h2>What Is Tracheal Collapse?</h2>

<p>Tracheal collapse is a condition in which the walls of the trachea become weak or damaged. This causes the trachea to become narrowed or collapsed, which can lead to difficulty breathing and other respiratory issues. Tracheal collapse is most commonly seen in small breed dogs, such as toy poodles, Chihuahuas, and Yorkshire terriers. It is also seen in larger breeds, such as German Shepherds and Labrador Retrievers. Tracheal collapse is often caused by a combination of genetic predisposition and environmental factors, such as air pollution, exposure to smoke, or dust.</p>

<p>Tracheal collapse can be a serious condition, and if left untreated it can lead to secondary conditions such as bronchitis, pneumonia, and even death. Treatment for tracheal collapse usually involves a combination of medications, lifestyle changes, and in some cases, surgery.</p>

<h2>What Is Involved In Tracheal Collapse Surgery?</h2>

<p>Tracheal collapse surgery is a delicate procedure that requires specialized surgical tools and techniques. During the surgery, the surgeon will make an incision in the dog's trachea and insert an artificial airway or stent. The stent is sutured into place to help support the trachea and prevent further collapse. In some cases, the surgeon may also perform a tracheoplasty, which involves surgically removing a portion of the trachea and then suturing the ends together. This procedure is performed in more severe cases of tracheal collapse.</p>

<p>The surgery is typically performed with general anesthesia and can take several hours to complete. After the surgery, the dog will be observed in the hospital for a few days to make sure the surgery was successful and that the dog is recovering properly.</p>
