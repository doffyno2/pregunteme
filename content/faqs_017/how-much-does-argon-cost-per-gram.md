<h2>What is Argon Used For?</h2>

Argon is used in a wide range of industrial, scientific, and medical applications. In welding, argon is used to shield the weld area from oxygen, which could cause oxidation. It also helps to prevent solidification of the molten metal and creates a more even weld.

In the production of lighting, argon is used to fill the gaps between the electrodes of fluorescent lamps, allowing the lamps to produce more light with the same amount of electricity. Argon is also used to fill the gaps between the electrodes of gas-discharge lamps, such as sodium vapor lamps and metal halide lamps, to enhance the efficiency of the light produced.

In the production of crystals, argon is used to create an inert atmosphere that prevents the growth of unwanted crystals while allowing desired crystals to form.

In the medical field, argon is used as a cryogenic agent to freeze and preserve tissue samples.
