<h2>What is Sodium?</h2>

Sodium is a mineral that is essential to human health. It helps the body regulate blood pressure, muscle contractions, and fluid balance. It also helps keep the heart beating and plays a role in nerve conduction. The body needs about 500 milligrams of sodium a day for optimal health.

<h2>Sodium Content in Yogurt</h2>

Yogurt typically contains between 10 and 50 milligrams of sodium per serving. This amount is much lower than other dairy products such as cheese, which can have up to 200 milligrams of sodium per serving. Low-fat or non-fat yogurt generally contains the least amount of sodium, while full-fat yogurt contains the most.

For those looking to reduce their sodium intake, yogurt can be a great option. It can be substituted in recipes that call for cheese, or eaten plain with a sprinkle of cinnamon or honey. It is also a great addition to smoothies or oatmeal.

Additionally, yogurt is a great source of vitamins and minerals, including potassium, magnesium, and vitamins A, B12, and D.

Regular yogurt is milder in flavor and has a thinner texture than Greek or non-dairy yogurt.
