<h2>What Is Butter?</h2>

Butter is a dairy product made by churning cream or milk. It is a solid fat that is composed of a combination of different fatty acids, including butyric acid, palmitic acid, and stearic acid. It is usually an off-white or yellowish color and is often used in baking, cooking, or as a spread.

<h2>How Much Calcium Is in Butter?</h2>

It may be surprising to learn that butter contains calcium. Butter is a dairy product, which means it is naturally high in calcium. In fact, one tablespoon of butter contains about 4 milligrams of calcium, which is about one-third of the recommended daily value for calcium.
