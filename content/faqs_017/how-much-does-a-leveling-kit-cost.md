<h2>What Is a Leveling Kit?</h2>

<p>A leveling kit is a suspension modification designed to level out a vehicle's stance. It typically involves adding spacers to the existing suspension components, such as the struts and shocks, to raise the vehicle's ride height. This can help to improve the vehicle's off-road performance, as well as its aesthetics.</p>

<h2>Types of Leveling Kits</h2>

<p>There are two main types of leveling kits: spacer kits and coil-over kits. Spacer kits are the most common type of kit and are usually the most affordable option. They are also the easiest to install, as they simply involve adding spacers to the existing suspension components. Coil-over kits, on the other hand, involve replacing the existing suspension components with aftermarket coil-overs. This makes them more expensive and more difficult to install, but can also provide better performance and handling.</p>

<h2>Leveling Kit Prices</h2>

<p>The cost of a leveling kit can vary significantly depending on the type of kit, the make and model of your vehicle, and even the specific kit you choose. Generally speaking, spacer kits are the most affordable option, with prices starting around $100 for a basic kit. Coil-over kits are usually more expensive, with prices ranging from $500 to $1,000 or more, depending on the kit and the vehicle.</p>

<h2>Factors Affecting Cost</h2>

<p>When it comes to the cost of a leveling kit, there are a few factors that can affect the price. These include the type of kit (spacer or coil-over), the make and model of your vehicle, and the specific kit you choose. You'll also need to factor in any additional parts or labor you may need for installation.</p>
