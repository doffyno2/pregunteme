<h2>What Are Net Carbs?</h2>

Net carbs are the amount of carbohydrates in a food minus the amount of fiber and sugar alcohols. Total carbohydrates are the sum of the starches, sugars, and dietary fiber found in a food. Fiber and sugar alcohols are not digested in the same way as other carbohydrates, so they are not included in the calculation of net carbs. For example, if a food contains 18 grams of carbohydrates, 10 grams of dietary fiber, and 2 grams of sugar alcohols, its net carb count is 6 grams.

<h2>How Many Net Carbs in a Sweet Potato?</h2>

The amount of net carbs in a sweet potato depends on the size of the potato. A small (5-inch) sweet potato contains approximately 15.5 grams of net carbs, while a large (7-inch) sweet potato contains approximately 22.7 grams of net carbs.

<h2>How to Cook Sweet Potatoes?</h2>

Sweet potatoes can be cooked in a variety of ways, including baking, roasting, boiling, or even microwaving. To bake a sweet potato, preheat the oven to 375°F. To roast a sweet potato, preheat the oven to 400°F. Cut the sweet potato into cubes, then place on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, then toss to coat.
