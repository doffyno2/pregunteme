<h2>What Causes Rotted Outdoor Trim?</h2>

<p>Rotted outdoor trim is caused by a variety of factors, including exposure to moisture, extreme temperatures, and the sun. Moisture can cause rot and damage to trim, especially if it is not sealed properly. Extreme temperatures can cause trim to warp and crack, which can lead to rot. The sun can also cause discoloration and fading of trim, which can lead to rot.</p>

<h2>How Much Does It Cost To Repair or Replace Rotted Outdoor Trim?</h2>

<p>The cost of repairing or replacing rotted outdoor trim will vary depending on the level of damage, the type of trim, and the materials and supplies needed. Generally, simple repairs such as filling cracks or replacing small pieces of trim can cost anywhere from $50 to $200. Completely replacing all of the trim on your home can cost anywhere from $500 to $2,000 or more.</p>
