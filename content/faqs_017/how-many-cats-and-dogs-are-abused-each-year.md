<h2>What is the definition of animal abuse?</h2>

<p>Animal abuse is defined as the intentional infliction of physical or psychological pain, suffering, or death on an animal. This may include overt or subtle physical abuse, neglect, or intentional cruelty. Abuse can take many forms, from neglecting to provide adequate food or shelter to physical violence.</p>

<h2>What are the most common forms of animal abuse?</h2>

<p>The most common forms of animal abuse include neglect, intentional cruelty, and hoarding. Neglect is the failure to provide an animal with basic needs such as food, water, shelter, or veterinary care. Intentional cruelty involves physical violence such as beating, kicking, or burning an animal. Hoarding occurs when an individual has too many animals and fails to provide adequate care for them.</p>

<h2>What are the effects of animal abuse?</h2>

<p>The effects of animal abuse can be devastating. Abused animals often suffer from physical and psychological trauma, including fear, depression, and aggression. They may also suffer from serious medical conditions due to neglect or intentional cruelty. In some cases, animals may even be killed due to abuse.</p>
