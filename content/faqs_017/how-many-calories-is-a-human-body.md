<h2>What Are Calories?</h2>

<p>Calories are units of energy, defined as the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 gram of water by 1 degree Celsius. In terms of nutrition, calories are the unit of measure used to quantify the energy content of food. Each food contains a certain amount of calories, and when we eat it, our bodies use the energy stored in the food to fuel our operations. The more energy-dense a food is, the more calories it contains.</p>

<h2>How Many Calories Do We Need?</h2>

<p>Calorie needs vary from person to person, depending on age, gender, activity level, and other factors. Generally speaking, adults should consume between 2000 and 3000 calories per day to maintain a healthy weight. People who are physically active may need to consume more calories, while those who are less active may need to consume fewer calories. Children and teenagers have different calorie needs than adults, and pregnant women may need to consume more calories than usual.</p>
