<h2>What Is A Transfer Case?</h2>

<p>A transfer case is a component of a vehicle’s drivetrain that transfers power from the engine to the axles. It is essentially a gearbox that is used to change the direction and speed of power delivery from the engine to the drive axle. In a four-wheel-drive vehicle, the transfer case is used to shift the power from the engine to the front and rear axles. It also contains a differential, which allows the wheels to rotate at different speeds when turning.</p>

<h2>What Are The Common Problems With Transfer Cases?</h2>

<p>The most common problems with transfer cases are wear and tear, leaks, and mechanical failure. Wear and tear can occur over time due to normal use, and can cause the seals inside the transfer case to start leaking. Leaks can cause fluid to leak out and eventually cause the transfer case to fail. Mechanical failure can be caused by a variety of factors, including a broken gear, worn bearings, or a faulty seal. In any case, it’s important to have your transfer case checked regularly by a qualified mechanic.</p>

<h2>What Are The Signs Of A Failing Transfer Case?</h2>

<p>The most common sign of a failing transfer case is excessive noise coming from beneath the vehicle. This noise is usually caused by a lack of lubrication in the transfer case, which can lead to grinding or squeaking sounds. If you hear this noise, it’s important to have your transfer case inspected by a qualified mechanic as soon as possible. Other signs of a failing transfer case include a burning smell, vibration, and a loss of power.</p>
