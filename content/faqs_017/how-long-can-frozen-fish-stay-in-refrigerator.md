<h2>What is Frozen Fish?</h2>

<p>Frozen fish is fish that has been frozen in order to preserve it for a longer period of time. It is a common practice for both commercial and recreational fishermen to freeze their catch in order to have a continuous supply of fresh fish to enjoy. The fish is typically frozen at -20°C or lower, and is stored in airtight containers or vacuum-sealed bags.</p>

<h2>How Long Can Frozen Fish Stay in the Refrigerator?</h2>

<p>The length of time that frozen fish can stay in the refrigerator depends on a few factors, including the type of fish, the temperature of the refrigerator, and how it is stored. Generally speaking, frozen fish can remain in the refrigerator for up to two months. Any longer than two months, and the quality of the fish can start to degrade.</p>

<h2>How to Store Frozen Fish in the Refrigerator</h2>

<p>When storing frozen fish in the refrigerator, it is important to take the necessary precautions to ensure that it stays fresh and safe to eat. The following tips can help you make sure your frozen fish stays fresh for as long as possible:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Store the frozen fish in an airtight container or vacuum-sealed bag.</li>
  <li>Make sure that the temperature of the refrigerator is set to 0°F or lower.</li>
  <li>Label the container with the date that the fish was frozen.</li>
  <li>Cook or consume the fish within two months of freezing.</li>
</ul>

<h2>What Happens if Frozen Fish Stays in the Refrigerator Too Long?</h2>

<p>If frozen fish stays in the refrigerator for too long, it can start to spoil. The fish may develop an off odor, off taste, or an unappetizing texture. Additionally, it may start to become discolored due to oxidation, or the growth of bacteria or other microorganisms. Eating spoiled fish can make you sick, so it is important to cook or consume frozen fish within two months of freezing.</p>
