<h2>How Fast Can Submarines Travel?</h2> 

The speed of a submarine underwater depends on many factors, including its design, the type of engine it uses, and the depth of the water. Generally, submarines can travel anywhere from 5 to 25 knots (5.8 to 28.8 mph). Nuclear-powered submarines can reach speeds of up to 30 knots (34.5 mph) while diesel-electric submarines can reach up to 18 knots (20.7 mph).

<h2>What Factors Influence Submarine Speed?</h2>

Submarine speed is affected by several factors. The type of engine a submarine uses has a major impact on its speed. Nuclear-powered submarines are the fastest, as they can reach speeds up to 30 knots (34.5 mph). Diesel-electric submarines can reach up to 18 knots (20.7 mph), and battery-powered submarines can reach up to 8 knots (9.2 mph).

The depth of the water also affects a submarine's speed. As a submarine dives deeper, the water pressure increases and the water becomes more resistant to the submarine's movement. At depths of over 1000 meters (3281 feet), the pressure of the water can be so great that a submarine cannot reach its maximum speed.

As a result, submarines must balance speed and stealth in order to maximize their effectiveness.
