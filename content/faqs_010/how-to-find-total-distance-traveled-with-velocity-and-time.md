<p>Distance is an important concept in physics and mathematics. It is used to measure the size of objects, the speed of objects or the time it takes to travel a certain distance. <h2>What is Velocity?</h2>

<p>Velocity is defined as a vector quantity that measures the rate of change of an object’s position. It is the speed of an object in a given direction. Velocity is measured in meters per second (m/s) or kilometers per hour (km/h).</p>

<h2>What is Time?</h2>

<p>Time is an abstract concept that measures the passage of events. It is measured in seconds (s), minutes (m), hours (h) or days (d).</p>

<h2>How to Find Total Distance Traveled with Velocity and Time</h2>

<p>The total distance traveled with velocity and time can be calculated using the following equation:</p>

<p>Distance = Velocity x Time</p>

<p>For example, if a car is traveling at a velocity of 10 m/s and the time taken is 10 seconds, the total distance traveled is 100 meters.</p>

<h2>Calculating Distance Traveled with Acceleration</h2>

<p>The total distance traveled with acceleration can be calculated using the following equation:</p>

<p>Distance = (Velocity x Time) + (1/2 x Acceleration x Time2)</p>

<p>For example, if a car is traveling at a velocity of 10 m/s and the time taken is 10 seconds, and the acceleration is 5 m/s2, the total distance traveled is 150 meters.</p>
