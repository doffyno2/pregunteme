<h2>What Is Collapsed Trachea?</h2>

<p>Collapsed trachea is a condition in which the trachea, or windpipe, is weakened, leading to a collapse of the airway. It is more common in small breeds, such as Chihuahuas and Pomeranians, but can also affect larger breeds. This condition can cause a range of symptoms, including coughing, gagging, and difficulty breathing. Over time, it can lead to more serious health problems, including heart failure.</p>

<h2>Causes of Collapsed Trachea</h2>

<p>The exact cause of collapsed trachea is not known. Some experts believe that the condition is genetic, while others believe that it is a result of environmental factors, such as air pollution or secondhand smoke. It is also possible that the condition is caused by an abnormality in the cartilage that makes up the trachea or by an infection or injury.</p>

<h2>Diagnosis and Treatment of Collapsed Trachea</h2>

<p>In order to diagnose collapsed trachea, your vet will use x-rays and other imaging tests to look for signs of the condition. They may also listen to your dog’s lungs for a characteristic “goose honk” sound that can indicate the presence of collapsed trachea. Once the condition is confirmed, your vet may recommend medical or surgical treatment.</p>

<h2>Medical Treatment for Collapsed Trachea</h2>

<p>Medical treatment for collapsed trachea usually involves the use of medications such as bronchodilators and corticosteroids to help reduce inflammation and open the airway. In addition, your vet may recommend lifestyle changes, such as avoiding activities that could worsen the condition, such as running or jumping. In some cases, a cough suppressant may be prescribed to help reduce the coughing.</p>

<h2>Surgery for Collapsed Trachea</h2>

<p>In some cases, surgery may be necessary to help relieve the symptoms of collapsed trachea. The most common type of surgery is a tracheal stent, in which a small tube is inserted into the trachea to help keep it open. This procedure can help to reduce the symptoms of the condition and improve the quality of life for the dog. However, it is important to note that surgery is not always successful and can be expensive.</p>
