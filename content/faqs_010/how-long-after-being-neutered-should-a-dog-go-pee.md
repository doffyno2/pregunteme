<h2>What is Neutering?</h2>

Neutering is the surgical removal of a dog’s reproductive organs. This procedure is typically performed on male dogs and is sometimes referred to as castration or neutering. The procedure is usually done when the dog is between 6 and 18 months of age.

<h2>Why Neuter a Dog?</h2>

There are several reasons why a person may choose to have a dog neutered. Neutering helps to reduce the pet population, as it prevents the dog from reproducing. It also eliminates the potential for certain health problems, such as testicular cancer. Neutering can also help reduce certain types of behavior problems, such as roaming and aggression.
