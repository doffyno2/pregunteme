Gefilte fish is a traditional Jewish dish that is served on special occasions such as Shabbat, Passover, and Rosh Hashanah. Gefilte fish is typically made from ground carp, whitefish, or pike, and is flavored with onion, carrot, and other seasonings. It is commonly served as a starter or side dish. Gefilte fish can be made in many ways, with or without eggs, and can be served either cooked or raw. One of the most popular ways to serve it is cooked in a broth or borscht.

Cooked gefilte fish can be stored in the refrigerator for several days, so long as it is covered tightly in an airtight container. But just how long does cooked gefilte fish last in the fridge?

<h2>How Long Does Cooked Gefilte Fish Last in the Fridge?</h2>
Cooked gefilte fish can be stored in the refrigerator for up to four days. However, it is important to ensure that the fish is kept cold. If the gefilte fish is not kept cold, it can spoil quickly. The best way to keep gefilte fish cold is to store it in an airtight container with an ice pack.

<h2>How to Tell If Gefilte Fish Has Gone Bad</h2>
It is important to inspect cooked gefilte fish before consuming it. If the fish has a slimy or off-putting odor, it has likely gone bad and should be discarded. If the fish has a slightly fishy smell, it is still safe to eat.

<h2>How to Store Gefilte Fish</h2>
When storing cooked gefilte fish, it is important to keep it cold. The best way to do this is to store it in an airtight container with an ice pack. It is also important to keep the fish away from other foods that may have strong odors, as these can transfer to the fish.
