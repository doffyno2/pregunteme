<h2>Are Scrambled Eggs with Milk Safe for Dogs?</h2>

<p>While eggs are generally safe for dogs to eat, adding milk to scrambled eggs can be dangerous. Milk can cause gastrointestinal upset and even lead to more serious problems like pancreatitis. If your dog does eat scrambled eggs with milk, look out for signs of distress such as vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. If you notice any of these symptoms, contact your veterinarian immediately. </p>

<h2>How to Make Scrambled Eggs for Dogs</h2>

<p>If you want to give your dog scrambled eggs as a treat, it is important to prepare them properly. Start by cracking the eggs into a bowl and whisking them until they are light and fluffy. If you want to add flavor, you can add a bit of chopped fresh herbs or a pinch of salt. Then, heat a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat and spray it lightly with cooking oil. Pour the eggs into the skillet and cook them until they are firm and cooked through. Be sure to avoid adding milk, butter, or other dairy products to the eggs. Once the eggs are cooked, let them cool before serving them to your dog. </p>

<p>Raw eggs should not be fed to dogs. Raw eggs may contain bacteria such as Salmonella or E. coli, which can be harmful to dogs.
