<h2>Why Do Tigers Climb Trees?</h2>

Tigers are apex predators and generally they have no need to climb trees. However, there are a few scenarios where tigers may choose to climb a tree:

<h3>To Escape Predators</h3>

When tigers feel threatened, they may climb a tree as a way to escape predators. <h3>To Reach Prey</h3>

Tigers may also climb trees in order to reach their prey. Smaller animals such as birds and monkeys are known to inhabit trees, so tigers may climb the tree in order to catch them. Tigers are also adept climbers, so they can easily scale the trunk of a tree and reach the higher branches.

Tigers are able to climb trees with their powerful claws and strong muscles. Tigers have retractable claws, which they can extend to dig into the bark of a tree and help them climb. Tigers also have incredibly strong hind legs, which they use to push themselves up the tree and gain momentum.
