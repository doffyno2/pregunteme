Onions may be a popular flavor enhancer in your kitchen, but you'll want to avoid giving them to your pup. Dogs can't tolerate onions and onion powder, and even a small amount can be toxic. <h2>How Much Onion Powder is Toxic to Dogs?</h2>

Onion powder, like other forms of onion, contains a substance called thiosulfate. This compound can be toxic to dogs in even the smallest amounts. In general, it's best to avoid giving your pup any onions or onion powder. However, if your pup has ingested some onion powder, you'll want to pay attention to the amount.

Ingesting as little as 15 to 30 grams of onion powder per kilogram of body weight can be toxic to dogs. For a 20-pound pup, this would equate to about 3 to 5 grams of onion powder. In other words, if a 20-pound pup eats just a teaspoon of onion powder, it could be toxic.

<h2>Signs of Onion Toxicity in Dogs</h2>

If your pup has ingested onion powder, there are a few signs you should watch out for. The most common signs of onion toxicity in dogs are:

<ul>
<li>Vomiting</li>
<li>Diarrhea</li>
<li>Lack of appetite</li>
<li>Pale gums</li>
<li>Lethargy</li>
</ul>

If you notice any of these signs, you'll want to take your pup to the vet immediately.

<h2>How to Prevent Onion Poisoning in Dogs</h2>

The best way to prevent onion poisoning in dogs is to avoid giving them onions or onion powder. If your pup has gotten into the onion powder, it's important to act quickly. The sooner you get your pup to the vet, the better the chances of a full recovery.
