<h2>Why Is Waiting Important?</h2>

<p>Waiting between primer and base coat is essential for a number of reasons. Primer is meant to provide a protective barrier between the metal and the paint. If the primer is not given enough time to dry, the paint will not adhere properly and could easily peel away. Additionally, waiting allows the primer to fully harden, creating an even surface for the base coat to adhere to. Skipping the waiting period could lead to a number of issues, including bubbling, flaking and peeling.</p>

<h2>How Long Should I Wait?</h2>

<p>The length of time you should wait between primer and base coat varies depending on the type of primer used, the temperature and humidity of the environment, and the type of paint you are using. Generally, you should wait for a minimum of 24 hours for the primer to dry before applying the base coat. However, some primers require more time to reach their optimal hardness. If this is the case, you should wait up to 48 hours before applying the base coat.</p>
