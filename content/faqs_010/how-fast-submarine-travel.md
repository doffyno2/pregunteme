<h2>What Factors Affect Submarine Speed?</h2>

When it comes to how fast do submarines travel, a number of factors can come into play. These include the size of the submarine, the type of propulsion system, and the water conditions. 

The size of a submarine can influence its speed. Generally, larger submarines can travel faster than smaller ones. This is because they have more space for engines and propulsion systems, which can power them through the water at faster speeds. 

The type of propulsion system used is also important. Submarines typically use either diesel engines or nuclear power. Diesel engines can be used to power the submarine while submerged, but they are not suitable for long-distance travel. Nuclear-powered submarines are much more powerful and can travel at faster speeds for longer distances.

The water conditions also play a role in how fast a submarine can travel. Underwater currents, temperatures, and salinity can all affect the speed of the submarine. For example, warmer water is more buoyant and can help the submarine move faster. 

<h2>How Fast Can Submarines Travel?</h2>

The speed of a submarine depends on its size, propulsion system, and water conditions. Smaller submarines can typically travel at speeds of up to 20 knots (about 23 mph). Larger submarines with nuclear power can travel at speeds of up to 40 knots (about 46 mph). 

Submarines can also be equipped with turbo-electric or turbo-prop propulsion systems, which can help them travel at even faster speeds. The fastest submarine on record is the Soviet-era K-222, which reached a top speed of 44.7 knots (about 51 mph).
