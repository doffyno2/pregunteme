<h2>What Are Electrolytes?</h2>

Electrolytes are minerals that are found in some foods and drinks. They are important for maintaining the body's fluid balance, and for proper functioning of the muscles, heart, and other body systems. Electrolytes are also important for maintaining energy levels, as they help supply the body with energy.

<h2>What Are the Benefits of Electrolytes?</h2>

Electrolytes have many benefits for the body. They help to regulate the body's fluid levels, balance the acidity of the blood, move nutrients around the body, and provide energy. Electrolytes are also important for muscle contraction, enabling the body to move and exercise.

<h2>What Electrolytes Are in Orange Juice?</h2>

Orange juice contains several electrolytes, including sodium, potassium, calcium, and magnesium. Sodium helps to regulate fluid levels in the body, while potassium helps to balance the acidity of the blood. Calcium and magnesium are important for muscle contraction and energy production. All of these electrolytes play an important role in the body's overall health and function.

<h2>How Much Electrolytes Are in Orange Juice?</h2>

The amount of electrolytes in orange juice varies, depending on the type of orange juice. For example, a cup of freshly squeezed orange juice can contain up to 20 milligrams of sodium, up to 400 milligrams of potassium, up to 20 milligrams of calcium, and up to 5 milligrams of magnesium. These amounts are much higher than those found in other juices or beverages.
