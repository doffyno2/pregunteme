<h2>What is Slime?</h2>

Slime is a unique tire sealant that’s designed to help cyclists prevent flat tires. It’s a green liquid that you pour into your bicycle tires to fill any holes or punctures that may occur as you ride. The slime will fill the puncture and quickly seal it, preventing air from escaping.

Slime is designed to be used on all types of bicycles and is safe to use on all road, mountain, and hybrid bike tires. It’s also safe to use with all types of wheel rims and inner tubes. 

<h2>Why Use Slime?</h2>

Slime is an effective way to prevent flat tires and keeps your tires inflated and rolling. It’s also easy to use and doesn’t require you to carry any tools or additional items when you ride.

<h2>How Much Slime Do You Need?</h2>

The amount of slime you need for your bicycle tires depends on the size of the tires. Generally, you will need between 8-12 ounces of slime for a standard 26-inch tire. For a 29-inch tire, you will need 14-16 ounces of slime. For a 27.5-inch tire, you will need between 10-14 ounces of slime. You should also adjust the amount of slime you use depending on the type of terrain you’re riding. If you’re riding in rough terrain, you may need more slime to fill any potential punctures. 

<h2>How to Apply Slime to Your Tires</h2>

Applying slime to your tires is easy. First, check your tires to make sure they’re properly inflated. Then, remove the valve core from the tire and insert the straw that comes with your slime bottle into the valve stem. Squeeze the bottle until the slime begins to fill the tire.
