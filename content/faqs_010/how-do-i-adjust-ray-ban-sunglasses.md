<h2>Step One: Check the Fit</h2>

<p>The first step in adjusting Ray Ban sunglasses is to check the fit. This includes checking the size of the frames and the distance between the lenses and your face. If the frames are too small or too large, the sunglasses will not fit properly. The distance between the lenses and your face should be about one inch. If the distance is too far or too close, you will need to adjust the frames.</p>

<h2>Step Two: Adjust the Nose Pads</h2>

<p>If you need to adjust the frames, the next step is to adjust the nose pads. Most Ray Ban sunglasses have adjustable nose pads that can be moved up or down to achieve the perfect fit. To adjust the nose pads, start by loosening the screws on the nose pads. Then, slide the nose pads up or down to adjust the distance between the lenses and your face. Finally, tighten the screws to secure the nose pads in place.</p>

<h2>Step Three: Adjust the Temples</h2>

<p>The next step in adjusting Ray Ban sunglasses is to adjust the temples. The temples are the arms of the glasses that go over your ears. If the temples are too loose or too tight, your sunglasses will not provide the best protection or the most comfortable fit. To adjust the temples, start by loosening the screws on the temples. Then, slide the temples up or down to adjust the fit. Finally, tighten the screws to secure the temples in place.</p>

<h2>Step Four: Check the Fit Again</h2>
