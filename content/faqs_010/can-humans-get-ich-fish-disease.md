<h2>What is Ich?</h2>

<p>Ich is a parasite that infects both fresh and saltwater fish. It is caused by the protozoan Ichthyophthirius multifiliis, which attaches itself to the fish's skin and gills. Once attached, the parasite feeds on the fish's blood and mucous, causing white spots (called cysts) to develop all over the fish's body. The Ich parasite is highly contagious and can spread quickly between fish, as well as between different tanks.</p>

<p>The symptoms of Ich are easy to spot. The most common symptom is the appearance of white cysts on the fish's body. These cysts may be as small as a grain of salt or as large as a pea.
