<h2>What Causes Yeast Infections in Dogs?</h2>

Yeast infections in dogs are usually caused by an overgrowth of the fungus Candida albicans. This fungus is normally present in small amounts in your dog’s body, but it can overgrow when the environment is favorable. Common factors that can lead to an overgrowth of yeast include:

<ul>
  <li>A weakened immune system</li>
  <li>Excessive moisture on the skin</li>
  <li>An unbalanced diet</li>
  <li>The use of certain medications</li>
  <li>Exposure to environmental toxins</li>
</ul>

Yeast infections can affect any part of your dog’s body, but they are most common on the skin and in the ears. If left untreated, a yeast infection can spread and cause more serious health problems.

<h2>Symptoms of Yeast Infections in Dogs</h2>

If your dog is suffering from a yeast infection, you may notice one or more of the following symptoms:

<ul>
  <li>Itchy skin</li>
  <li>A rash or red, inflamed skin</li>
  <li>Hair loss</li>
  <li>Excessive licking or chewing of the skin</li>
  <li>Bad odor</li>
  <li>Ear infections</li>
  <li>Diarrhea or vomiting</li>
</ul>

If your dog is exhibiting any of these symptoms, it is important to take them to the vet to get a proper diagnosis and treatment.
