Popcorn is one of the most beloved snacks around the world, with many people enjoying it as a treat when watching their favorite movies or tv shows. But how many carbs are in a cup of popped popcorn? It may surprise you to learn that popcorn is actually a surprisingly low-carb snack.

<h2>The Basics of Carbs</h2>

Before we get into the specifics of how many carbs are in a cup of popcorn, let's take a look at the basics of carbs. Carbs are one of the three main macronutrients, along with proteins and fats. Carbs are the body's preferred energy source, and they are broken down into glucose, which is then used by the body for energy. Carbs are found in a variety of foods, including grains, fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

<h2>Carbs in a Cup of Popped Popcorn</h2>

So, how many carbs are in a cup of popped popcorn? The answer is surprisingly low - only 5.7 grams per cup. This is significantly lower than many other types of snacks, such as chips and cookies. In fact, popcorn has fewer carbs than many fruits and vegetables, such as apples and potatoes. The low carb content of popcorn makes it an ideal snack for people trying to watch their carb intake.

<h2>Carbs in Unpopped Popcorn</h2>

But what about unpopped popcorn? How many carbs are in a cup of unpopped popcorn? The answer is a little bit higher - about 17 grams per cup. This is still relatively low compared to other snacks, and is something to consider if you are watching your carb intake.

<h2>Popcorn Nutrition Facts</h2>

It's important to note that these numbers are based on plain, air-popped popcorn. If you are eating flavored popcorn, the carb content may be higher due to the added ingredients. Additionally, popcorn is a good source of fiber, with 3.5 grams per cup. It also contains a small amount of protein (2.5 grams per cup) and fat (1.5 grams per cup). 

<h2>Health Benefits of Popcorn</h2>

In addition to being a low-carb snack, popcorn also has several other health benefits. It is a whole grain, which means it is a good source of vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients. Additionally, popcorn is a good source of antioxidants, which can help protect the body from damage caused by free radicals. Finally, popcorn is a good source of fiber, which can help keep you feeling full longer and aid in digestion.
