<h2>What Causes Car Windows to Fog Up When It Rains?</h2>

<p>Car windows fogging up when it rains is caused by the combination of cooler outside temperatures and warm, humid air inside the car. As the rainwater evaporates, it cools the air inside the car to the temperature of the outside environment. This causes condensation to form on the windows, which in turn causes the windows to fog up.</p>

<p>In addition to rain, car windows can also fog up when it's cold outside and the car is running. The condensation is caused by the same combination of warm, humid air inside the car and the cooler outside temperature. The engine of the car produces heat which increases the temperature of the air inside the car. When this warm air meets the cooler outside temperature, it causes condensation to form on the windows.</p>

<h2>How to Prevent Car Windows From Fogging Up When It Rains</h2>

<p>One of the best ways to prevent car windows from fogging up when it rains is to keep the air in your car as dry as possible. This can be done by using a dehumidifier or a desiccant pouch. These devices work to absorb moisture from the air, thus keeping the air inside your car dry and preventing windows from fogging up.</p>

<p>Another way to keep your car windows from fogging up when it rains is to keep the temperature inside your car consistent. If the temperature inside your car is much warmer or cooler than the temperature outside, it can create a significant difference in temperature between the inside and outside of the car, which can lead to condensation forming on the windows. The easiest way to keep the temperature inside your car consistent is to use a car fan or air conditioner.</p>

<p>Finally, you can also use anti-fog products to keep your car windows from fogging up when it rains. These products are designed to form a barrier between the window and the moisture in the air, thus preventing fog from forming on the windows. These products are easy to apply and can be found in most auto supply stores.</p>
