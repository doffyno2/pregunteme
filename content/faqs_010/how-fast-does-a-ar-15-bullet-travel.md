<p>AR-15 rifles are popular weapons for hunters and recreational shooters alike. They are typically lightweight, easy to use and require minimal maintenance. But how fast does an AR 15 bullet travel? The answer to this question depends on the particular rifle, ammunition and environmental conditions.</p>

<h2>The Average Speed of an AR-15 Bullet</h2>

<p>An AR-15 bullet has a typical muzzle velocity of between 2,500-3,000 feet per second (fps). It is important to note that this is not the actual speed of the bullet as it travels through the air, but rather the speed of the bullet as it exits the barrel. The actual speed of the bullet will depend on the distance it has traveled, its weight, the air resistance it encounters and other factors.</p>

<h2>The Effect of Ammunition on Bullet Speed</h2>

<p>The speed of an AR-15 bullet is largely determined by the type of ammunition used. Different types of ammunition produce different muzzle velocities. For example, a .223 Remington cartridge typically produces a muzzle velocity of between 2,600-3,200 fps. A 5.56 NATO cartridge, on the other hand, typically produces a muzzle velocity of between 2,900-3,400 fps.</p>

<h2>The Effect of Environmental Conditions on Bullet Speed</h2>

<p>Environmental conditions can also dramatically affect the speed of an AR-15 bullet. Wind, humidity, temperature and altitude can all have an effect on the bullet's speed. For example, a bullet fired in the wind will typically travel slower than a bullet fired in still air. Similarly, a bullet fired in cold weather will typically travel slower than a bullet fired in warm weather.</p>

<h2>The Maximum Speed of an AR-15 Bullet</h2>

<p>The maximum speed of an AR-15 bullet is typically around 3,400 fps. This is the maximum muzzle velocity of the most powerful ammunition typically used in an AR-15 rifle. It is important to note that this speed is only achieved in ideal conditions. In the real world, the speed of an AR-15 bullet will typically be lower than this.</p>
