<p>If you have a spray bottle that won't spray, you're not alone; this is a common problem. The good news is that it's usually a simple fix that doesn't require a lot of time or money. In this article, we'll explore some of the common causes of a spray bottle that won't spray, along with some easy solutions.</p>

<h2>Common Causes of a Spray Bottle That Won't Spray</h2>

<p>When it comes to spray bottles, there are several common causes of a bottle that won't spray. The most common cause is a clogged nozzle, which is easily fixed by cleaning the nozzle or replacing it if necessary. Another common cause is an airlock, which can be fixed by removing the airlock and replacing it with a new one. Finally, a weak pump can also be the cause of a spray bottle that won't spray, and this can be fixed by replacing the pump.</p>

<h2>Cleaning the Nozzle</h2>

<p>If you suspect that the nozzle is clogged, the first thing you should do is clean it. To do this, you'll need a small piece of wire, such as a paperclip or a toothpick. Gently insert the wire into the nozzle and gently twist it around to clear any blockages. You may have to do this a few times to get the nozzle clear.</p>

<h2>Replacing the Nozzle</h2>

<p>If the nozzle is too badly clogged or damaged, it may need to be replaced. To do this, unscrew the nozzle from the bottle and replace it with a new one. Make sure to use a nozzle that is compatible with the bottle in order to avoid any leaks.</p>

<h2>Removing the Air Lock</h2>

<p>An air lock can also be the cause of a spray bottle that won't spray. To remove an air lock, you'll need to unscrew the top of the bottle and remove the airlock. Once the airlock is removed, replace it with a new one and reassemble the bottle.</p>

<h2>Replacing the Pump</h2>

<p>If the pump is weak or broken, it will need to be replaced. To do this, you'll need to unscrew the top of the bottle, remove the pump and replace it with a new one. Make sure to use a pump that is compatible with the bottle in order to avoid any leaks.</p>
