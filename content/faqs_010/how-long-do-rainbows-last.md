<h2>What Causes a Rainbow?</h2>

<p>Rainbows are caused by sunlight reflecting off of individual droplets of rainfall. When the sun shines on the rain, the tiny droplets of water act like tiny prisms, bending and refracting the sunlight into its component colors. This is why rainbows appear in a range of colors. The most common colors are red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.</p>
