<h2>What is a Percent Grade?</h2>

<p>A percent grade is a measure of the steepness of a hill or incline expressed as a percentage. It is equal to the vertical rise of the hill divided by its horizontal distance (rise/run). For example, a hill with a 10 foot vertical rise and a 100 foot horizontal distance would have a 10% grade (10/100 = 0.1 x 100 = 10%).</p>

<h2>How to Calculate Percent Grade of a Hill</h2>

<p>To calculate the percent grade of a hill, you'll need to measure the vertical rise and the horizontal distance. You'll also need a calculator or a smartphone with a calculator app. Here's what you do:</p>

<ul>
<li>Measure the vertical rise of the hill. This is the difference in elevation from the bottom to the top. </li>
<li>Measure the horizontal distance. This is the distance from the bottom of the hill to the top. </li>
<li>Divide the vertical rise by the horizontal distance. </li>
<li>Multiply the result by 100 to convert the decimal to a percentage. </li>
</ul>
