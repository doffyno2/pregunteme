<p>If you’re a fan of the Fallout series, you know that Fallout 4 is one of the best games ever released. But, like with any game, it can be prone to crashing. So what do you do when you’re playing Fallout 4 and it keeps crashing?</p>

<p>First and foremost, you need to make sure your computer meets the minimum system requirements for Fallout 4. If your hardware is not up to the task, then you’ll experience a lot of crashing. You can find the system requirements for Fallout 4 on the official website or in the game’s manual.</p>

<h2>Verify the Game Files</h2>

<p>If your computer meets the minimum system requirements, the next thing you should do is verify the game files. This will check the game’s installation and make sure all of the necessary files are present and in the correct version. To do this, open the <b>Steam</b> client and select <b>Library</b>. Then right-click on Fallout 4 and select <b>Properties</b>. Select the <b>Local Files</b> tab and click <b>Verify Integrity of Game Files</b>. Steam will now verify all of the game files and download any missing or incorrect files.</p>

<h2>Update Your Graphics Drivers</h2>

<p>Another thing you should do to prevent Fallout 4 from crashing is to make sure your graphics drivers are up to date. Outdated or incorrect graphics drivers can cause a lot of problems, including crashes. To update your graphics drivers, you need to know what type of graphics card you have. You can find this information by opening the <b>Device Manager</b> in Windows and looking for the <b>Display Adapters</b> category. Once you know what type of graphics card you have, you can download the latest drivers from the manufacturer’s website.</p>

<h2>Disable Mods</h2>

<p>If you’re using any mods with Fallout 4, you should disable them and see if that fixes the crashing. Mods are a great way to customize the game, but they can also introduce instability and cause the game to crash. To disable mods, open the <b>Steam</b> client and select <b>Library</b>. Right-click on Fallout 4 and select <b>Properties</b>. Select the <b>Updates</b> tab and uncheck the <b>Enable Modding</b> box. This will disable all mods for Fallout 4.</p>

<h2>Repair the Game</h2>

<p>If none of the above solutions work, you can try repairing the game. This will check all of the game files and make sure they are in the correct version. To repair the game, open the <b>Steam</b> client and select <b>Library</b>. Right-click on Fallout 4 and select <b>Properties</b>. Select the <b>Local Files</b> tab and click <b>Verify Integrity of Game Files</b>. Steam will now check all of the game files and repair any that are missing or incorrect.</p>

<h2>Reinstall the Game</h2>

<p>If none of the above solutions work, the last resort is to reinstall the game. This will completely remove Fallout 4 from your computer and then reinstall it from scratch. To reinstall the game, open the <b>Steam</b> client and select <b>Library</b>. Right-click on Fallout 4 and select <b>Delete Local Content</b>. Then click <b>Install</b> to reinstall the game.</p>
