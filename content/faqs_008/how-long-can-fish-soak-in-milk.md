Have you ever wondered if it was possible to soak fish in milk? Is it a safe or even healthy idea? In this article, we will discuss the basics of fish soaking in milk and how long it is safe for the fish to be left in the milk.

<h2>Why Soak Fish in Milk?</h2>

Soaking fish in milk is a traditional method of removing the fishy smell from the fish. It is also said to help tenderize the fish, making it easier to cook and eat. It is thought that the lactic acid in the milk helps to break down the proteins in the fish and make them more digestible.

<h2>What Kind of Milk Should I Use?</h2>

When soaking fish in milk, it is important to use full-fat milk. This is because the fat in the milk helps to tenderize the fish. Skimmed or low-fat milks will not provide the same tenderizing effect.

<h2>How Long Should I Soak The Fish?</h2>

Fish should be soaked in the milk for a maximum of two hours. This is because the lactic acid in the milk can start to break down the proteins in the fish and make it mushy if left for too long. After two hours, the fish should be removed from the milk and rinsed in cold water.

<h2>Are There Other Benefits to Soaking Fish in Milk?</h2>

There are other benefits to soaking fish in milk. It is believed that the lactic acid in the milk helps to kill any bacteria present on the fish, making it safer to eat. It is also thought to help preserve the fish, making it last longer in the fridge.

<h2>Is Soaking Fish in Milk Safe?</h2>

Soaking fish in milk is generally considered to be safe, provided that the fish is not left in the milk for longer than two hours. It is important to rinse the fish thoroughly before cooking it to remove any residual milk.
