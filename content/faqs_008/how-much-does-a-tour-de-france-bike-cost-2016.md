<h2>What Type of Bike Do Professional Cyclists Use for Tour De France?</h2>

<p>Professional cyclists participating in Tour de France typically use a road bike that is designed for racing. These bikes are typically lightweight and designed to reduce wind resistance. The frame is usually made of aluminum, titanium, or carbon fiber, and most of the components are made of similar lightweight materials. The components of the bike are also designed to be as aerodynamic as possible.</p>

<h2>What Components Make Up a Tour De France Bike?</h2>

<p>The components of a Tour de France bike can vary greatly depending on the rider’s preferences and budget. However, most professional cyclists use a combination of lightweight components that are designed to increase speed and reduce wind resistance. These components typically include a lightweight frame, aero handlebars, an aerodynamic saddle, and aero wheels. Additionally, professional cyclists often use a 11-speed cassette, shifters, and a drivetrain that allows them to quickly and efficiently change gears.</p>
