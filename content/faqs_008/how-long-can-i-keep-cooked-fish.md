<p>Cooked fish is a delicious and nutritious meal that many people enjoy. However, it can quickly spoil if it is not stored correctly. Knowing how to store cooked fish and how long it can be kept is essential for keeping it safe to eat. </p>

<h2>How to Store Cooked Fish</h2>

<p>Cooked fish should be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Make sure to place the fish in the coldest part of the refrigerator, which is usually in the back. It should be eaten within two days of being cooked. If it is not going to be consumed within that time, it should be frozen. </p>

<p>When freezing cooked fish, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or place it in an airtight container. Label the container with the date it was cooked, and use it within four to six months for best results. </p>

<h2>What to Look for When Eating Cooked Fish</h2>

<p>When eating cooked fish, it is important to look for any signs of spoilage. Cooked fish should not have a strong fishy smell. If it does, it is likely spoiled and should be discarded. It should also not have any unusual color or texture. If it looks slimy or discolored, it should not be eaten. </p>

<h2>How Long Can Cooked Fish Be Left Out?</h2>

<p>Cooked fish should not be left out at room temperature for longer than two hours. If it is left out longer than that, it could become a breeding ground for bacteria and should be discarded. </p>

<h2>Tips for Storing Cooked Fish</h2>
