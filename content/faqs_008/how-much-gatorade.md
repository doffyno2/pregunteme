Gatorade is a sports drink that was developed in 1965 by researchers at the University of Florida. Now, Gatorade is a top-selling sports drink in the United States and is popular among athletes and everyday exercisers alike. 

The drink contains a mixture of carbohydrates, electrolytes, and other beneficial ingredients, including sodium, potassium, and vitamins B6, B12, and C. Gatorade also contains electrolyte-rich ingredients like sodium chloride, magnesium chloride, and calcium chloride. The electrolytes help to replace lost minerals during exercise, while the carbohydrates provide energy.
