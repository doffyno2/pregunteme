<h2>What Is Furunculosis?</h2>

Furunculosis is a bacterial infection of the skin and hair of a dog that is caused by the bacterium Staphylococcus intermedius. It is most commonly seen in short-haired breeds, such as Labradors, Dachshunds, and Boxers. The infection results in small, red bumps on the dog’s skin that can become infected and painful. The infection can spread to other parts of the dog’s body, such as the ears, eyes, and mouth.

<h2>Symptoms of Furunculosis in Dogs</h2>

The most common symptom of furunculosis in dogs is the formation of small, red bumps on the skin. These bumps are usually itchy and painful and can become infected. Other common symptoms include hair loss, scabbing, pus-filled lesions, fever, and lethargy. If left untreated, the infection can spread to other parts of the dog’s body.

<h2>Causes of Furunculosis in Dogs</h2>

The most common cause of furunculosis in dogs is the bacterium Staphylococcus intermedius. This bacterium is typically found on the skin of healthy dogs and can become a problem when it enters the body through an open wound or other area of compromised skin. Other causes of furunculosis include allergies, parasites, and hormonal imbalances.

<h2>Diagnosis of Furunculosis in Dogs</h2>

In order to diagnose furunculosis in dogs, your veterinarian will need to perform a physical exam and take a sample of any pus or discharge from the lesions on your dog’s skin. They may also take a swab of the affected area to identify the type of bacteria causing the infection.

<h2>Treatment of Furunculosis in Dogs</h2>

The treatment of furunculosis in dogs will depend on the severity and type of infection. In most cases, the infection can be treated with antibiotics, anti-inflammatories, pain medication, and topical treatments such as ointments or shampoos. In more severe cases, surgery may be necessary.

<h2>How Much Does Furuncle Treatment Cost for Dogs?</h2>

The cost of furuncle treatment for dogs will vary depending on the type and severity of the infection, as well as the specific medications and treatments used. Generally speaking, the cost of treatment for furunculosis can range from a few hundred dollars to several thousand dollars. In some cases, the cost of the treatment may be covered by pet insurance.
