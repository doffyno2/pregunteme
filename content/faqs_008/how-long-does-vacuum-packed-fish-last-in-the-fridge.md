<h2>What Is Vacuum Packing? </h2>

Vacuum packing is a process of removing air from a package or container to preserve its contents. It is a great way to extend the shelf life of food and keep it fresh for longer. Vacuum packing is especially effective for seafood, as it prevents the growth of bacteria that can cause spoilage.

<h2>How Does Vacuum Packing Work? </h2>

The vacuum packing process involves sealing food in an airtight package and then removing the air. This creates a vacuum inside the package, which helps prevent the growth of bacteria and slows down the process of spoilage. Vacuum packed fish can last for up to three months in the fridge.
