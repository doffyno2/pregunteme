<h2>Causes of Subfloor Damage</h2>

The subfloor around a toilet can become damaged for a variety of reasons. The most common reason is improper installation of the toilet or the subfloor itself. If the toilet is not properly secured to the floor, the weight of the toilet can cause the subfloor to become cracked or warped over time. Another cause of subfloor damage is water damage from leaking pipes or a leaking toilet. If the subfloor is not sealed properly, water can seep in and cause the wood to rot or warp.
