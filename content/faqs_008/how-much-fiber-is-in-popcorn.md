<h2>What Is Fiber?</h2>

Fiber is a type of carbohydrate that the body cannot digest. Because of this, it is not broken down into the same form of energy as other carbohydrates. Instead, fiber passes through the body relatively unchanged. It is considered a type of indigestible carbohydrate, or resistant starch.

Fiber can be found in many foods, including fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, seeds, and grains. It is important for several reasons. It helps to slow down digestion, which can help you feel fuller for longer. It can also help to keep your blood sugar levels stable and can help to reduce the risk of heart disease.

<h2>How Much Fiber Is In Popcorn?</h2>

Popcorn is a whole grain, which means that it is a good source of fiber. A single cup of popcorn contains about 1.2 grams of fiber. This is about 5% of the recommended daily intake of fiber for adults. Compared to other grains, popcorn is not a particularly high source of fiber, but it can still be a valuable addition to your diet.
