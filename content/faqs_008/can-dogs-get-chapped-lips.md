<h2>What Causes Chapped Lips in Dogs?</h2>

<p>Chapped lips in dogs are caused by a variety of factors. Exposure to wind and cold can cause dryness and chapping, as can dehydration. Dogs who lick their lips excessively can also cause chapping. Allergies, hormonal imbalances, and certain medications can also lead to dryness and chapping.</p>

<h2>Symptoms of Chapped Lips in Dogs</h2>

<p>Chapped lips in dogs can vary in severity. In mild cases, the lips may be pink and dry, with some flaking. In more severe cases, the lips may be cracked and bleeding. Your dog may also show signs of discomfort such as excessive licking, drooling, and head shaking.</p>

<h2>Treating Chapped Lips in Dogs</h2>

<p>The first step in treating chapped lips in dogs is identifying and eliminating the underlying cause. If your dog is exposed to wind and cold, make sure they have access to a warm and sheltered area. If your dog is dehydrated, make sure they have access to plenty of fresh water. If your dog is licking their lips excessively, try to find out why and address the underlying cause.</p>

<p>You can also help your dog's lips heal by using a lip balm formulated specifically for dogs. These balms are typically made with natural ingredients such as coconut oil and shea butter, which help nourish and hydrate dry and chapped lips. Make sure to use a balm specifically formulated for dogs, as human lip balms can contain ingredients that are toxic to dogs.</p>
