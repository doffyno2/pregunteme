<h2>What Is Vacuum Sealing?</h2>

Vacuum sealing is a method of preserving food by removing the air from the food and sealing it in airtight plastic bags. This process helps to slow down the process of spoiling by preventing bacteria and other microorganisms from entering the food. It also helps to preserve the food's original flavor and texture.

<h2>How Long Does Vacuum Sealed Fish Last?</h2>

The shelf life of vacuum sealed fish depends on the type of fish and how it was caught and processed prior to being vacuum sealed. Generally, vacuum sealed fish will last up to two weeks in the refrigerator, and up to six months if stored in the freezer. However, if the fish has been frozen prior to being vacuum sealed, it will last up to one year in the freezer.

<h2>How To Tell If Vacuum Sealed Fish Is Still Good?</h2>

When it comes to determining whether or not vacuum sealed fish is still good to eat, there are a few simple tests you can do:

<h3>Smell Test</h3>

<h3>Texture Test</h3>

The second test is the texture test. If the fish feels slimy or mushy, it is likely that it has gone bad and should be thrown away.

<h3>Taste Test</h3>

The third test is the taste test. If the fish tastes off or has a strange flavor, it is likely that it has gone bad and should be thrown away.

When it comes to storing vacuum sealed fish, it is important to keep the fish in a cool, dry place. The fish should also be kept away from direct sunlight and any sources of heat or moisture. Additionally, it is important to keep the fish away from other foods to prevent cross-contamination.
