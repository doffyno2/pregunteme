<h2>What Is Lightning?</h2>

Lightning is a type of electrical discharge that occurs when the air around a particular area of the atmosphere becomes charged. This charge builds up until it is discharged in a sudden flash of bright light and thunder. Lightning is often accompanied by strong winds, heavy rains, and hail, and can cause significant damage to property and vegetation. Lightning strikes are also responsible for causing forest fires.

<h2>Types of Lightning</h2>

There are three main types of lightning: cloud-to-ground, cloud-to-cloud, and intra-cloud. Cloud-to-ground lightning occurs when a charge builds up in the clouds and is discharged to the ground. This is the most common type of lightning as it is responsible for most of the lightning strikes that occur. Cloud-to-cloud lightning occurs when a charge builds up in one cloud and is then discharged to another cloud. Intra-cloud lightning occurs when a charge builds up within a single cloud and is then discharged within the cloud itself.
