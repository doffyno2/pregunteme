<p>Dark oak planks can be used for a variety of projects, from building furniture to constructing walls and flooring. They are also a popular choice for home decor due to their dark, rich coloring which can add an air of sophistication to any room. The process of filling dark oak planks is a relatively simple one, but it does require some preparation and patience. Read on to learn how to fill dark oak planks.</p>

<h2>Gather the Necessary Materials</h2>

<p>Before you start filling your dark oak planks, you will need to make sure you have all the necessary materials. You will need a putty knife, a drill, sandpaper, and a wood filler. You will also need to choose a color of wood filler that matches the color of your dark oak planks. Once you have all these materials, you are ready to begin.</p>

<h2>Prepare the Planks</h2>

<p>The next step is to prepare the planks for filling. You will need to use the putty knife to scrape away any dirt, dust, or debris from the planks. You should also sand the planks lightly with sandpaper to ensure that the wood filler will adhere properly. Once the planks are prepared, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Drill the Holes</h2>

<p>The next step is to drill holes into the planks. You should space the holes about one-half inch apart and make sure they are deep enough to hold the wood filler. Once all the holes are drilled, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Fill the Holes</h2>

<p>Now it is time to fill the holes with wood filler. You should use a putty knife to carefully fill each hole with the wood filler, making sure to fill it completely. Once all the holes are filled, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Smooth the Filler</h2>

<p>Once the holes are filled, you will need to use a putty knife to smooth the wood filler. You should take care to make sure the wood filler is completely smooth and that all the holes are completely filled. Once the wood filler is smooth, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Sand the Planks</h2>

<p>The next step is to sand the planks. You should use a fine-grit sandpaper to lightly sand the planks, making sure to remove any excess wood filler. Once the planks are sanded, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Finish the Planks</h2>

<p>The last step is to finish the planks. You can finish the planks with a clear sealant or a stain of your choice. Once the planks are finished, you can enjoy your newly filled dark oak planks.</p>
