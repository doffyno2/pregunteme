<h2>What Are Ginger Snaps?</h2>

<p>Ginger snaps are a type of cookie made from a dough of flour, sugar, butter, and ground ginger. They’re typically round and crunchy, and some varieties may contain other spices like cinnamon or nutmeg. Ginger snaps are usually prepared with shortening or margarine and baked until they are golden brown. The cookies are typically served with tea, coffee, or milk.</p>

<h2>Nutritional Value of Ginger Snaps</h2>

<p>Ginger snaps are a source of carbohydrates, fats, and a few essential vitamins and minerals. One serving (about three ginger snaps) contains approximately:</p>

<ul>
  <li>110 calories</li>
  <li>5 grams of fat</li>
  <li>17 grams of carbohydrates</li>
  <li>1 gram of protein</li>
  <li>Small amounts of vitamins A and C, calcium, and iron</li>
</ul>

<h2>Possible Health Benefits of Ginger Snaps</h2>

<p>Although eating ginger snaps alone isn’t likely to provide any significant health benefits, some research suggests that consuming ginger in other forms may be beneficial for certain health conditions. Here are some potential health benefits of ginger:</p>

<h3>Ginger May Help Reduce Nausea and Morning Sickness</h3>

<p>Ginger has long been used to treat nausea and vomiting. Several studies have found that ginger may be effective in reducing nausea caused by motion sickness, chemotherapy, or pregnancy. For example, one study found that taking 500 milligrams of ginger daily was effective at reducing nausea in pregnant women with morning sickness.</p>

<h3>Ginger May Help Reduce Inflammation</h3>

<p>Ginger contains a compound called gingerol, which is believed to be responsible for its anti-inflammatory effects. Several studies have found that ginger may be effective at reducing inflammation, which can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and diabetes.</p>

<h3>Ginger May Help Lower Cholesterol Levels</h3>

<p>Ginger may also help lower cholesterol levels. A 2011 study found that taking two grams of ginger per day for 12 weeks was effective at reducing cholesterol levels in people with high cholesterol. The study also found that ginger was more effective than a placebo at reducing cholesterol levels.</p>

<h3>Ginger May Help Reduce Muscle Pain</h3>

<p>Ginger may also help reduce muscle pain caused by exercise. Several studies have found that taking ginger before or after exercise can reduce muscle pain and soreness. For example, one study found that taking 2 grams of ginger before exercise was effective at reducing muscle pain in runners.</p>

<h2>Are Ginger Snaps Healthy?</h2>

<p>Although ginger snaps may contain some of the beneficial compounds found in ginger, they’re not a health food. Ginger snaps are high in calories and sugar, and they’re best enjoyed in moderation. If you’re looking for a healthier way to get the benefits of ginger, try adding freshly grated ginger to your meals or drinks.</p>
