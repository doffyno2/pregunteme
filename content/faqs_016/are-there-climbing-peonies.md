<h2>What Are Peonies?</h2>

Peonies are perennial flowers that come in a variety of colors, sizes, and shapes. They are native to northern temperate areas of the world, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. Peonies have a reputation for being difficult to grow and maintain, but they are actually quite hardy and can be grown in many climates.

<h2>Types of Peonies</h2>

There are two main types of peonies: herbaceous and tree. Herbaceous peonies are the more common type of peony, and they grow in a bush-like form. They die back to the ground in the winter, and their stems are usually short and woody. Tree peonies are woody-stemmed shrubs that grow up to 10 feet tall and can have a lifespan of up to 50 years.

<h2>Are There Climbing Peonies?</h2>

Yes, there are! Climbing peonies are a type of herbaceous peony that grows in a vine-like form. They can reach heights of up to 15 feet and can be trained to climb up walls or trellises. Climbing peonies typically bloom in late spring to mid-summer and come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, red, and yellow.

<h2>How To Plant Climbing Peonies</h2>

Climbing peonies should be planted in the fall, when the soil is still warm. Choose a spot that gets full sun and has well-drained soil. Dig a hole that is twice as wide and deep as the root ball of the peony. Carefully remove the plant from its pot, and place it in the hole. Fill the hole with soil, and water it thoroughly.

<h2>Caring For Climbing Peonies</h2>

Climbing peonies require minimal care. Water them when the soil is dry, and fertilize them once a year in the spring. If you are training them to climb, use twine or wire to help them along. Prune them in the late winter or early spring to keep them healthy and shapely.
