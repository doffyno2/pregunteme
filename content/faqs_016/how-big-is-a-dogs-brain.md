<h2>The Anatomy of a Dog's Brain</h2>

<p>A dog's brain is made up of two main parts – the cerebrum and the cerebellum. The cerebrum is the part of the brain responsible for higher level thinking and complex decision making while the cerebellum is responsible for coordination and balance. Both parts of the brain are essential for canine behavior and communication.</p>

<p>A dog's brain is also divided into four lobes – the frontal, temporal, parietal and occipital. The frontal lobe controls the dog's emotions and cognitive abilities while the temporal lobe is involved in memory and language. The parietal lobe is involved in the sense of touch and spatial awareness while the occipital lobe is involved in vision.</p>

<p>A dog's brain also contains a number of other structures, including the hypothalamus and the amygdala. The hypothalamus is responsible for regulating hunger, thirst, and body temperature while the amygdala is involved in the processing of emotions. Both of these structures are essential for canine behavior and communication.</p>
