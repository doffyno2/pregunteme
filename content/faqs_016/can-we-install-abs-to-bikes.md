<h2>What is ABS?</h2>

<p>ABS, or Anti-lock Braking System, is a feature found on most cars and some motorcycles. It is designed to prevent the wheels from locking up during hard braking, which can cause skidding and loss of control. ABS works by monitoring the speed of the wheels and modulating the brake force applied to each wheel. If the wheel is spinning too fast, the brake force is reduced until the wheel speed is stabilized. This allows the rider to maintain control of the bike, even during emergency braking.</p>
