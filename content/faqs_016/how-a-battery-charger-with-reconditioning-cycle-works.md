<h2>What is Reconditioning?</h2>

<p>Reconditioning is a way to restore the original capacity of a battery by reversing the effects of sulfation, which is caused by the battery's inability to hold a charge. Sulfation occurs when the lead plates inside the battery become covered in lead sulfate crystals, which prevents the battery from being able to hold a charge. This can cause the battery's capacity to decrease and eventually fail.</p>

<p>Reconditioning helps to reverse this process by breaking down the lead sulfate crystals and allowing the battery to once again hold a charge. While reconditioning won't completely restore a battery's original capacity, it can extend its life by a few months or years.</p>

The charger also monitors the battery's voltage, temperature, and current in order to ensure that the battery is properly reconditioned.</p>
