<p>The short answer to the question “Can a Dog Take Advil PM?” is no. Advil PM is an over-the-counter medication that contains ibuprofen, which is not safe for dogs to take. Ibuprofen is toxic to animals, and can cause serious side effects such as vomiting, diarrhea, stomach ulcers, and kidney failure. It’s important to note that Advil PM also contains diphenhydramine, an antihistamine that can cause excessive sedation, heart palpitations, and seizures in dogs. If your dog has any of these symptoms after taking Advil PM, seek veterinary help immediately.</p>

Your veterinarian may also recommend other treatments, such as physical therapy, acupuncture, or laser therapy.</p>

<h2>Are There Any Over-the-Counter Medications Safe for Dogs?</h2>

<p>Yes, there are some over-the-counter medications that are safe for dogs. Acetaminophen (Tylenol) is generally safe in small doses, but should not be given to puppies or dogs with liver or kidney disease. Aspirin can be given in small doses, but should not be given to puppies or dogs with certain health conditions. Ask your veterinarian for advice before giving your dog any over-the-counter medications.</p>
