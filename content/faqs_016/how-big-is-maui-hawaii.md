<p>Maui is the second largest Hawaiian island, after the Big Island. It is a part of the Hawaiian archipelago in the Central Pacific and is known for its stunning beaches, rugged terrain, and beautiful waterfalls. The island measures 727 square miles in area, making it approximately the size of the state of Delaware. It is approximately 56 miles long, 26 miles wide, and is composed of two volcanoes - West Maui and East Maui. The highest point on the island is the summit of Haleakala, which stands at 10,023 feet above sea level. </p>

<h2>How Many Beaches Does Maui Have?</h2>

<p>Maui is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the world. The island boasts a whopping 79 miles of stunning coastline, with more than 30 beaches to explore. From the tranquil shores of Kapalua and Makena, to the world-renowned surfing spots of Hookipa and Honolua, there's a beach to suit every taste.</p>

<h2>What Activities Can I Do in Maui?</h2>

<p>Maui is a paradise for those seeking adventure. From the lush rainforests of Hana, to the majestic Haleakala volcano, there are plenty of activities to keep you busy. Nature lovers can explore the many trails, waterfalls, and pools that the island has to offer. For thrill seekers, there's snorkeling, scuba diving, and surfing, as well as parasailing, windsurfing, and kiteboarding. Other activities include horseback riding, ziplining, and kayaking. </p>

<h2>What Is the Weather Like in Maui?</h2>

<p>Maui is known for its year-round tropical climate. The temperatures rarely drop below 68 degrees Fahrenheit, and the average temperature is in the 70s and 80s. The island's beauty and mild temperatures make it a popular destination for travelers from all over the world. </p>

<h2>What Are the Different Regions of Maui?</h2>

<p>Maui is divided into five distinct regions. The West Maui region includes the popular beach towns of Lahaina, Kaanapali, and Kapalua. The South Maui region includes the towns of Kihei, Wailea, and Makena. The Central Maui region includes the towns of Wailuku, Kahului, and Pukalani. The Upcountry region includes the towns of Makawao, Kula, and Pukalani. Finally, the East Maui region includes the towns of Hana, Paia, and Haiku.</p>
