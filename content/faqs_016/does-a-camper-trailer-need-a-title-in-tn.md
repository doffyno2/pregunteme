<h2>What Is a Camper Trailer?</h2>

A camper trailer is a type of vehicle that is towed behind a motor vehicle and is used for recreational purposes. It is usually composed of a towing frame and chassis, an A-frame, and a fully enclosed shelter or body. Camper trailers are designed to provide temporary living quarters for recreational purposes, such as camping and travelling. 

<h2>Who Needs to Title a Camper Trailer in Tennessee?</h2>

Anyone who operates a camper trailer in Tennessee must title the vehicle. This includes individuals and businesses. Camper trailers must be titled and registered with the Department of Safety in order to be operated on public roads. 

<h2>What Documents Are Needed to Title a Camper Trailer in Tennessee?</h2>

In order to title a camper trailer in Tennessee, the owner must provide the following documents: 
<ul>
    <li>Title/registration application (TR-1)</li>
    <li>Bill of sale</li>
    <li>Proof of insurance</li>
    <li>Proof of identity</li>
    <li>Proof of residency</li>
    <li>Proof of ownership (if applicable)</li>
    <li>Proof of payment of sales tax (if applicable)</li>
    <li>Vehicle identification number (VIN) inspection certificate</li>
</ul>

<h2>What Is the Process for Titling a Camper Trailer in Tennessee?</h2>

The process for titling a camper trailer in Tennessee is as follows: 
<ul>
    <li>Gather the required documents and fill out the title/registration application (TR-1).</li>
    <li>Take the documents and application to your local county clerk's office.</li>
    <li>Pay the applicable title and registration fees.</li>
    <li>Take the title/registration application, bill of sale, and proof of insurance to a local Tennessee Department of Safety office.</li>
    <li>Have a vehicle identification number (VIN) inspection performed at the Department of Safety office.</li>
    <li>Once the VIN inspection is complete, the Department of Safety will issue a title and registration for the camper trailer.</li>
