<h2>What Causes Scratched Mirrors?</h2>

Scratched mirrors can be caused by a variety of things. Common culprits include cleaning products, abrasive cloths, and careless handling. Many mirrors are made of glass, which is a very delicate material. Any contact with a sharp object can scratch the surface, as can rubbing too hard with a cloth.

<h2>How to Repair Minor Scratches</h2>

If you notice a minor scratch on your mirror, you may be able to repair it yourself. Start by gathering the following materials: a clean, soft cloth, toothpaste, and a small bowl.

Begin by dampening the cloth with water. Squeeze a small amount of toothpaste onto the cloth and rub it into the scratch. This should fill in the scratch and make it less visible.

If the scratch is still visible, you can try using a glass polishing compound. This compound is available at most hardware stores and should be used in the same way as toothpaste. Rub the compound into the scratch in a circular motion until the scratch is no longer visible.

<h2>How to Repair Deeper Scratches</h2>

If the scratch on your mirror is deeper than just a surface scratch, you may need to take it to a professional for repair. Glass repair technicians have the tools and techniques required to repair deeper scratches. They may be able to remove the scratch or fill it with an epoxy resin.
