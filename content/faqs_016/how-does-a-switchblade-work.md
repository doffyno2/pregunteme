A switchblade is a type of knife with a folding or sliding blade contained in the handle which is opened automatically by a spring when a button, lever, or switch on the handle is activated. Originally known as an automatic knife, switchblade knives have been around since the early 1900s, and have become increasingly popular since then. This article will explain how a switchblade works and discuss the types of switchblade knives available.

<h2>The Mechanics of a Switchblade</h2>

A switchblade knife consists of two main components: the blade and the handle. The blade is usually made of steel, and is sharpened on one side. It is connected to a spring-loaded mechanism inside the handle, which opens and closes the blade when a button, lever, or switch on the handle is pressed.

When the switch is pressed, the spring inside the handle is released and the blade is propelled out of the handle. As the blade moves out of the handle, a locking mechanism engages, keeping the blade in the open position. When the switch is released, the spring inside the handle contracts, pulling the blade back into the handle.

<h2>Types of Switchblade Knives</h2>

There are a variety of types of switchblade knives available. The most common type is the side-opening knife, which opens when a lever on the side of the handle is pressed. This type of knife is often referred to as an Italian stiletto switchblade.

Another type of switchblade knife is the out-the-front (OTF) knife. This type of knife has a blade which moves out of the front of the handle when a switch on the handle is pressed. OTF knives are becoming increasingly popular, as they are easier to open and close than side-opening knives.

Finally, there are switchblade knives which open with a push button. These knives are similar to side-opening knives, but instead of a lever, a push button is used to open the blade. Push button switchblades are generally less expensive than other types of switchblades.
