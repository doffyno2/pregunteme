<p>Giardia is a nasty parasite that can infect both humans and animals. In dogs, it is usually referred to as <b>canine giardiasis</b>, and it can cause a variety of gastrointestinal problems including diarrhea, vomiting, and weight loss. If your dog has been diagnosed with giardia, it is important to pay attention to what you are feeding them in order to help them fight off the infection and stay healthy.</p>

<h2>What is Giardia?</h2>

<p>Giardia is a microscopic parasite that lives in the intestines of animals and humans. It is usually spread through contaminated food or water, or through contact with an infected animal. Giardia can cause a variety of gastrointestinal symptoms in infected animals, including diarrhea, vomiting, and weight loss. In addition to these physical symptoms, giardia can also cause psychological symptoms such as depression or anxiety. If left untreated, giardia can cause long-term health problems.</p>

<h2>What to Feed a Dog with Giardia</h2>

<p>When it comes to feeding a dog with giardia, it is important to remember that the goal is to reduce the amount of stress on their digestive system and help them fight off the infection. It is best to feed them a diet that is high in protein, low in fat, and easy to digest. This means avoiding foods that are high in fat and sugar, as well as processed foods. It is also important to make sure that they are getting enough essential vitamins and minerals, as these can help them fight off the infection.</p>

<h3>High-Protein Foods</h3>

<p>Lean meats such as chicken, turkey, and fish are all good sources of protein for dogs with giardia. <h3>Low-Fat Foods</h3>

<p>It is important to avoid feeding your dog foods that are high in fat, as this can make it harder for them to digest and can also contribute to weight gain.
