<h2>What Is Penicillin?</h2>

Penicillin is an antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections. It belongs to a class of antibiotics called beta-lactams. It works by inhibiting the growth of bacteria in the body. Penicillin is commonly used to treat skin infections, urinary tract infections, and upper respiratory infections.

<h2>How Is Penicillin Administered to Dogs?</h2>

Penicillin is typically administered to dogs in one of two ways: oral or injection. Oral penicillin is given in the form of tablets, liquid suspensions, or capsules. Injection of penicillin is usually done by a licensed veterinarian and is usually done intramuscularly or subcutaneously.

Penicillin is used to treat a variety of bacterial infections in dogs, including skin infections, urinary tract infections, and upper respiratory infections. It is also used to treat certain types of infections caused by bacteria that are resistant to other antibiotics.
