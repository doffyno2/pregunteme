RFID, or Radio Frequency Identification, is a technology that makes it possible to identify objects, animals, or people using radio waves. It is used in many areas such as access control, asset tracking, supply chain management, and payment processing. RFID technology works by sending electromagnetic waves to a tag attached to an object, which then sends an identification number back to the reader.

However, there are certain materials that can block the signal of RFID waves, making it difficult or even impossible for the reader to detect the tag. In this article, we will take a look at what materials block RFID signals and how to use them to protect your RFID tags.

<h2>What Is RFID?</h2>

Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) is a technology that allows objects, animals, or people to be identified using radio waves. It is used in many areas such as access control, asset tracking, supply chain management, and payment processing.

RFID technology works by sending electromagnetic waves to a tag attached to an object, which then sends an identification number back to the reader. The reader then interprets the data and can be used to identify the object, animal, or person.

The most common type of RFID tag is the passive tag, which does not require a power source and relies on the reader to power it. This type of tag is usually very small and can be attached to objects and even embedded in products.

<h2>What Materials Block RFID Signals?</h2>

<h3>Metals</h3>

<h3>Foam</h3>

Foam is another material that can be used to block RFID signals. Foam is a porous material that is able to absorb the radio waves sent out by the RFID reader, making it difficult for the reader to detect the tag.

<h3>Paper</h3>

Paper is also an effective material for blocking RFID signals. Paper is not a conductor of radio waves, so it is able to absorb the RFID waves and block them from reaching the tag. 

<h3>Fabrics</h3>
