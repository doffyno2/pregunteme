<h2>What is Map Rotation?</h2>

Map rotation is the process of changing the orientation of a map. It is used to orient the map in a different direction, usually to make it easier to navigate. This can be useful when trying to view a map from a different angle or get a better overall view of the area.

<h2>How To Rotate Map in Google Maps?</h2>

Rotating a map in Google Maps is easy and only takes a few steps. Here is how to do it:

<h3>Step 1: Open the Google Maps app</h3>

Open the Google Maps app on your mobile device or computer. You should see the map of the area you are currently in.

<h3>Step 2: Select the “Menu” icon</h3>

At the bottom of the screen, select the “Menu” icon, which looks like three horizontal lines. This will open the side menu.

<h3>Step 3: Select “Rotate Map”</h3>

In the side menu, select “Rotate Map”. This will open the map rotation tool.

<h3>Step 4: Rotate the Map</h3>

Using the rotational arrows, rotate the map by dragging the arrows in the desired direction. The map will rotate according to your input.

<h3>Step 5: Adjust the Map</h3>

Once the map is rotated, you can adjust the angle of the map by dragging the circular knob at the bottom of the rotation tool. This will allow you to fine-tune the angle of the map to your preference.

<h3>Step 6: Save the Map</h3>

When you are satisfied with the angle of the map, select the “Save” button at the bottom of the rotation tool. This will save your changes and the map will now remain in the rotated position until you rotate it again.
