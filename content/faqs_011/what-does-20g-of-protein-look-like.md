<h2>What is Protein?</h2>

<p>Protein is a nutrient made up of amino acids. It helps to maintain and build muscle, and is essential for the body to function. Protein is found in both animal-based and plant-based foods. Animal-based proteins include meats, fish, eggs, dairy products, and whey protein. Plant-based proteins include beans, lentils, nuts, and seeds.</p>

<h2>How Much Protein Should You Eat Each Day?</h2>

<p>The Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) for protein is 0.8g per kilogram of body weight per day for adults. This equates to around 56g per day for the average man and 46g per day for the average woman. However, those who are physically active may need more than this. For example, those who undertake regular resistance exercise may need 1.2–2.0 g/kg of body weight per day.</p>

<h2>What Does 20g of Protein Look Like?</h2>

<p>20g of protein is equivalent to:</p>

<ul>
<li>2 large eggs</li>
<li>1/2 cup of cooked beans or lentils</li>
<li>4 ounces of chicken or fish</li>
<li>1/2 cup of nuts or seeds</li>
<li>1 cup of nonfat Greek yogurt</li>
<li>2 tablespoons of peanut butter</li>
</ul>
