<h2>What Causes Dark Dog Poop?</h2>

<p>Dark dog poop is usually related to diet, specifically the foods your pup is eating. If your pup has recently started a new diet or has been given a food with a higher concentration of natural coloring agents, the end result could be darker, firmer stools. This is perfectly normal and nothing to be worried about.</p>

<p>It's also possible that your pup has eaten something dark-colored like soil, charcoal, or feces from other animals. This can cause the poop to become darker in color and may also result in some gastrointestinal distress. If you've noticed a sudden change in color and it's accompanied by vomiting or diarrhea, it's best to bring your pup to the vet for a checkup.</p>

<h2>Are There Any Health Concerns With Dark Dog Poop?</h2>

<p>Certain gastrointestinal illnesses, such as parvovirus or pancreatitis, can cause the poop to become darker in color. Darker stools can also be indicative of internal bleeding, which is why it's important to bring your pup to the vet if you notice a sudden change in color.</p>
