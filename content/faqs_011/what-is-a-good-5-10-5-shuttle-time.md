The 5-10-5 shuttle time is a test measuring an athlete's ability to quickly change direction, accelerate, and decelerate. It is a commonly used test to measure agility and is used in football, basketball, soccer, and other sports. The test involves running between two points five yards apart, touching each point, and then returning to the starting point. It is a timed test, and the athlete's score is the total time it takes them to complete the test.
