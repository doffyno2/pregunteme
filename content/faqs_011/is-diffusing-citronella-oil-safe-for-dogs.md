<h2>What Is Citronella Oil?</h2>

Citronella oil is extracted from a tropical grass called Cymbopogon nardus, or citronella grass. The oil is made up of several components, including citronellal, geraniol, and citronellol. When the oil is diffused, these components are released into the air, creating a pleasant aroma that is believed to repel insects.

<h2>How Does Citronella Oil Work?</h2>

Citronella oil works by masking the scents that attract insects. When diffused in the air, the oil releases a scent that masks the scents of humans and animals, making it difficult for insects to locate their targets. The oil also contains compounds that are believed to be toxic to insects, effectively killing them or repelling them.

<h2>Is Citronella Oil Safe For Dogs?</h2>

In general, citronella oil is considered safe for use around dogs, as long as it is used in diffused form. When used in diffused form, the oil is less likely to cause irritation or other adverse effects. However, it is important to note that citronella oil can cause irritation if it comes into contact with your dog's skin or eyes.
