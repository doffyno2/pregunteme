<p>Nicotine is a sticky substance that is found in cigarettes and other tobacco products. When it comes into contact with walls and ceilings, it can leave behind a yellowish-brown stain. Fortunately, there are a few simple steps you can take to remove nicotine stains from walls and ceilings. </p>

<h2>Step 1: Gather the Supplies You Will Need</h2>

<p>Before removing nicotine stains from walls and ceilings, you will need to gather the following supplies: a bucket, white vinegar, a sponge, a soft cloth, and a vacuum cleaner. </p>

<h2>Step 2: Prepare the Solution</h2>

<p>Mix one cup of white vinegar with one gallon of warm water in the bucket. </p>

<h2>Step 3: Apply the Solution</h2>

<p>Dip the sponge into the solution and wring out the excess liquid. Gently scrub the nicotine stains on the walls or ceilings. Make sure to get into the corners and crevices. Once you have cleaned the area, use the soft cloth to wipe away the residue. </p>

<h2>Step 4: Vacuum the Area</h2>

<p>Once you have removed the nicotine stains, use the vacuum cleaner to remove any remaining debris. This will help to prevent the stains from reappearing later. </p>
