<h2>What is a Tailback?</h2>

A tailback is a position in football that is typically the offensive player in the backfield. The tailback is responsible for carrying the ball on running plays, as well as for catching passes and blocking for other players. The tailback is usually the player who is most likely to touch the ball on any given play, and his performance is critical for the success of the team. 

<h2>What Does a Tailback Do?</h2>

The tailback's primary responsibility is to run the ball. He is typically the player who receives the handoff from the quarterback and then runs upfield. The tailback must be able to make quick decisions and be able to find the right hole to run through. He must also have the speed and agility to outrun defenders and the strength to break tackles. 

In addition to running the ball, the tailback must also be able to catch passes. The tailback is sometimes used as a receiver, especially on screen passes and swing routes. He must have good hands and the ability to run precise routes. 

Finally, the tailback must also be a good blocker. While the other offensive players are typically responsible for blocking, the tailback must be able to recognize blitzes and pick up blitzers. This requires quick thinking and the ability to make quick decisions.
