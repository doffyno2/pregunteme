<h2>What You Will Need</h2>

<p>Before you start, you will need to make sure you have the right tools for the job. Here is a list of things you will need:</p>

<ul>
<li>Tire iron</li>
<li>Tire valve stem remover</li>
<li>Tire pressure gauge</li>
<li>Tire bead breaker</li>
<li>Tire lube</li>
<li>Tire valve core wrench</li>
</ul>

<h2>Step One: Remove the Tire</h2>

<p>The first step is to remove the tire from the rim. Start by loosening the lug nuts on the wheel. Once they are loose, use the tire iron to carefully pry the bead of the tire off the rim. Make sure not to damage the rim in the process. Once the bead is off, use a tire bead breaker to help break the bead if needed. Once the bead is broken, you can carefully remove the tire from the rim.</p>

<h2>Step Two: Remove the Tire Valve Stem</h2>

<p>Once the tire is off the rim, you will need to remove the tire valve stem. Use a tire valve stem remover to carefully remove the stem from the rim. Make sure not to damage the rim in the process. Once the stem is off, use a tire pressure gauge to check the tire pressure. If the tire pressure is too low, you can add air to the tire before putting it back on the rim.</p>

<h2>Step Three: Reinstall the Tire</h2>

<p>Once the tire pressure is correct, you can reinstall the tire on the rim. Start by applying a thin layer of tire lube to the rim and the bead of the tire. This will help the tire seat properly on the rim. Once the lube is applied, carefully place the tire on the rim and start to work the bead back onto the rim. Use the tire iron to help seat the bead. Once the bead is seated, use a tire valve core wrench to reinstall the tire valve stem.</p>

<h2>Step Four: Tighten the Lug Nuts</h2>

<p>Once the tire is back on the rim, you can tighten the lug nuts. Make sure to tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to make sure the wheel is evenly tightened. Once all the lug nuts are tightened, you can use a tire pressure gauge to check the tire pressure and make sure it is correct.</p>
