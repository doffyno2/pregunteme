<h2>What Size Battery Does an Apple TV Remote Take?</h2>

<p>The Apple TV remote takes a CR2032 lithium coin battery. This type of battery is a flat, round, 3-volt lithium battery that is about the size of a quarter. It is also known as a 2032, CR2032, DL2032, or ECR2032 battery. The CR2032 battery is the same size and type of battery used in many other electronics and devices such as calculators, watches, and car alarms.</p>

<h2>How Long Does an Apple TV Remote Battery Last?</h2>

<p>The CR2032 battery in the Apple TV remote usually lasts for about 6-12 months, depending on how much it is used. If you use the remote control regularly, you may need to replace the battery more frequently.</p>

<p>Replacing an Apple TV remote battery is a fairly simple process. First, you will need to locate the battery compartment, which is usually located on the back of the remote. The battery compartment will have a small door or tab that needs to be opened. Once the door is open, you can gently remove the old battery and insert the new one, making sure to insert it the right way so that the positive and negative sides are in the right places. Once the battery is in place, you can close the compartment and your remote should now be powered up and ready to use.</p>
