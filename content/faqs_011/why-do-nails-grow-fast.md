<h2>What Causes Nail Growth?</h2>

Nails grow because the cells in the nail bed divide and multiply, forming new layers of cells that make up the nail. This process is called keratinization and it is responsible for the growth of the nail. Keratinization occurs when the cells in the nail bed are exposed to oxygen. The oxygen helps to harden the cells of the nail and makes them appear white.

<h2>How Fast Do Nails Grow?</h2>

<h2>Factors That Affect Nail Growth</h2>

There are many factors that can affect the rate of nail growth, including age, gender, health, genetics, and diet. Poor health can also slow down nail growth, as can genetics and diet. Eating a balanced diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals is essential for healthy nail growth.
