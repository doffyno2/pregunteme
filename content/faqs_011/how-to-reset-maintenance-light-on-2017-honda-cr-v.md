<h1>How to Reset Maintenance Light on 2017 Honda CR-V
</h1>

As a Honda CR-V owner, you may have noticed the maintenance light on your dashboard. This light indicates that it is time for routine maintenance on your vehicle. To reset the maintenance light, you must be familiar with the process for the specific year and model of your Honda CR-V. In this article, we will discuss the steps to reset the maintenance light for a 2017 Honda CR-V.

<h2>What Does the Maintenance Light Mean?
</h2>

The maintenance light is a signal from your Honda CR-V that indicates it is time for routine maintenance. This could include oil changes, tire rotations, and other types of service. It is important to keep up with routine maintenance in order to keep your vehicle running smoothly and to prevent larger problems down the road.

<h2>How to Reset the Maintenance Light on a 2017 Honda CR-V
</h2>

<h3>Step 1: Locate the Maintenance Light
</h3>

The first step in resetting the maintenance light is to locate the maintenance light on your dashboard. On a 2017 Honda CR-V, the maintenance light is located to the left of the speedometer.

<h3>Step 2: Turn the Ignition Key
</h3>

Once you have located the maintenance light, the next step is to turn the ignition key to the "On" position. Do not start the engine; just turn the key until all the lights come on.

<h3>Step 3: Press and Hold the Trip Meter Button
</h3>

Next, press and hold the trip meter button on the dashboard until the maintenance light begins to blink.

<h3>Step 4: Turn the Ignition Key Off and then On Again
</h3>

Once the light has begun to blink, turn the ignition key off and then on again. This will reset the maintenance light and it should remain off until it is time for routine maintenance.
