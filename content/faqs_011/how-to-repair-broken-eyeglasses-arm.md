<h2>Step 1: Clean the Broken Arm</h2>

<p>The first step in repairing a broken eyeglass arm is to clean the broken pieces. Use a damp cloth or some rubbing alcohol to gently clean the broken arm. Be careful not to use too much rubbing alcohol or it could damage the frame.</p>

<h2>Step 2: Remove Any Rust or Corrosion</h2>

<p>If the eyeglasses arm has any rust or corrosion, use a fine sandpaper to remove it. Make sure to use a light touch and only sand the affected area. Once the rust or corrosion is gone, you can move on to the next step.</p>

<h2>Step 3: Glue the Arm Back Together</h2>

<p>Once the arm is clean and free of rust or corrosion, it’s time to glue it back together. Use a strong glue that is designed for metal such as epoxy or super glue. Apply a small amount of the glue to the broken pieces and press them together. Make sure to hold them together for a few minutes to allow the glue to set.</p>

<h2>Step 4: Test the Arm</h2>

<p>Once the arm is glued back together, it’s time to test it. Gently bend the arm back and forth to make sure it is strong and secure. If it feels weak, you may need to apply more glue. Be sure to give the glue time to set before testing the arm.</p>

<h2>Step 5: Reattach the Arm</h2>

<p>Once you have tested the arm and it is strong enough to be reattached, you can now put it back on the eyeglasses frame. Make sure to line up the arm with the other arms on the frame and press it firmly into place. Once the arm is secure, you can enjoy your eyeglasses with a repaired arm.</p>
