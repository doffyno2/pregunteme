<h2>What Is Picaridin?</h2>

Picaridin is a synthetic chemical insect repellent. It was developed in the 1980s by Bayer AG, a German chemical and pharmaceutical company, and is now widely used around the world. It is odorless, tasteless, and non-irritating to the skin, making it an attractive alternative to DEET, which has a strong odor and can cause skin irritation. It is available in several different forms, including sprays, wipes, and lotions. 

<h2>How Does Picaridin Work?</h2>

Picaridin works by creating a barrier between humans and mosquitoes and other biting insects. When applied to the skin, it forms an invisible layer that essentially repels the insects and keeps them away from the person wearing it. It is effective for up to 8 hours, and can be reapplied as needed.

<h2>Is Picaridin Safe for Humans?</h2>

Yes, picaridin is generally considered safe for humans. Although it is a synthetic chemical, it is not considered to be a hazardous chemical and is not absorbed into the skin. The EPA has rated it as safe for humans, and it is approved for use in the United States.

<h2>Is Picaridin Safe for Dogs?</h2>
