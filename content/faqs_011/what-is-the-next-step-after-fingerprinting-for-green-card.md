<h2>What is Fingerprinting?</h2>

Fingerprinting is a biometric process used by governments and law enforcement to help identify individuals. It involves taking an individual's fingerprints and storing them in a database. When someone applies for a green card, they need to go through the fingerprinting process. This is done to ensure the identification of the individual, and to make sure that the person does not have a criminal history.

The purpose of fingerprinting is to identify individuals and to ensure that no one with a criminal history can get a green card. Fingerprinting is used to check the background of the applicant and to verify that the person is legally eligible to receive a green card.
