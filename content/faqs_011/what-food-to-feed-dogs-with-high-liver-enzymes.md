<h2>What Causes High Liver Enzymes in Dogs?</h2>

<p>High liver enzymes in dogs are caused by a variety of things, and often the exact cause is unknown. The most common causes include infections, toxins, certain medications, and even cancer. Other causes may include nutritional deficiencies, injury, or a genetic disorder. The liver enzymes most commonly tested are alanine aminotransferase (ALT) and aspartate aminotransferase (AST). If either of these enzymes is elevated, it is a sign of liver damage or malfunction.</p>
