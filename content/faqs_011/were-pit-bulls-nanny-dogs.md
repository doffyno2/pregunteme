<h2>History of Pit Bulls</h2>

Pit bulls have been around for centuries. They were originally bred in England for the purpose of bull-baiting, a popular sport in which dogs were set upon a bull and encouraged to attack it. The dogs were selected for their strength and courage, and were prized for their ability to bring down large animals.

When bull-baiting was outlawed in England in 1835, the breed was crossed with other types of dogs, such as terriers, to create the modern pit bull. The new breed was smaller and less aggressive than its predecessor, making it an ideal pet for families.
