<h2>What is Braid?</h2>

<p>Braid is a type of fishing line made of multiple strands of synthetic fibers that are woven together. It’s often referred to as “superline” because of its strength, abrasion resistance, and small diameter. There are many different types of braided lines available, and they vary in terms of materials, diameter, and strength.</p>

<p>The majority of braided lines are made from ultra-high molecular weight polyethylene (UHMWPE). This material is incredibly strong and offers excellent abrasion resistance. It’s also very thin and can be woven into small diameter lines. Some braided lines are made from other materials such as Kevlar or spectra, but the majority are made from UHMWPE.</p>

<p>Braided lines are popular for baitcasting because they offer a number of advantages. They have little to no stretch, so they’re great for long casts and for detecting bites. They’re also incredibly strong and abrasion resistant, so they’re perfect for battling with strong fish. Finally, they’re very thin, so they don’t add much weight to your setup, allowing for longer casts.</p>
